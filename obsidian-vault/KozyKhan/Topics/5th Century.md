**5th Century Influential Figures: A Global Outline (100 Figures)**

  

The 5th century was a time of great transition, as the Western Roman Empire crumbled and new powers emerged across Europe, the Eastern Mediterranean, Asia, and Africa. Below is an outline featuring 100 influential figures from this formative era. Each name is enclosed in double brackets to emphasize its significance.

**Concluding Reflections**

  

The 5th century witnessed the end of classical antiquity and the beginning of the Middle Ages—a time of both turmoil and transformation. From the collapse of Roman authority in Western Europe and the rise of barbarian kingdoms, to the continuity of the Byzantine state in the Eastern Mediterranean; from the dynamic political landscapes of Asia—with its emerging dynasties and vibrant intellectual traditions—to the enduring legacy of North African Christianity and the shifting power structures of the Vandals, these 100 figures offer a window into a world in flux. Their lives and achievements continue to inform our understanding of how the ancient world transitioned into the medieval era, leaving an indelible mark on global history.
