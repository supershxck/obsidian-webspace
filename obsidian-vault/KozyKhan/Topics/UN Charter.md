**United Nations Charter: A Structured Chapter**

  

**I. Introduction**

  

The United Nations Charter is the foundational treaty of the United Nations, signed on June 26, 1945, in San Francisco and effective October 24, 1945. It establishes the legal framework, purposes, and structure of the UN, codifying a new era of collective security and international cooperation.

---

**II. Historical Context ([[history]])**

• **World War II Aftermath:** Response to catastrophic global conflict and failures of the League of Nations.

• **San Francisco Conference (April–June 1945):** Delegates from 50 countries drafted and negotiated the Charter.

---

**Major Organs**

---

**IV. Ideological & Legal Foundations ([[Political Philosophy]])**

• **Collective Security:** Rejects unilateral war; mandates collective response to aggression.

• **Sovereign Equality:** All member states share equal status regardless of power.

• **Human Rights:** Embedded in Charter preamble; expanded by Universal Declaration (1948).

---

**V. Impact & Legacy ([[economics]])**

• **Peacekeeping:** Over 70 operations since 1948; mixed success in conflict resolution.

• **Development & Humanitarian Aid:** Facilitated global poverty reduction frameworks (SDGs).

• **International Law:** Basis for treaties on war crimes, environment, trade regulation.

**VIII. Conclusion**

  

The UN Charter codifies a revolutionary international order grounded in collective security, sovereign equality, and human dignity. By examining its origins, structure, and enduring challenges through [[history]], [[Political Philosophy]], and [[economics]], we derive actionable insights to strengthen multilateralism and uphold global stability in an evolving world.
