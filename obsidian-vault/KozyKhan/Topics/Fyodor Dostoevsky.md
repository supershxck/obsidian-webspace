**Fyodor Dostoevsky (1821–1881): Explorer of the Human Soul**

---

**Introduction**

  

Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoevsky is one of Russia’s—and world literature’s—most profound novelists. His psychologically intense narratives probe morality, faith, and existential despair, profoundly shaping modern thought in literature, psychology, and philosophy.

---

**Early Life & Historical Context**

• **Born:** November 11, 1821 in Moscow

• **Background:** Son of a military doctor; orphaned by mother at 15; educated at the Military Engineering Academy

• **Siberian Exile (1849–1854):** Sentenced for political dissent to hard labor; transformative spiritual crisis that informs his work

---

**Major Works & Themes**

|**Title**|**Year**|**Genre**|**Core Themes**|
|---|---|---|---|
|_Notes from Underground_|1864|Novella|Alienation, free will, irrationality|
|_Crime and Punishment_|1866|Novel|Guilt, redemption, moral psychology|
|_The Idiot_|1869|Novel|Innocence vs corruption, Christian love|
|_Demons_|1872|Novel|Political nihilism, collective evil|
|_The Brothers Karamazov_|1880|Novel|Faith, doubt, family, justice|

  

---

**Philosophical & Psychological Dimensions**

|**Dimension**|**Insight**|
|---|---|
|Existential Angst|Anticipated existentialist concerns about meaning and choice|
|Collective Shadow|Exposed societal repression and capacity for violence (Jungian)|
|Redemption Arc|Explored suffering as path to spiritual transformation|

  

---

**Legacy & Influence**

• **Literature:** Prototype for psychological realism; inspired Kafka, Camus, and Borges

• **Psychology:** Influenced Freud, Jung, and Viktor Frankl’s understanding of conscience and the unconscious

• **Philosophy & Theology:** Central to existentialist and Christian apologetic discourse

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Nihilism:** Rejection of all religious and moral principles

• **Übermensch (prefigured):** Individual transcending societal norms

• **Polyphony:** Multiple, independent voices in narrative (Bakhtin)

• **Existential Redemption:** Finding meaning through suffering
