**Neoliberalism: Market Primacy and the Restructuring of State**

---

**Introduction**

  

**Neoliberalism** is a late‑20th‑century political‑economic paradigm prioritizing free markets, minimal state intervention, and individual entrepreneurship. Emerging as a response to perceived failures of Keynesian welfare states, it reshaped global policy frameworks — influencing everything from trade to social welfare.

---

**Historical Origins & Context**

| **Era** | **Milestone**                                        | **Key Figure**        |
| ------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| 1970s   | Crisis of stagflation undermines Keynesian consensus | [[Milton Friedman]]   |
| 1979    | Thatcher elected Prime Minister (UK)                 | [[Margaret Thatcher]] |
| 1980    | Reagan elected President (US)                        | [[Ronald Reagan]]     |
| 1990s   | Washington Consensus codifies policy prescriptions   | IMF & World Bank      |

  

---

**Core Principles**

|**Principle**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|Deregulation|Remove state controls over markets|
|Privatization|Transfer public assets/services to private sector|
|Fiscal Austerity|Cut government spending to reduce deficits|
|Free Trade|Eliminate tariffs and capital controls|
|Market-Based Solutions|Social outcomes achieved through competition|

  

---

**Neoliberalism vs Other Liberal Traditions**

|**Tradition**|**State Role**|**Economic Focus**|
|---|---|---|
|[[Classical Liberalism]]|Limited to protection of rights|Free markets|
|[[Social Liberalism]]|Active welfare provision|Regulated markets|
|Neoliberalism|Enable markets to self‑regulate|Market efficiency above all|

  

---

**Socio‑Economic Impacts**

|**Domain**|**Positive Effects**|**Critiques**|
|---|---|---|
|Growth & Innovation|Stimulated entrepreneurship, tech boom|Rising inequality; precarity|
|Efficiency|Reduced bureaucracy; consumer choice|Market failures; under‑provision of public goods|
|Globalization|Expanded trade; foreign investment|Erosion of labor rights; capital flight|

  

---

**Psychological & Philosophical Reflections**

|**Dimension**|**Insight**|
|---|---|
|Collective Shadow|Overemphasis on competition suppresses communal values, empathy, and meaning (Jungian)|
|Ethical Paradox|Liberty of markets can undermine individual autonomy through insecurity and commodification|
