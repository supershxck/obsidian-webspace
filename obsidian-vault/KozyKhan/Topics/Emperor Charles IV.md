**Charles IV (1316–1378): Visionary Emperor of the Holy Roman Empire**

---

**Introduction**

  

Charles IV reigned as King of Bohemia (1346–1378) and Holy Roman Emperor (1355–1378). His rule marked a cultural renaissance, institutional consolidation, and legal foundation for the Empire—most famously through the **[[Golden Bull of 1356]]**.

---

**Early Life & Ascension**

• **Born:** May 14, 1316 in Prague to John of Bohemia

• Educated in Paris; fluent in Latin, French, German, Italian

• Elected King of the Romans in 1346; crowned Emperor by Pope Innocent VI in 1355

---

**Major Achievements**

|**Domain**|**Contribution**|**Impact**|
|---|---|---|
|Legal|Golden Bull (1356)|Established electoral college, stabilizing imperial succession|
|Cultural|Founded Charles University (1348)|First university in Central Europe; hub of learning|
|Urban Development|Rebuilt Prague (New Town, Charles Bridge)|Elevated Prague as imperial capital|
|Diplomacy|Pragmatic alliances across Europe|Maintained relative peace amid rising regional tensions|

  

---

**Connection to [[Council of Constance]]**

  

While Charles predated the Council, his institutional reforms and promotion of conciliar dialogue laid groundwork for resolving later ecclesiastical crises, including the Western Schism addressed at the [[Council of Constance]].

• **Electoral College:** Seven princes empowered to elect the emperor
