**Hispania: The Roman and Post-Roman History of the Iberian Peninsula**

  

**Hispania** was the Roman name for the **Iberian Peninsula**, which includes modern-day **Spain and Portugal**. It was an important province in the Roman Empire, contributing to military, economic, and cultural life. After the fall of Rome, it became the battleground for various Germanic kingdoms before eventually evolving into medieval Spain and Portugal.

---

**1. Pre-Roman Hispania: The Land of Iberians and Celts**

  

Before the Romans arrived, Hispania was home to a mix of **Iberians, Celts, Tartessians, and Phoenicians**.

  

**Key Civilizations and Influences:**

• **[[Iberians]]** (Eastern and Southern Hispania) – Known for their advanced metalwork and trade.

• **[[Celts]]** (North and West) – A warrior society with strong ties to Gaul.

• **[[Tartessians]]** (Southwest) – A mysterious kingdom mentioned in Greek records, possibly linked to Atlantis legends.

• **Phoenicians and Carthaginians** – Founded coastal cities like **Cádiz (Gades) and Cartagena (Carthago Nova)**, influencing trade and culture.

  

Hispania became a **battleground during the Punic Wars** as Rome and Carthage fought for control.

---

**2. Roman Conquest (218–19 BC): Turning Hispania into a Province**

  

During the **Second Punic War (218–201 BC)**, Rome invaded Hispania to cut off Carthaginian power. This led to a long and brutal conquest.

  

**Major Roman Campaigns in Hispania:**

• **Scipio Africanus (209 BC)** defeated the Carthaginians and captured Cartagena.

• **Lusitanian Resistance** – The fierce **Lusitanians** (ancestors of the Portuguese) rebelled under **Viriathus** (147–139 BC).

• **Numantine War (134 BC)** – The **Celtiberians** fought against Rome in a desperate last stand at **Numantia**, but Rome crushed them.

  

By **19 BC**, Emperor **Augustus** completed the conquest, fully integrating Hispania into the Roman Empire.

---

**3. Roman Hispania: Prosperity and Romanization**

  

Once conquered, Hispania became one of the **most valuable provinces** of the empire.

  

**Economic Importance**

• **Silver and Gold Mines** (especially in modern Spain’s north and Portugal).

• **Olive Oil and Grain Production** – Hispania supplied **Rome with essential goods**.

• **Trade Networks** – Cities like **Tarraco (Tarragona), Emerita Augusta (Mérida), and Corduba (Córdoba)** flourished.

  

**Cultural and Political Contributions**

• Hispania produced **three Roman emperors**:

• **Trajan** (r. 98–117 AD) – Expanded the empire to its greatest extent.

• **Hadrian** (r. 117–138 AD) – Strengthened borders and built Hadrian’s Wall.

• **Theodosius I** (r. 379–395 AD) – The last emperor to rule both East and West Rome.

• **Latin language** spread, forming the roots of Spanish, Portuguese, and Catalan.

  

Hispania was heavily Romanized, with roads, aqueducts, and **amphitheaters** built throughout the province.

---

**4. The Fall of Rome and the Germanic Kingdoms (5th–8th Century AD)**

  

By the **5th century**, Rome was declining, and Germanic tribes invaded Hispania.

  

**Key Events in the Post-Roman Period:**

• **409 AD** – The **Vandals, Alans, and Suebi** crossed into Hispania as Rome lost control.

• **Visigothic Conquest (415–585 AD)** – The **Visigoths**, initially Roman allies, took control and established the **Visigothic Kingdom**, ruling from **Toledo**.

• **587 AD** – **King Reccared I** converted to **Catholicism**, ending Arian Christianity among the Visigoth elite.

  

The **Visigothic Kingdom** ruled Hispania until the **Muslim invasion in 711 AD**.

---

**5. Muslim Conquest and the Birth of Medieval Spain (711 AD–1492 AD)**

• **711 AD** – The Umayyad Muslim general **Tariq ibn Ziyad** defeated the last Visigothic king, **Roderic**, at the **Battle of Guadalete**.

• Hispania became **Al-Andalus**, an advanced Islamic civilization that flourished for centuries.

• Christian resistance in the **north (Asturias, León, Castile, Aragon)** led to the **Reconquista (718–1492 AD)**.

  

By **1492 AD**, the Catholic Monarchs **Ferdinand and Isabella** completed the **Reconquista**, ending Muslim rule in Spain.

---

**6. Legacy of Hispania**

• **Linguistic Influence** – The Latin spoken in Hispania evolved into **Spanish, Portuguese, Catalan, and Galician**.

• **Cultural Fusion** – Roman, Visigothic, and Islamic traditions merged to shape Spanish and Portuguese identities.

• **Medieval Kingdoms** – The region’s fragmentation gave rise to **Castile, Aragon, Navarre, and Portugal**, leading to the modern nations of **Spain and Portugal**.

  

Hispania was more than just a Roman province—it was a **cultural crossroads** that shaped the history of the Iberian Peninsula for centuries.
