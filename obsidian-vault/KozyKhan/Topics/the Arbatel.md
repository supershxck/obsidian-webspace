The **_Arbatel de Magia Veterum_** (Arbatel: Of the Magic of the Ancients) is a fascinating text of **Renaissance ceremonial magic**. It was first published in **1575 in Latin** in Basel, Switzerland. The book is unique among magical grimoires of its time because it emphasizes a more philosophical and ethical approach to magic, aligning with Christian Neoplatonic thought rather than purely occult practices.

  

**Structure and Content**

  

The Arbatel is divided into **seven sections called “Philosophies”**, although only the first part, called the **“Isagoge” (Introduction)**, survives. This introductory section contains **49 aphorisms** that serve as guiding principles for the aspiring magician. These aphorisms mix elements of theology, philosophy, and practical advice, emphasizing humility, divine grace, and moral uprightness.

  

Key themes include:

1. **Divine Wisdom**: The magician must seek divine wisdom and align themselves with God’s will to perform magic ethically and effectively.

2. **Celestial Influences**: The book discusses the **Olympian Spirits**, celestial beings associated with planetary forces, who can assist the practitioner. These spirits, such as **Aratron**, **Bethor**, and **Och**, govern cosmic and earthly phenomena.

3. **Practical Magic**: The text emphasizes the importance of self-discipline, prayer, fasting, and virtuous living as prerequisites for success in magical endeavors.

4. **Avoidance of Evil**: The Arbatel strongly warns against demonic magic, necromancy, and other practices that violate Christian principles.

  

**The Olympian Spirits**

  

The **Olympian Spirits** are unique to the Arbatel and are described as benevolent beings ruling over different parts of creation. Each spirit is associated with a planet and governs specific aspects of life and nature. For example:

• **Aratron**: Linked to Saturn, governs agriculture, alchemy, and long life.

• **Bethor**: Linked to Jupiter, grants wealth, honor, and healing.

• **Och**: Linked to the Sun, offers health, wisdom, and gold.

The practitioner is encouraged to approach these spirits through prayer and meditation, seeking their guidance for noble purposes.

  

**Distinctive Features**

  

Unlike many grimoires that focus on summoning demons or using complex magical tools, the Arbatel emphasizes:

• A **spiritual relationship with God** as the foundation of magical practice.

• A **philosophical and contemplative approach** rather than purely ritualistic.

• Ethical guidelines to avoid harming others or seeking selfish gains.

  

**Influence**

  

The Arbatel has had a significant impact on Western esotericism, inspiring later magical traditions, including **Hermeticism**, **Christian mysticism**, and **modern ceremonial magic**. Its influence can be seen in the works of figures such as **John Dee** and **Robert Fludd**. In contemporary times, it is studied by practitioners of magic who value its ethical and philosophical orientation.
