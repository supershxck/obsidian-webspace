**John Cabot** (_Giovanni Caboto_) was a **15th-century Italian explorer** who sailed under the **English flag** and is credited with the **European rediscovery of North America** in **1497**, nearly five centuries after the Norse expeditions of Leif Erikson. His voyages were instrumental in England’s early claims to territories in the New World, laying the groundwork for future exploration and colonization.

  

**Early Life**

• **Birthplace**: Cabot was born around **1450** in **Genoa**, Italy, but spent much of his early life in **Venice**, becoming a Venetian citizen in **1476**.

• **Occupation**: As a young man, Cabot worked as a merchant and navigator, gaining knowledge of **mapmaking**, **navigation**, and the Mediterranean trade routes, particularly dealing in spices.

• **Influence of the Age of Exploration**:

• Cabot was inspired by the voyages of **Christopher Columbus**, believing that a shorter route to Asia could be found by sailing westward across the Atlantic.

  

**Sponsorship by England**

• After failing to secure backing for his expeditions from Spain and Portugal, Cabot turned to **England**.

• **King Henry VII** granted him a patent in **1496**, authorizing Cabot and his sons to explore and claim lands in the name of the English crown.

  

**The 1497 Voyage**

• **Ship and Crew**:

• Cabot sailed from **Bristol** aboard a small ship called the **Matthew**, with a crew of about **18 men**.

• **Objective**:

• His goal was to find a **northwest passage** to Asia for trade in spices and other valuable goods.

• **Discovery**:

• On **June 24, 1497**, Cabot landed on the coast of **North America**, though the exact location remains debated. Possible sites include:

• **Newfoundland**

• **Cape Breton Island**

• **Labrador**

• Cabot claimed the land for England, marking the first European expedition to North America since the Norse settlements in **Vinland**.

  

**Significance of the Voyage**

1. **Claiming Territory**:

• Cabot’s discovery provided the basis for **England’s territorial claims** in North America, which would later lead to colonization efforts.

2. **Fisheries**:

• Reports of abundant fish in the waters off Newfoundland spurred interest in the region as a rich fishing ground.

3. **Early Exploration**:

• His voyage marked England’s entry into the **Age of Exploration**, competing with Spain and Portugal.

  

**The 1498 Expedition**

• Encouraged by his success, Cabot launched a second expedition in **1498**, possibly with **five ships** and a larger crew.

• **Uncertain Fate**:

• The details of this voyage are unclear, but it is widely believed that Cabot’s fleet was lost at sea. Some theories suggest that he may have reached North America again before perishing.

  

**Legacy**

1. **Historical Impact**:

• Cabot’s voyages solidified England’s interest in exploring the **Atlantic** and contributed to the eventual establishment of British colonies in the Americas.

2. **Recognition**:

• Cabot’s achievements were celebrated during his lifetime, and he was rewarded by **King Henry VII** for his success.

• His son, **Sebastian Cabot**, continued his legacy by undertaking further explorations in the Americas.

3. **Modern Commemoration**:

• Cabot’s contributions are remembered in both **England** and **Canada**:

• **Cabot Tower** in **Bristol**, England.

• **Cabot Tower** in **St. John’s, Newfoundland**, built to commemorate the 400th anniversary of his voyage.

  

**Debates and Mysteries**

1. **Landing Site**:

• Historians still debate the precise location of Cabot’s first landfall, with Newfoundland being the most widely accepted.

2. **1498 Voyage**:

• The fate of Cabot’s second expedition remains one of the enduring mysteries of early exploration.

3. **Recognition Compared to Columbus**:

• Though Cabot’s voyages were crucial for England’s imperial ambitions, his fame is often overshadowed by **Christopher Columbus**, despite Cabot being the first to definitively reach mainland North America during this era.

  

**Key Takeaways**

• **John Cabot** played a pivotal role in England’s entry into the Age of Exploration and laid the groundwork for its later dominance in the New World.

• His **1497 voyage** is considered one of the most significant achievements of early transatlantic exploration.

• The legacy of Cabot continues to shape historical narratives about the discovery and settlement of the Americas.
