**Egyptian Arithmetic**

  

The ancient Egyptians developed a sophisticated system of arithmetic that was **practical**, primarily focused on solving problems related to agriculture, construction, trade, and astronomy. Their system was highly functional rather than theoretical.

  

**Number System:**

• The Egyptians used a **decimal system** based on **powers of ten**.

• Numbers were written in **hieroglyphs**, with separate symbols for 1, 10, 100, 1,000, 10,000, 100,000, and 1,000,000.

• Their number system was **additive**, meaning they combined symbols to express larger numbers (e.g., three 10-symbols represented 30).

  

**Basic Arithmetic Operations:**

1. **Addition and Subtraction:**

• Performed by combining or canceling out symbols.

• Example: Adding three “10s” to two “10s” gives five “10s.”

**Egyptian Astronomy**

  

Egyptian astronomy was deeply tied to their **religion**, **calendar**, and **agriculture**, and they developed a remarkable understanding of celestial phenomena.

  

**Celestial Observations:**

• The Egyptians observed **stars**, **planets**, the **moon**, and the **sun** to mark time and predict seasonal changes.

• Key observations included:

• The movement of the **sun** across the sky, which influenced their **solar calendar**.

• The heliacal rising of the star **Sirius (Sopdet)**, which heralded the annual **Nile flood**.

  

**Calendars:**

1. **Lunar Calendar:**

• Initially based on the moon’s phases, with 12 lunar months of 29–30 days.

• Adjusted with an intercalary month to keep it aligned with the solar year.

2. **Solar Calendar:**

• A later innovation of **365 days**: 12 months of 30 days each, plus 5 extra days (**epagomenal days**) for festivals.

• This calendar was more consistent with the agricultural cycle but slightly shorter than the solar year, causing a drift over centuries.

  

**Astronomical Tools:**

• **Merkhet:** A sighting tool used to align temples and observe stars.

• **Palm-leaf Sundials:** Used to measure time during the day.

• Water clocks (**clepsydrae**) measured time intervals at night or in shadowed areas.

  

**Star Charts and Zodiacs:**

• The Egyptians mapped **decans**, a system of 36 star groups that divided the night sky into sections.

• The **ceiling of the Dendera Temple** (Ptolemaic Period) features a famous **zodiac**, incorporating Greek influences but rooted in earlier Egyptian stellar knowledge.

  

**Planetary Knowledge:**

• The Egyptians recognized five planets visible to the naked eye: Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, and Saturn.

• These were often associated with gods but did not play a predictive role (as in later Babylonian astrology).

  

**Astronomy in Architecture:**

• Temples and pyramids were aligned with celestial events:

• The Great Pyramid of Giza aligns with the **cardinal points**.

• Temples like **Abu Simbel** were constructed so sunlight illuminated inner sanctuaries on specific dates (e.g., Ramses II’s birthday).

  

**Applications of Arithmetic and Astronomy**

1. **Irrigation and Agriculture:**

• Astronomy helped predict the Nile’s flooding (based on Sirius’ heliacal rising).

• Arithmetic was used for measuring land for taxation after the floodwaters receded.

2. **Construction:**

• Precise measurements and geometry were critical for building pyramids, temples, and tombs.

• The slope of the Great Pyramid (51° 50′) demonstrates advanced mathematical knowledge.

3. **Timekeeping and Rituals:**

• Astronomy governed the scheduling of religious ceremonies.

• Calendars ensured festivals aligned with agricultural and celestial cycles.

4. **Medicine and Magic:**

• Astronomical cycles influenced Egyptian medicine, where timing treatments or rituals aligned with favorable celestial events.
