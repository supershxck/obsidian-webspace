### Accomplishments of the [[Seventh Ecumenical Council]] ([[Second Council of Nicaea]], 787 AD)

#### **1. Restoration of Icon Veneration**
   - **Key Issue**: The council addressed the iconoclastic controversy, which involved a significant debate within the Church about the veneration of icons.
   - **Affirmation of Icon Veneration**: The council declared that the veneration (but not worship) of icons is legitimate and should be practiced by Christians. It differentiated between veneration (proskynesis) given to icons and worship (latreia) given to God alone.

#### **2. Theological Basis for Icon Veneration**
   - **Incarnation Doctrine**: The council based its decision on the doctrine of the Incarnation, asserting that because Christ became incarnate and took on a visible, tangible form, it is permissible to create and venerate images of Him, as well as of the saints.
   - **Support from Tradition**: The council cited the continuous tradition of the Church, including the teachings of the Church Fathers, to support the practice of venerating icons.

#### **3. Condemnation of Iconoclasm**
   - **Rejection of Iconoclasts**: The council condemned the iconoclastic position, which opposed the use of religious images, and anathematized those who promoted iconoclasm.
   - **Restoration of Icons**: The decisions of the council led to the restoration of icons in churches and homes, reinstating their central role in Orthodox worship and devotion.

#### **4. Issuance of Canons**
   - **Regulations on Icon Veneration**: The council issued canons that provided guidelines for the proper veneration of icons and the production of religious art, ensuring that the practice aligned with orthodox theology and liturgical norms.
   - **Church Governance**: Additional canons addressed various issues of church governance, discipline, and administration, further strengthening the structure of the Church.

#### **5. Confirmation of Previous Councils**
   - **Continuity with Past Councils**: The council reaffirmed the decisions and teachings of the previous ecumenical councils, maintaining continuity in the Church's doctrinal development.

#### **Key Figures**
   - **Empress Irene and Emperor Constantine VI**: Played crucial roles in convening the council and supporting the restoration of icon veneration.
   - **Patriarch Tarasios of Constantinople**: Presided over the council and was a key proponent of the veneration of icons.

### Conclusion

The Seventh Ecumenical Council, or the Second Council of Nicaea, was instrumental in resolving the iconoclastic controversy and restoring the veneration of icons within the Church. By affirming the theological basis for icon veneration and condemning iconoclasm, the council upheld the legitimacy of religious images as expressions of the Incarnation and as aids to devotion and worship. The decisions made at this council reinforced the continuity of orthodox tradition and contributed significantly to the liturgical and devotional life of the Orthodox Church.
