**Emperor Justinian II: The Turbulent and Vengeful Ruler of Byzantium**

  

Emperor Justinian II, also known as “Rhinotmetus” (the “slit-nosed” or “cut-nosed”), reigned during two separate periods (685–695 CE and 705–711 CE) in the Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire. His reign is marked by dramatic reversals of fortune, extreme cruelty, and ambitious attempts at centralizing power, which left a lasting imprint on Byzantine history.

---

**Early Life and Rise to Power**

• **Imperial Lineage and Ambition:**

Born into a period of considerable instability, Justinian II was a member of the ruling dynasty during a time when the empire was plagued by internal strife and external threats. His ascent to power was marked by his relentless ambition and his willingness to employ ruthless tactics to secure his position.

• **First Reign (685–695 CE):**

Justinian II ascended to the throne in 685 CE, initially consolidating power through military campaigns and harsh measures. His early reign, however, was characterized by growing discontent among the military and the elite due to his autocratic and often brutal methods of governance.

---

**Downfall and Exile**

• **Overthrow and Punishment:**

In 695 CE, widespread opposition forced Justinian II’s deposition. In an infamous act of retribution, his nose was cut off—a physical disfigurement that earned him the moniker “Rhinotmetus.” This brutal punishment was intended to disqualify him from reclaiming the throne, as imperial legitimacy was closely tied to physical wholeness.

• **Exile and Return:**

Despite his dramatic downfall and exile, Justinian II managed to rally support and staged a daring comeback. In 705 CE, he reclaimed the throne, initiating his second reign, which was marked by even greater cruelty and vindictiveness against his former rivals and those who had opposed him.

---

**Second Reign and Policies**

• **Restoration of Authority Through Repression:**

Upon his return, Justinian II employed severe measures to consolidate power. His second reign was defined by a reign of terror, where he systematically eliminated potential threats, reasserting his authority through brutal repression.

• **Administrative and Military Efforts:**

Justinian II sought to strengthen the empire’s administrative structure and reestablish control over its military forces. His policies aimed at centralizing power and curbing the autonomy of regional authorities, though his methods left a legacy of fear and resentment.

• **Legacy of Vengeance:**

His second reign left a controversial legacy. Although he succeeded in reasserting imperial control, his vengeful actions and ruthless rule contributed to long-term instability. His reign exemplified the dangers of absolute power exercised without restraint.

---

**Legacy and Historical Impact**

• **A Symbol of Turbulence:**

Emperor Justinian II’s career is emblematic of the volatility and brutality of Byzantine politics during the 7th and 8th centuries. His dramatic rise, brutal punishment, and subsequent return to power illustrate the extremes of ambition and revenge that could characterize imperial rule.

• **Influence on Byzantine Governance:**

While his methods were harsh, his attempts to centralize authority and reform military structures influenced the ongoing evolution of Byzantine administrative practices. His reign, however, also served as a cautionary tale about the perils of unbridled autocracy.

• **Enduring Controversy:**

Justinian II remains a deeply polarizing figure in Byzantine history. Revered by some for his determination and strategic acumen, he is equally condemned by others for the cruelty and instability that marked his rule.

---

**Vocabulary and Key Concepts**

• **Rhinotmetus:** A nickname meaning “cut-nosed,” referring to the mutilation inflicted upon Justinian II after his first deposition.

• **Byzantine Politics:** The intricate and often brutal dynamics of power in the Eastern Roman Empire, exemplified by Justinian II’s tumultuous reign.

• **Autocratic Repression:** The use of extreme measures to consolidate power, a hallmark of Justinian II’s second reign.

• **Restoration and Vengeance:** Themes that define his legacy—his dramatic return to power was fueled by a desire for vengeance against those who had deposed him.

---

**Conclusion**

  

Emperor Justinian II’s life and reign are marked by dramatic upheaval, ruthless ambition, and a cycle of downfall and restoration. His first reign ended in brutal punishment and exile, yet his return to power in 705 CE showcased a leader determined to reclaim and consolidate his authority by any means necessary. Despite his efforts to restore order, his reign is remembered as a period of extreme repression and personal vengeance—a vivid illustration of the turbulent nature of Byzantine imperial politics.
