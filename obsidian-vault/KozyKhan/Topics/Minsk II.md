The Minsk II agreement, formally known as the "Package of Measures for the Implementation of the Minsk Agreements," is a peace deal aimed at ending the conflict in eastern Ukraine, specifically the war in the Donbas region between Ukrainian forces and separatist groups backed by Russia. The agreement was signed on February 12, 2015. Here are the key aspects:

1. **Background**:
   - The conflict in eastern Ukraine began in 2014 following Russia's annexation of Crimea and the subsequent uprising by pro-Russian separatists in the Donetsk and Luhansk regions.
   - The initial Minsk Protocol, signed in September 2014, failed to stop the fighting, leading to the Minsk II negotiations.

2. **Participants**:
   - The agreement was negotiated by the Trilateral Contact Group, which included representatives from Ukraine, Russia, and the Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe (OSCE).
   - The leaders of France and Germany also played key roles in facilitating the agreement, collectively known as the Normandy Format.

3. **Key Provisions**:
   - **Ceasefire**: An immediate and comprehensive ceasefire in specific areas of the Donetsk and Luhansk regions, starting from February 15, 2015.
   - **Withdrawal of Heavy Weapons**: Both sides were to pull back heavy weapons from the front lines to create a buffer zone, with oversight by the OSCE.
   - **Release of Prisoners**: An exchange of all prisoners and detainees related to the conflict.
   - **Humanitarian Aid**: Provision of safe access for the delivery of humanitarian assistance to affected areas.
   - **Political Reforms**: Constitutional reforms in Ukraine, including decentralization and granting special status to certain areas of the Donetsk and Luhansk regions.
   - **Local Elections**: Conducting local elections in the separatist-held areas, in accordance with Ukrainian law and monitored by the OSCE.
   - **Border Control**: Restoration of full control of the state border by the Ukrainian government throughout the conflict zone, starting the day after local elections and ending after a comprehensive political settlement.

4. **Challenges and Implementation**:
   - Despite the agreement, fighting has continued sporadically, and both sides have accused each other of violations.
   - The political aspects, particularly the constitutional reforms and local elections, have been contentious and difficult to implement.
   - The issue of control over the border has been a major sticking point, with Ukraine insisting on regaining control before elections, while Russia and the separatists have demanded elections first.

5. **Current Status**:
   - The Minsk II agreement remains the basis for diplomatic efforts to resolve the conflict, but its full implementation has not been achieved.
   - Periodic ceasefires have been declared and violated, and the situation in eastern Ukraine has remained unstable.
   - Ongoing negotiations and diplomatic efforts continue, but the conflict remains unresolved, with significant geopolitical implications involving Ukraine, Russia, and Western countries.

Minsk II is seen as a critical, albeit imperfect, framework for potential peace, emphasizing the need for continued dialogue and compromise to achieve a lasting resolution to the conflict.
