**Theodore Roosevelt (1858–1919): Architect of Progressive Reform and American Conservation**

---

**Introduction**

  

Theodore “Teddy” Roosevelt was the 26th President of the United States (1901–1909), a dynamic leader whose energetic vision forged the foundations of modern [[Progressive Liberalism]]. Renowned for trust‑busting, social reform, and pioneering conservation, Roosevelt reshaped the federal government’s role in serving public welfare and stewardship.

---

**Early Life & Rise to Prominence**

• **Born:** October 27, 1858 in New York City to a wealthy family

• Overcame childhood asthma through rigorous self‑improvement and outdoorsmanship

• Served in New York State Assembly (1882–1884), New York City Police Commissioner, Assistant Secretary of the Navy

---

**Presidential Achievements & Progressive Reforms**

|**Domain**|**Initiative**|**Impact**|
|---|---|---|
|Trust Regulation|Sherman Antitrust prosecutions (45 suits)|Curbed corporate monopolies|
|Consumer Protection|Pure Food and Drug Act (1906); Meat Inspection Act|Safeguarded public health|
|Labor Rights|Mediated 1902 Coal Strike|Established federal arbitration precedent|
|Conservation|Created 150 national forests; 5 national parks; Antiquities Act (1906)|Preserved 230 million acres of public land|
|Regulatory Oversight|Founded Bureau of Corporations|Precursor to FTC|

**Legacy & Modern Relevance**

• Established federal government as guardian of public interest

• Model for [[Social Liberalism]] and [[Progressive Liberalism]]’s blend of regulation, equity, and stewardship

• Influences contemporary environmental policy and corporate accountability

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Square Deal:** Roosevelt’s domestic policy ethos emphasizing fairness

• **Trust‑busting:** Government action to break up monopolies

• **Conservationism:** Sustainable management of natural resources

• **Antiquities Act:** Authority to protect landmarks and cultural sites
