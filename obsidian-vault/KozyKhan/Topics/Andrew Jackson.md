**Andrew Jackson (1767–1845): The People’s President and Architect of Jacksonian Democracy**

---

**Introduction**

  

Andrew Jackson was the seventh President of the United States (1829–1837), a polarizing figure whose populist vision transformed American politics. Known as **“Old Hickory,”** Jackson championed the common man, expanded suffrage, and reshaped the presidency into a powerful, directly‑elected office.

**Key Policies & Actions**

| **Policy Area**        | **Initiative**                                                        | **Impact**                                             |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Jacksonian Democracy:** Political movement expanding white male suffrage and executive power

• **Indian Removal Act (1830):** Legislation authorizing forced relocation of Native Americans

• **Nullification:** State assertion to invalidate federal law deemed unconstitutional

• **Bank War:** Jackson’s campaign against central banking authority
