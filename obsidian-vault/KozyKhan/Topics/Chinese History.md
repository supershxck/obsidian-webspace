Here is an **outline of known Chinese history**, highlighting key dynasties, periods, and milestones:

---

### **1. Prehistoric China (c. 2.5 million - 2070 BCE)**  
- **Paleolithic and Neolithic Era**: Early human settlements and cultures (e.g., the **Peking Man**, c. 750,000 BCE).  
- **[[Yangshao Culture]] (5000–3000 BCE)** and **[[Longshan Culture]] (3000–2000 BCE)**: Early agriculture, pottery, and social organization.

---

### **2. The Legendary [[Xia Dynasty]] (c. 2070–1600 BCE)**  
- **Legendary Era**: The "[[Three Sovereigns and Five Emperors]]," semi-mythical rulers credited with teaching civilization (e.g., Fu Xi, Huang Di).  
- **Xia Dynasty**: The first dynasty described in historical texts (e.g., *[[Records of the Grand Historian]]*), though archaeological evidence is debated.

---

### **3. [[Shang Dynasty]] (1600–1046 BCE)**  
- First historically verified Chinese dynasty.  
- Developed **bronze metallurgy**, early writing ([[oracle bones]]), and stratified society.  
- Religion focused on ancestor worship and divination.

---

### **4. [[Zhou Dynasty]] (1046–256 BCE)**  
- **Western Zhou (1046–771 BCE)**: Introduction of the **Mandate of Heaven** concept.  
- **Eastern Zhou (770–256 BCE)**: Divided into the **Spring and Autumn Period** and the **Warring States Period**.  
  - Rise of major philosophies: **[[Confucianism]], [[Taoism]], [[Legalism]]**, and **[[Mohism]]**.

---

### **5. [[Qin Dynasty]] (221–206 BCE)**  
- First unified Chinese empire under **Qin Shi Huang**.  
- Standardized writing, currency, weights, and measures.  
- Construction of **[[the Great Wall of China]]** and **[[Terracotta Army]]**.  
- Harsh Legalist rule but short-lived.

---

### **6. [[Han Dynasty]] (206 BCE–220 CE)**  
- One of China's most influential dynasties.  
- Expanded territory, established **[[Silk Road]]**, and developed **Confucian bureaucracy**.  
- Scientific and technological advancements (e.g., paper, seismograph).  
- Split into **Western (Former) Han** and **Eastern (Later) Han**.

---

### **7. Three Kingdoms Period (220–280 CE)**  
- Fragmentation into the states of **Wei, Shu, and Wu** after the Han collapse.  
- Famous for military conflicts, figures like **Cao Cao**, and the epic *Romance of the Three Kingdoms*.

---

### **8. [[Jin Dynasty]] and Sixteen Kingdoms (265–420 CE)**  
- **Western Jin** reunited China briefly but fell to internal strife and nomadic invasions.  
- **Sixteen Kingdoms**: Period of chaos and northern invasions by non-Han groups.

---

### **9. Southern and Northern Dynasties (420–589 CE)**  
- Division between **southern dynasties** (Han Chinese-led) and **northern dynasties** (non-Han rulers).  
- Spread of **[[Buddhism]]** in China, mixing with Confucian and Daoist traditions.

---

### **10. [[Sui Dynasty]] (581–618 CE)**  
- Reunified China and began massive infrastructure projects (e.g., **[[the Grand Canal]]**).  
- Short-lived but paved the way for the Tang Dynasty.

---

### **11. [[Tang Dynasty]] (618–907 CE)**  
- Considered a golden age of culture, art, and cosmopolitanism.  
- Flourishing of **poetry** (e.g., Li Bai, Du Fu) and trade via **[[Silk Road]]**.  
- Heightened influence of **[[Buddhism]]** before later suppression.  
- Decline due to internal rebellion (e.g., **[[An Lushan Rebellion]]**).

---

### **12. [[Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms]] (907–960 CE)**  
- Fragmentation after Tang's collapse.  
- Competing regimes in the north (dynasties) and south (kingdoms).

---

### **13. [[Song Dynasty]] (960–1279 CE)**  
- Divided into **Northern Song** and **Southern Song** after losing the north to the **[[Jurchen Jin Dynasty]]**.  
- Advances in **science** (e.g., gunpowder, compass, movable type printing).  
- Economic revolution and flourishing trade.  
- Eventually conquered by the **Mongols**.

---

### **14. [[Yuan Dynasty]] (1271–1368 CE)**  
- Established by **[[Kublai Khan]]** after the Mongol conquest.  
- Integration into the **[[Mongol Empire]]** but retained Chinese administrative systems.  
- Promoted trade and cultural exchange (e.g., **[[Marco Polo]]'s travels**).  

---

### **15. [[Ming Dynasty]] (1368–1644 CE)**  
- Restoration of Han rule, focus on strengthening borders (e.g., Great Wall reconstruction).  
- Maritime exploration led by **Zheng He**'s fleets.  
- Decline due to internal corruption, peasant uprisings, and Manchu invasions.

---

### **16. [[Qing Dynasty]] (1644–1912 CE)**  
- Last imperial dynasty, ruled by the **Manchu**.  
- Expansion of China's territory (e.g., [[Tibet]], Xinjiang, Mongolia).  
- Challenges from Western imperialism, **[[Opium Wars]]**, and **[[Taiping Rebellion]]**.  
- Overthrown in the **[[Xinhai Revolution]]** of 1911.

---

### **17. [[Republic of China]] (1912–1949)**  
- Established after the fall of the Qing.  
- Struggles with warlordism, Japanese invasions, and the Chinese Civil War between the **Nationalists** ([[Kuomintang]]) and **Communists**.

---

### **18. [[People's Republic of China]] (1949–present)**  
- Founded by **[[Mao Zedong]]** after the Communist victory.  
- Key events:  
  - **[[Great Leap Forward]]** (1958–1962): Attempted rapid industrialization, resulting in famine.  
  - **[[Cultural Revolution]]** (1966–1976): Social and political upheaval.  
  - **Reforms under Deng Xiaoping** (1978 onward): Transition to a **[[market-oriented economy]]**.  
  - Modern era: Economic rise to global superpower status, with significant challenges (e.g., environmental, political).  

---
