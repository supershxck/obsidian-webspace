**Social Liberalism: Balancing Freedom with Social Justice**

---

**Introduction**

  

**Social Liberalism** is a strand of liberal political thought that builds on [[Classical Liberalism]]’s commitment to individual liberty while emphasizing the state’s positive role in ensuring equality of opportunity and social welfare. It underpins much of modern [[British Liberalism]] and Western social‑democratic policy.

---

**Historical Evolution**

| **Era**            | **Milestone**                              | **Key Figure**                                              |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Late 19th Century  | Recognition of market failures and poverty | T.H. Green, L.T. Hobhouse                                   |
| Early 20th Century | Welfare state foundations                  | [[David Lloyd George]], [[John Maynard Keynes]]             |
| Post‑WWII          | Consolidation of social democracy          | William Beveridge (UK), Franklin D. Roosevelt (US New Deal) |

  

---

**Core Principles**

|**Principle**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|Positive Liberty|Freedom to achieve one’s potential via education, health, social security|
|Fair Opportunity|State intervention to remove structural barriers|
|Social Safety Net|Universal healthcare, unemployment insurance, progressive taxation|
|Rule of Law & Rights|Protection of civil liberties alongside economic rights|

  

---

**Social Liberalism vs Related Ideologies**

|**Ideology**|**State Role**|**Economic Approach**|
|---|---|---|
|Classical Liberalism|Minimal|Laissez‑faire markets|
|Social Liberalism|Active facilitator|Regulated markets with redistribution|
|Socialism|Extensive|Collective/state ownership|

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Positive Liberty:** Capacity to act on one’s free will through enabling conditions

• **Welfare State:** Government provision of social services to ensure minimum living standards

• **Progressive Taxation:** Higher rates on greater incomes to fund public goods

• **Social Contract:** Implicit agreement prioritizing mutual obligations
