**Theosophy** is an esoteric spiritual movement that emerged in the late 19th century, primarily associated with the work of **Helena Petrovna Blavatsky** (1831–1891) and the organization she co-founded, the **Theosophical Society** (est. 1875). The term “theosophy” literally means _divine wisdom_ (from the Greek words _theos_ meaning “God” or “divine” and _sophia_ meaning “wisdom”). Below is an overview of its origins, core beliefs, and legacy:

  

**1. Origins and Key Figures**

1. **Helena P. Blavatsky (HPB)**

A Russian noblewoman and traveler who claimed to have studied hidden knowledge in [[Tibet]] and elsewhere. She authored foundational texts like _Isis Unveiled_ (1877) and _The Secret Doctrine_ (1888).

2. **Henry Steel Olcott & William Quan Judge**

Co-founders of the Theosophical Society alongside Blavatsky. They helped establish the society’s organizational and educational efforts.

3. **Annie Besant**

A British social reformer and orator who joined the society later and became one of its most influential leaders, expanding its teachings and reach globally.

  

**2. Core Beliefs and Teachings**

1. **Universal Brotherhood**

The Theosophical Society’s motto is “There is no religion higher than truth.” Theosophy stresses the unity of all beings and advocates a universal fellowship without distinctions of race, creed, or social class.

2. **Ancient Wisdom Tradition**

Theosophists believe that a “hidden” or “primordial” wisdom underlies all world religions and mythologies. They see each faith as a reflection of a single, universal truth.

3. **Reincarnation and Karma**

Drawing on Hindu and Buddhist concepts, Theosophy teaches reincarnation (the soul’s rebirth across lifetimes) and karma (the law of moral causation), shaping each individual’s spiritual evolution.

4. **Hierarchical Cosmos**

The universe is seen as layered with subtle planes beyond the purely physical. Advanced spiritual beings—sometimes referred to as “Mahatmas” or “Masters of the Ancient Wisdom”—guide humanity’s progress.

5. **Spiritual Self-Transformation**

Through study, meditation, and ethical living, individuals can access deeper truths, accelerate their own evolution, and contribute to global spiritual awakening.

  

**3. Influence and Legacy**

1. **Bridging East and West**

Theosophy was instrumental in introducing Western audiences to Eastern philosophies, including Hinduism, Buddhism, and various yoga traditions.

2. **Modern Esotericism**

It helped lay the groundwork for the later New Age movement, modern occultism, and other esoteric traditions by popularizing concepts like karma, reincarnation, and chakra systems in the West.

3. **Social and Educational Initiatives**

Under leaders like Annie Besant, the Theosophical Society supported educational reforms, championed women’s rights, and promoted intercultural dialogue.

  

**In Short**

  

Theosophy is a cross-cultural spiritual tradition aimed at uncovering a universal wisdom said to underpin all religions. It emphasizes the unity of life, the evolutionary journey of the soul through reincarnation and karma, and the guidance of advanced spiritual teachers. Through study, moral development, and meditation, followers seek a deeper understanding of the divine mysteries and the ultimate oneness of existence.
