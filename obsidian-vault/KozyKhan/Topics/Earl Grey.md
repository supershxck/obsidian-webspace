**Charles Grey, 2nd Earl Grey (1764–1845): Reformist Prime Minister and Architect of Modern Britain**

---

**Introduction**

  

Charles Grey, 2nd **Earl Grey**, served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1830 to 1834. A leading figure of early [[British Liberalism]], his administration enacted transformative social and political reforms that reshaped parliamentary democracy and civil rights — most famously the **[[Reform Act of 1832]]** and abolition of slavery in the British Empire.

---

**Early Life & Political Formation**

• **Born:** March 13, 1764, Northumberland into Whig aristocracy

• Educated at Eton and Trinity College, Cambridge

• Entered Parliament in 1794; championed civil liberties, Catholic emancipation, and anti‑slavery causes

---

**Prime Ministership (1830–1834)**

| **Reform**                 | **Year** | **Impact**                                                                           |
| -------------------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Reform Act                 | 1832     | Expanded male suffrage from ~5% to ~12% of adult males; eliminated “rotten boroughs” |
| Slavery Abolition Act      | 1833     | Abolished slavery across British colonies; compensated slave‑owners                  |
| Municipal Corporations Act | 1835     | Democratized local government; introduced elected councils                           |
