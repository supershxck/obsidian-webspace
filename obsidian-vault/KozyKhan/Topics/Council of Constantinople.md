### Accomplishments of the [[Second Ecumenical Council]] (Council of Constantinople, 381 AD)

#### **1. Expansion and Confirmation of the [[Nicene Creed]]**
   - **Niceno-Constantinopolitan Creed**: The council expanded the original Nicene Creed to address additional theological concerns, particularly those related to the Holy Spirit.
   - **Key Additions**: Affirmed the divinity of the Holy Spirit, stating, "And in the Holy Spirit, the Lord, the Giver of Life, who proceeds from the Father, who with the Father and the Son is worshiped and glorified, who spoke by the prophets."
   - **Condemnation of Heresies**: Condemned various heresies, including [[Macedonianism]] (which denied the divinity of the Holy Spirit) and [[Apollinarianism]] (which denied the full humanity of Christ).

#### **2. Reaffirmation of the Divinity of Christ**
   - **Against Arianism**: Reaffirmed the teachings of the First Council of Nicaea against [[Arianism]], which continued to be a significant issue in the church.

#### **3. Establishment of the Hierarchical Structure of the Church**
   - **Bishop of Constantinople**: Elevated the status of the Bishop of Constantinople, declaring him second in honor after the Bishop of Rome. Canon 3 stated, "The bishop of Constantinople, however, shall have the prerogative of honor after the bishop of Rome because Constantinople is the New Rome."
   - **Ecclesiastical Jurisdictions**: Clarified and reaffirmed the ecclesiastical jurisdictions and the administrative hierarchy of the church.

#### **4. Issuance of Important Canons**
   - **Canons of Discipline**: The council issued several canons (rules) that addressed various aspects of church discipline, organization, and administration.
   - **Handling of Heretics**: Established guidelines for dealing with heretics and those who wished to return to the church.

#### **5. Condemnation of Heretical Groups**
   - **Macedonianism**: Also known as Pneumatomachianism, which denied the divinity of the Holy Spirit.
   - **Apollinarianism**: The belief that Christ had a divine mind instead of a human rational soul.
   - **[[Sabellianism]] and [[Eunomianism]]**: Other heretical teachings regarding the nature of the Trinity and Christ.

#### **Key Figures**
   - **[[Emperor Theodosius I]]**: Convened the council and provided imperial support for its decisions.
   - **[[Gregory of Nazianzus]]**: Played a significant role in the theological discussions and served as the president of the council for a time.
   - **Meletius of Antioch**: Initially presided over the council but died during its proceedings.
   - **Nectarius of Constantinople**: Elected as the Bishop of Constantinople during the council and played a significant role in its deliberations.

### Conclusion

The Second Ecumenical Council at Constantinople was crucial in solidifying the Nicene Creed and expanding it to include a clear affirmation of the Holy Spirit's divinity. By condemning various heresies and clarifying the hierarchical structure of the Church, the council strengthened the doctrinal unity and organizational coherence of early Christianity. Its decisions continue to have a lasting impact on Orthodox and other Christian theological traditions.
