In **[[Greek mythology]]**, the **Muses** (Μοῦσαι) are nine sister goddesses who preside over different realms of the **arts, literature, and sciences**. Daughters of **[[Zeus]]** (king of the gods) and **Mnemosyne** (the personification of memory), they inspire creativity and guide artists, poets, and thinkers. Below is a concise list of the nine Muses and their traditional domains:

1. **Calliope** (Καλλιόπη) – *Epic Poetry* and *Eloquence*  
2. **Clio** (Κλειώ) – *History*  
3. **Euterpe** (Εὐτέρπη) – *Music* (often associated with flutes and lyric poetry)  
4. **Thalia** (Θάλεια) – *Comedy* (and pastoral poetry)  
5. **Melpomene** (Μελπομένη) – *Tragedy*  
6. **Terpsichore** (Τερψιχόρη) – *Dance* (and choral song)  
7. **Erato** (Ἐρατώ) – *Love Poetry* (and lyrics of erotic or amatory themes)  
8. **Polyhymnia** (Πολυύμνια) – *Sacred Hymns* (and religious oratory)  
9. **Urania** (Οὐρανία) – *Astronomy* (and the celestial arts)

### Significance
- **Patrons of Inspiration**: The Muses are invoked by poets and authors to guide them toward literary or artistic excellence.  
- **Cultural Influence**: Ancient Greek artists, philosophers, and historians viewed the Muses as divine sources of insight, ensuring that human endeavors in the arts and sciences were touched by a spark of the sacred or sublime.  

They remain enduring symbols of creativity, knowledge, and the connection between divine inspiration and human artistry.
