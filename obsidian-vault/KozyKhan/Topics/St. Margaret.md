**1. St. Margaret of Antioch (c. 3rd–4th century CE):**

• **Virgin Martyr:** Venerated as a virgin martyr, she is celebrated for her steadfast faith in the face of persecution.

• **Legendary Miracles:** One of the most famous legends about her involves being swallowed by a dragon and then miraculously escaping unharmed, symbolizing the triumph of faith over evil.

• **Widespread Veneration:** She is honored in both the Eastern Orthodox and Roman Catholic Churches and has been an enduring symbol of purity and courage.

  

**2. St. Margaret of Scotland (c. 1045–1093):**

• **Queen Consort:** As the wife of King Malcolm III of Scotland, she played a significant role in promoting Christianity in Scotland.

• **Charitable Works:** Known for her deep piety and charitable initiatives, she worked to improve the lives of the poor and marginalized.

• **Cultural Influence:** Her life and example left a lasting impact on Scottish society, and she is celebrated as a model of Christian virtue and compassion.
