Charles de Gaulle was a dominant figure in 20th-century French history, renowned as a general and statesman.1 Here's a summary of his key roles:

- **World War II Leader:**
    - He led the Free French Forces in opposition to Nazi Germany during World War II.2
    - His famous "Appeal of 18 June" in 1940, broadcast from London, urged the French people to resist the German occupation.3
- **Post-War Political Figure:**
    - He headed the Provisional Government of the French Republic from 1944 to 1946, playing a crucial role in restoring democracy in France.4
    - He was a key architect of the [[Topics/Fifth Republic of France]] and served as its first president from 1959 to 1969.5
- **Key Policies:**
    - He pursued a policy of French independence, particularly in foreign affairs.6
    - He oversaw the granting of independence to Algeria.
    - He also heavily influenced the creation of the current french govermental structure.
- **Legacy:**
    - He is remembered for his strong leadership and his dedication to French national pride.7
    - He is considered one of the most important french figures of the 20th century.8

In essence, Charles de Gaulle was a pivotal figure who shaped modern France, particularly through his wartime leadership and his role in establishing the Fifth Republic.9
