#### Overview
- **Full Name:** World Health Organization (WHO)
- **Established:** April 7, 1948
- **Headquarters:** Geneva, [[Switzerland]]
- **Parent Organization:** [[United Nations]] (UN)
#### Purpose
The WHO is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. Its primary objective is to ensure the highest possible level of health for all people.
#### Key Functions
1. **Global Health Leadership:**
   - Provides leadership on global health matters.
   - Shapes the health research agenda and stimulates the generation, translation, and dissemination of valuable knowledge.
2. **Health Standards and Guidelines:**
   - Sets norms and standards and promotes their implementation.
   - Develops evidence-based policy options and guidelines.
3. **Technical Support and Capacity Building:**
   - Provides technical support to countries.
   - Builds sustainable institutional capacity to address public health challenges.
4. **Monitoring and Assessment:**
   - Monitors the global health situation and assesses health trends.
   - Conducts health surveillance and gathers data to inform policies and programs.
#### Key Programs and Initiatives
1. **Disease Prevention and Control:**
   - **Communicable Diseases:** Works on eradicating or controlling diseases like HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, and neglected tropical diseases.
   - **Non-Communicable Diseases:** Focuses on reducing the burden of diseases like cancer, diabetes, and cardiovascular diseases through prevention and treatment strategies.
2. **Health Emergencies:**
   - **Emergency Response:** Coordinates international response to health emergencies, including outbreaks, natural disasters, and humanitarian crises.
   - **Preparedness and Resilience:** Strengthens countries' capacity to prepare for and respond to health emergencies.
3. **Universal Health Coverage:**
   - Promotes universal health coverage (UHC) to ensure all individuals and communities have access to quality health services without suffering financial hardship.
4. **Mental Health and Substance Abuse:**
   - Implements programs to improve mental health services and address substance abuse disorders.
5. **Maternal and Child Health:**
   - Works to reduce maternal and child mortality and improve reproductive health.
6. **Health Systems Strengthening:**
   - Supports countries in building robust health systems, including workforce development, infrastructure improvement, and health financing.
#### Major Achievements
1. **Smallpox Eradication:**
   - Successfully led the global effort to eradicate smallpox, with the last known natural case in 1977 and official eradication declared in 1980.
2. **Polio Eradication Initiative:**
   - Significant progress in reducing polio cases worldwide through vaccination campaigns, although challenges remain in some regions.
3. **Global Health Standards:**
   - Established international health regulations (IHR) to help countries prevent and respond to acute public health risks that have the potential to cross borders.
4. **COVID-19 Response:**
   - Played a critical role in coordinating the global response to the COVID-19 pandemic, providing guidance, data, and support for vaccine distribution.
#### Challenges and Criticisms
1. **Funding and Resources:**
   - Relies on voluntary contributions from member states and other donors, leading to variability in funding and resource constraints.
2. **Political Pressures:**
   - Faces criticism for perceived political influences and pressures from member states that can impact decision-making and effectiveness.
3. **Bureaucracy and Efficiency:**
   - Criticized for bureaucratic inefficiencies that can slow down response times and implementation of programs.
#### Structure
1. **World Health Assembly (WHA):**
   - The decision-making body, consisting of representatives from all 194 member states.
   - Meets annually to set policies, approve the budget, and review WHO’s work.
2. **Executive Board:**
   - Composed of 34 technically qualified members elected for three-year terms.
   - Implements the decisions and policies of the WHA and advises and facilitates its work.
3. **Secretariat:**
   - Led by the Director-General, who is appointed by the WHA.
   - Manages day-to-day operations and implements programs and initiatives.
#### Current Director-General
- **Name:** Dr. Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus
- **Term:** Elected in May 2017
### Conclusion
The World Health Organization plays a crucial role in addressing global health challenges by providing leadership, setting standards, and coordinating international efforts to improve public health. Despite facing various challenges, the WHO remains a central institution in the global health landscape, striving to ensure health for all.
