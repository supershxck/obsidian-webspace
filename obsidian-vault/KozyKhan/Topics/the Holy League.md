### Introduction
The Holy League of 1684 was a coalition formed by several European powers with the primary goal of combating the expansion of the Ottoman Empire in Europe. This alliance played a significant role in the Great Turkish War (1683-1699), which ultimately led to significant territorial losses for the Ottomans and a shift in the balance of power in Europe.
### Formation and Members
- **Pope Innocent XI**: The Holy League was initiated by Pope Innocent XI, who sought to unite Christian states against the common threat of the Ottoman Empire.
- **Founding Members**: The initial members of the Holy League included the Holy Roman Empire, the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth, and the Republic of Venice.
- **Later Members**: Over time, other states, such as Russia (under Peter the Great), joined the coalition.
### Background
- **Ottoman Expansion**: By the late 17th century, the Ottoman Empire had reached its territorial zenith, exerting control over much of Southeastern Europe, including Hungary and parts of the Balkans.
- **Siege of Vienna (1683)**: The immediate catalyst for the formation of the Holy League was the Ottoman siege of Vienna in 1683. The siege was successfully lifted by the combined forces of the Holy Roman Empire and the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth, led by King John III Sobieski.
### Goals
- **Defense and Offense**: The primary goal of the Holy League was to defend against Ottoman aggression and to reclaim territories lost to the Ottomans. This involved both defensive operations and offensive campaigns to push the Ottomans back.
### Key Campaigns and Battles
- **Battle of Vienna (1683)**: Although technically predating the formal establishment of the Holy League, the Battle of Vienna was crucial. The victory by Christian forces provided the momentum for the formation of the Holy League.
- **Siege of Buda (1686)**: One of the major successes of the Holy League was the capture of Buda (now Budapest) from the Ottomans, which had been under Ottoman control since 1541.
- **Battle of Mohács (1687)**: The League achieved another significant victory at the Battle of Mohács, which further weakened Ottoman control in Hungary.
- **Naval Campaigns**: The Republic of Venice conducted naval operations in the Aegean Sea and the eastern Mediterranean, capturing several key islands and ports from the Ottomans.
### Outcomes
- **Treaty of Karlowitz (1699)**: The Great Turkish War ended with the Treaty of Karlowitz in 1699. The treaty marked a significant turning point, with the Ottomans ceding large territories in Hungary, Transylvania, and the Balkans to the members of the Holy League.
- **Shift in Power**: The successes of the Holy League significantly curtailed Ottoman expansion in Europe and marked the beginning of the Ottoman Empire's gradual decline in the region.
- **Christian Resurgence**: The victories of the Holy League bolstered the position of Christian states in Southeastern Europe and allowed for the re-establishment of Christian control over previously Ottoman-dominated regions.
### Conclusion
The Holy League of 1684 was a pivotal alliance that played a crucial role in curbing Ottoman power in Europe. Through coordinated military efforts, the League's members were able to reclaim significant territories and shift the balance of power in favor of European states. The legacy of the Holy League is reflected in the subsequent decline of Ottoman influence and the rise of European powers in the region.
