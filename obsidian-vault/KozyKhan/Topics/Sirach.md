# **The Book of Sirach**
The Book of Sirach, also known as Ecclesiasticus, was highly regarded by many early Christians and the early Church Fathers. Here's an overview of its reception and how it was viewed within the early Church:
### Early Church Use and Reception of Sirach
#### 1. **Inclusion in the Septuagint**:
   - The Book of Sirach was included in the Septuagint (LXX), the Greek translation of the Hebrew Scriptures. This collection of books was widely used by Greek-speaking Jews and early Christians.
#### 2. **Church Fathers' References**:
   - **Clement of Alexandria**: Clement frequently quoted Sirach, treating it as part of Scripture. In his work *Stromata*, he cites Sirach to support his theological arguments and moral teachings.
   - **Origen**: Origen, one of the most influential early theologians, referred to Sirach in his writings and used it for teaching and doctrinal development.
   - **Athanasius**: Although Athanasius distinguished between canonical and ecclesiastical books, he included Sirach in the latter category, indicating its use in the Church for moral instruction.
   - **Cyprian of Carthage**: Cyprian quoted Sirach in his writings, using its teachings to reinforce Christian ethical and moral behavior.
   - **Augustine**: Augustine frequently cited Sirach, treating it with reverence and incorporating its wisdom into his theological and moral discussions.
#### 3. **Liturgical Use**:
   - The Book of Sirach was often used in the liturgical readings and prayers of the early Church. Its wisdom literature was seen as valuable for teaching and moral edification.
#### 4. **Councils and Canonical Status**:
   - The Council of Carthage (397 AD) and the Council of Hippo (393 AD) listed Sirach as part of the canonical Scriptures. These councils were influential in shaping the canon of the Western Church.
   - In the Eastern Church, Sirach was also held in high esteem and included in the Septuagint, which the Eastern Orthodox Church considers authoritative.
### Theological and Moral Value
- **Wisdom Literature**: Sirach is part of the wisdom literature tradition, offering practical advice on how to live a righteous and godly life. Its teachings on ethics, piety, and social justice were aligned with early Christian values.
- **Christological Readings**: Some Church Fathers saw foreshadowings of Christ and Christian teachings in Sirach’s wisdom and moral guidance.
- **Moral Instruction**: Sirach was used extensively for catechesis and moral instruction, providing a foundation for Christian behavior and community ethics.
## Summary
The Book of Sirach was highly esteemed in the early Church for its wisdom, moral teachings, and theological insights. It was frequently quoted by the Church Fathers, used in liturgical contexts, and included in the early canons of Scripture. Despite some distinctions between canonical and ecclesiastical books, Sirach played a significant role in shaping the ethical and doctrinal framework of early Christianity.
