### [[Petrus Leonis]]
- **Full Name**: Pietro Leoni (also known as Pier Leoni or Peter Leo)
- **Born**: c. 1040
- **Died**: 1128
- **Title**: Roman nobleman, Patrician of [[Rome]]
- **Influence**: Founder of the powerful Pierleoni family in [[medieval Rome]].

#### Background
- **Family Origins**: Born into a wealthy Jewish family in Rome. His grandfather, Baruch, converted to Christianity and adopted the name Benedictus Christianus. His father, Leo de Benedicto, was a wealthy and influential figure in Rome.
- **Conversion**: The family’s conversion to Christianity and subsequent rise in influence are notable aspects of medieval Roman society’s complexities and the interplay between religion and politics.

#### Political Career
- **Patrician Role**: Held the title of Patrician of Rome, a prestigious position that gave him considerable influence over the city's administration and politics.
- **Papal Relations**: Close association with the papacy. His family provided significant financial support to various popes, and several members of his family held important positions within the Church.
- **Conflicts**: Involved in various political and military conflicts in Rome, often aligning with the papacy against other powerful Roman families.

#### Legacy
- **Pierleoni Family**: His descendants, the Pierleoni family, continued to wield considerable power in Rome for several generations. The family produced several notable ecclesiastics, including Antipope Anacletus II.
- **Influence on Rome**: Played a significant role in shaping the political landscape of Rome during a tumultuous period in the city's history. The Pierleoni family's wealth and influence made them key players in the power struggles of medieval Rome.
