**Haile Selassie: A Structured Chapter**

  

**I. Introduction**

  

Haile Selassie I (1892–1975), Ethiopia’s last emperor (reigned 1930–1974), is celebrated as a modernizer, anti‑colonial icon, and central figure in Rastafarian faith. His leadership shaped Ethiopia’s 20th‑century trajectory and Pan‑Africanism.

---

**II. Early Life & Ascension ([[history]])**

• **Born:** Tafari Makonnen in Harar (July 23, 1892).

• **Rise to Power:** Named Regent (1916) under [[Empress Zewditu]]; crowned Emperor Haile Selassie (“Power of the Trinity”) in November 1930.

---

**III. Reign & Achievements**

| **Domain**              | **Achievement**                                  | **Impact**                                                                  |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Modernization           | 1931 Constitution; infrastructure projects       | Centralized state authority; limited political reform                       |
| International Diplomacy | League of Nations appeal (1936)                  | Global condemnation of Italian invasion; symbol of anti‑colonial resistance |
| [[Pan‑Africanism]]      | Founded [[Organization of African Unity]] (1963) | Fostered continental solidarity and decolonization                          |
| Social Reform           | Abolished slavery (1942); education expansion    | Progressive social policy, uneven rural reach                               |

  

---

**IV. Exile & Return**

• **Italian Occupation (1936–1941):** Forced exile in Britain; rallied Ethiopian patriots via radio broadcasts.

• **Liberation:** Returned with Allied support; restored sovereignty.

---

**V. Downfall & Legacy ([[Political Philosophy]], [[psychology]])**

• **Overthrow (1974):** Deteriorating economy, famine, student protests led to Derg coup.

• **Death:** Died under house arrest (1975).

• **Legacy:** Revered as symbol of African dignity; criticized for autocratic rule and failure to democratize.

**IX. Conclusion**

  

Haile Selassie’s complex legacy blends visionary leadership with autocratic shortcomings. Through [[history]], [[Political Philosophy]], and [[psychology]], his reign teaches enduring lessons on balancing charismatic authority, modernization, and democratic accountability.
