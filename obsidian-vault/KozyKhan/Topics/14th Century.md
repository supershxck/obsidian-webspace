**14th Century Influential Figures: A Global Outline (50 Figures)**

  

Below is an outline featuring 50 influential people from the 14th century across various regions. Each name is enclosed in double brackets to emphasize their significance during this transformative era.

---

**I. The Islamic World**

1. **[[Ibn Khaldun]]**

• Renowned historian and sociologist whose work laid the foundations of modern historiography and social theory.

2. **[[Timur (Tamerlane)]]**

• Conqueror and founder of the Timurid Empire, whose campaigns reshaped Eurasia and influenced world history.

3. **[[Sultan Barquq]]**

• A Mamluk Sultan of Egypt whose reign (1382–1399) consolidated power and stabilized the region during turbulent times.

4. **[[Mir Sayyid Ali Hamadani]]**

• A celebrated Sufi mystic and preacher whose spiritual influence extended across Central Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

5. **[[Muhammad bin Tughluq]]**

• An ambitious and controversial ruler of the Delhi Sultanate known for his bold, albeit erratic, administrative reforms.

6. **[[Ibn Battuta]]**

• One of history’s greatest travelers, whose extensive journeys provided invaluable insights into the medieval Islamic world.

7. **[[Sultan al-Nasir Muhammad]]**

• A powerful Mamluk Sultan whose long reign contributed significantly to the cultural and military strength of Egypt.

8. **[[Shah Rukh]]**

• Son of Timur and a key ruler who helped stabilize and culturally enrich the Timurid realm.

9. **[[Ibn Taymiyyah]]**

• Influential theologian and jurist whose ideas would later shape reformist trends in Islamic thought.

10. **[[Firoz Shah Tughlaq]]**

• A ruler of the Delhi Sultanate noted for his architectural projects and attempts to strengthen centralized administration.

---

**II. The Byzantine Empire & Eastern Mediterranean**

11. **[[Andronikos II Palaiologos]]**

• Byzantine Emperor (reigned 1282–1328) who faced internal strife and external threats during a period of gradual decline.

12. **[[Andronikos III Palaiologos]]**

• Emperor (reigned 1328–1341) known for his vigorous military campaigns and efforts to revitalize the empire.

13. **[[John VI Kantakouzenos]]**

• Emperor and historian whose memoirs provide key insights into Byzantine politics and society in the mid-14th century.

14. **[[John V Palaiologos]]**

• Byzantine Emperor whose long, tumultuous reign (1341–1391) was marked by internal divisions and external pressures.

---

**III. Western Europe**

15. **[[Dante Alighieri]]**

• Italian poet whose _Divine Comedy_ remains one of the masterpieces of world literature and a cornerstone of medieval thought.

16. **[[Giotto di Bondone]]**

• Pioneering painter whose innovations in realism and perspective laid the groundwork for the Renaissance.

17. **[[William of Ockham]]**

• Philosopher and theologian best known for formulating the principle of parsimony, or “Ockham’s Razor.”

18. **[[John Wycliffe]]**

• Theologian and reformer whose early criticisms of the Church foreshadowed the Protestant Reformation.

19. **[[Geoffrey Chaucer]]**

• Poet and author of _The Canterbury Tales_, whose vivid portrayals of English society influenced subsequent literature.

20. **[[Edward III of England]]**

• Monarch whose reign (1327–1377) helped shape English national identity and set the stage for the Hundred Years’ War.

21. **[[Philip VI of France]]**

• King whose policies and military campaigns contributed to the evolution of the French state during the early phase of the Hundred Years’ War.

22. **[[Charles V of France]]**

• Monarch celebrated for his effective governance and efforts to restore French fortunes during a period of conflict.

23. **[[Alfonso XI of Castile]]**

• King known for his military successes against the Moors and his role in consolidating Castilian power.

24. **[[Richard II of England]]**

• Controversial king whose reign (1377–1399) was marked by internal strife and shifting alliances among the nobility.

25. **[[John of Gaunt]]**

• A powerful English noble whose political influence and progeny significantly shaped medieval European dynasties.

26. **[[Pope Clement V]]**

• Pontiff whose reign (1305–1314) saw the relocation of the papal court to Avignon, marking the beginning of the Avignon Papacy.

27. **[[Pope John XXII]]**

• Influential pope (1316–1334) whose theological and administrative reforms left a lasting impact on the Church.

28. **[[Pope Benedict XII]]**

• Reformer pope (1334–1342) who sought to restore discipline and address corruption within the Church.

29. **[[Pope Clement VI]]**

• Pontiff (1342–1352) noted for his patronage of the arts and efforts to support the impoverished during times of crisis.

30. **[[Pope Urban V]]**

• One of the last Avignon popes, remembered for his attempts to return the papacy to Rome and reform Church administration.

31. **[[Isabella of France]]**

• Queen consort of England whose political acumen and influence contributed to significant shifts in English governance.

32. **[[David II of Scotland]]**

• King of Scotland (reigned 1329–1371) who faced internal conflicts and external threats while striving to preserve Scottish independence.

33. **[[Casimir III the Great]]**

• Monarch of Poland known for his reforms in law, education, and military organization, laying the foundations for a modern Polish state.

---

**IV. East Asia**

34. **[[Tugh Temür]]**

• Yuan Dynasty emperor whose reign (mid-14th century) was marked by efforts to stabilize a fracturing empire.

35. **[[Chen Youliang]]**

• Rebel leader in China whose state, Chen Han, briefly contested Yuan authority during the tumultuous 14th century.

36. **[[Zhu Yuanzhang]] (Hongwu Emperor)**

• Founder of the Ming Dynasty, whose uprising against the Yuan established a new era in Chinese history.

37. **[[Li Shanchang]]**

• Key advisor to Zhu Yuanzhang, instrumental in organizing the administrative structures of the nascent Ming state.

38. **[[Liu Bowen]]**

• Strategist and scholar whose counsel was vital to Zhu Yuanzhang during the Ming revolutionary period.

39. **[[King Gongmin of Goryeo]]**

• Korean monarch (reigned 1351–1374) who undertook significant reforms and navigated complex relations with neighboring powers.

40. **[[Emperor Go-Daigo]]**

• Japanese emperor whose efforts to restore imperial authority led to the brief Kemmu Restoration, challenging the shogunate’s dominance.

41. **[[Ashikaga Takauji]]**

• Founder of the Ashikaga Shogunate, whose military and political maneuvers redefined Japanese governance in the late 14th century.

---

**V. South Asia**

42. **[[Alauddin Bahman Shah]]**

• Founder of the Bahmani Sultanate in Deccan India, whose establishment of an independent state marked a new chapter in South Asian history.

---

**VI. Africa**

43. **[[Mansa Musa]]**

• Legendary ruler of the Mali Empire (reigned c. 1312–1337), celebrated for his immense wealth and promotion of Islamic culture.

44. **[[Mansa Muhammad]]**

• Successor in the Mali Empire whose reign furthered the empire’s prestige and economic prosperity.

45. **[[Abu Ishaq al-Shatibi]]**

• Influential Maliki jurist whose writings on Islamic jurisprudence and ethics impacted legal thought in North Africa.

46. **[[Yusuf II of Granada]]**

• Nasrid ruler whose governance in the Emirate of Granada exemplified the refined culture of medieval Andalusia.

---

**VII. The Americas**

47. **[[Acamapichtli]]**

• The first ruler of the Aztec Empire, whose leadership (c. 1376–1395) set the stage for the rise of one of Mesoamerica’s most influential civilizations.

---

**VIII. Central Asia**

48. **[[Tokhtamysh]]**

• Khan of the Golden Horde whose reign in the mid-14th century was marked by both internal consolidation and conflicts with neighboring powers.

---

**IX. Additional Figures from Western & Islamic Realms**

49. **[[Isabella of France]]**

• Already noted above for her influence in Western Europe, her role as a political operator extended across borders and shaped dynastic alliances.

50. **[[Sultan Abu Sa’id Bahadur]]**

• A prominent Ilkhanate ruler whose reign in the later 14th century played a critical role in the cultural and administrative evolution of Central Asia.

---

**Concluding Reflections**

  

The 14th century was a period of profound transformation, marked by sweeping conquests, vibrant cultural renaissances, and significant political realignments. From the dynamic leadership of Islamic rulers and the resurgent efforts of Byzantine emperors to the revolutionary changes in East Asia and the evolving landscapes of Western Europe, each of these 50 figures—encapsulated in double brackets—played a pivotal role in shaping their respective regions and, by extension, the course of global history. Their enduring legacies continue to offer rich insights into a world of ceaseless change and interconnected cultural evolution.
