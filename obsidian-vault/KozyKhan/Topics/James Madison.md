**James Madison (1751–1836): Father of the United States Constitution**

---

**Introduction**

  

James Madison was an American statesman, political theorist, and the fourth President of the United States (1809–1817). Renowned as the **“Father of the Constitution,”** his profound influence on American republicanism and the Bill of Rights shaped modern democratic governance.

---

**Early Life & Education**

• **Born:** March 16, 1751 in Port Conway, Virginia

• Educated at the College of New Jersey (now Princeton University)

• Deeply influenced by Enlightenment thought, classical republicanism, and British political philosophy

---

**Foundational Contributions**

| **Contribution**              | **Year**  | **Significance**                                                       |
| ----------------------------- | --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Virginia Plan                 | 1787      | Blueprint for a strong federal government at Constitutional Convention |
| Federalist Papers (#10 & #51) | 1787–1788 | Defended separation of powers and control of factionalism              |
| [[Bill of Rights]]            | 1789      | Championed first ten amendments guaranteeing individual liberties      |

  

---

**Presidential Achievements (1809–1817)**

| **Issue**       | **Action**                               | **Outcome**                                                 |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| [[War of 1812]] | Led nation through conflict with Britain | Asserted U.S. sovereignty; “Second War of Independence”     |
| Domestic Policy | Continued Jeffersonian principles        | Reduced national debt; strengthened democratic institutions |
| Foreign Policy  | Embargo Act repeal; negotiated peace     | Stabilized trade relations                                  |

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Federalism:** Division of power between national and state governments

• **Separation of Powers:** Distinct legislative, executive, and judicial branches

• **Faction:** Group of citizens united by interests adverse to rights of others (Federalist No.10)

• **Republicanism:** Political ideology prioritizing common good and civic virtue
