Below is an in-depth overview of the **Algerian War of Independence**—a seminal anti-colonial struggle that reshaped Algeria, France, and the modern world:

---

**I. Overview**

• **Definition:**

The **Algerian War of Independence** (1954–1962), also known simply as the **Algerian Revolution**, was a conflict between France and Algerian nationalist movements seeking independence after more than a century of French colonial rule.

• **Core Significance:**

The war was among the most significant and brutal anti-colonial conflicts of the 20th century. It influenced global anti-colonial movements, redefined French identity and politics, and dramatically shaped modern Algeria.

---

**II. Historical Background**

  

**A. French Colonization of Algeria (1830–1954)**

• Algeria was invaded and colonized by France in **1830**.

• Colonization led to displacement, oppression, economic exploitation, and discrimination against the indigenous Algerian (primarily Arab and Amazigh) populations.

• By the early 20th century, a large European settler population (**Pieds-Noirs**) dominated political and economic life, enjoying privileges denied to native Algerians.

  

**B. Rise of Nationalist Movements**

• Nationalism intensified post-WWI and WWII, as Algerians served in French forces but continued to face severe discrimination at home.

• Influential nationalist organizations emerged, notably:

• **Étoile Nord-Africaine (ENA)**, founded in 1926.

• **Parti du Peuple Algérien (PPA)** in 1937, and its successor, the **Mouvement pour le Triomphe des Libertés Démocratiques (MTLD)**.

• Frustration over French reluctance to grant significant political reforms pushed Algerian nationalists toward militancy.

---

**III. Outbreak and Phases of the War**

  

**A. Start of the War (1954)**

• The war began on **November 1, 1954**, when the **National Liberation Front (FLN)** launched coordinated guerrilla attacks against French targets, marking the start of the armed struggle.

• The FLN quickly became the main Algerian revolutionary force, uniting various nationalist factions.

  

**B. Guerrilla Warfare and Escalation (1955–1957)**

• The FLN conducted rural guerrilla warfare, seeking international recognition and support from Arab nations, the USSR, China, and other anti-colonial states.

• France responded by deploying hundreds of thousands of troops, instituting repressive measures including collective punishments, forced relocations, and torture.

• **Battle of Algiers (1956–1957):**

A pivotal urban battle characterized by severe violence, terrorism, counter-terrorism, and extensive human rights abuses, highlighted internationally in Gillo Pontecorvo’s film _“The Battle of Algiers”_.

  

**C. Intensified Conflict and International Attention (1958–1961)**

• Political turmoil in France led to the collapse of the **Fourth Republic** and the return of **Charles de Gaulle**, who established the **Fifth Republic** in 1958.

• De Gaulle initially sought military victory but gradually shifted toward negotiating independence due to military stalemate, international pressure, and domestic unrest.

• The FLN intensified diplomatic efforts, winning widespread recognition and support from the UN and newly independent African nations.

  

**D. OAS and Internal French Conflict (1961–1962)**

• The **Organisation Armée Secrète (OAS)**, a far-right paramilitary group composed of hardline settlers and military personnel, violently opposed Algerian independence and carried out terrorist attacks against both Algerians and French officials.

---

**IV. Resolution and Algerian Independence (1962)**

• After extensive negotiations, the **Évian Accords** were signed in **March 1962**, granting Algeria independence following a referendum.

• On **July 5, 1962**, Algeria officially gained independence, ending 132 years of French rule.

• Post-independence, hundreds of thousands of Pieds-Noirs fled Algeria, significantly altering the demographic and economic landscapes of both countries.

---

**V. Human and Societal Costs**

• **Casualties and Atrocities:**

Estimates suggest over 1 million Algerians died during the conflict. French military losses numbered about 25,000, with significant psychological trauma on both sides.

• **Displacement:**

Mass displacements of Algerians and Pieds-Noirs occurred, dramatically reshaping populations.

• **Psychological and Social Impact:**

The war left deep scars on Algerian society and French collective memory, creating lasting divisions and shaping political identities.

---

**VI. Global Impact and Legacy**

• **Influence on Decolonization:**

Algeria’s struggle inspired anti-colonial and revolutionary movements worldwide, particularly in Africa, Asia, and Latin America.

• **French Domestic Politics:**

The war precipitated the collapse of France’s Fourth Republic, birth of the Fifth Republic, and reshaped France’s foreign policy and national identity.

• **Contemporary Relations:**

The historical trauma and memory of the war continue to influence Algeria-France diplomatic, cultural, and political relationships.

---

**VII. Conclusion**

  

The **Algerian War of Independence** was a transformative and traumatic event that marked a decisive end to European colonialism in Africa. Algerian resistance became a symbol of struggle and liberation globally, deeply affecting international politics, colonial powers, and anti-colonial struggles. Its legacy endures today in Algeria’s national identity, France’s historical consciousness, and broader global narratives of freedom, independence, and justice.

  

This comprehensive overview encapsulates the historical depth, human cost, global significance, and lasting legacy of Algeria’s struggle for independence—an event whose repercussions echo throughout the modern world.
