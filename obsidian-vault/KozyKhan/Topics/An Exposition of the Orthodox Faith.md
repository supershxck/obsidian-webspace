"We know the following facts. A) the Theotokos was living in the temple of Jerusalem as a temple virgin. Temple virgins were given in marriage after puberty around age 14. So she would've been placed in the guardianship of St. Joseph around 14. Important that I say guardianship, they were "betrothed" according to the law because otherwise he would not have been able to be her guardian. St. Joseph, being an old man felt awkward and intended to give her in marriage to one of his sons from his first marriage. Before that could happen, the events of the conception and birth of Jesus happened.

We also know that the Theotokos continued to serve in the temple for several years after being given in guardianship to St. Joseph. So all of that to say, she was somewhere between 16-20 at the time of Jesus's birth.

St. James the Brother of the Lord, is one of those children that St. Joseph had from his first marriage.

 [...]A lot of people wrongly mistake "betrothed" with "married". The reason why the Theotokos was given to St. Joseph by the priest's of the temple is because of a miracle. His rod (walking staff) budded like Aaron's did. His from among about a hundred other men who were present to be chosen from. Even among the jews of that time, that age gap was very uncommon and a lot of people ridiculed St. Joseph for it.

In the book "The Life of the Virgin Mary the Theotokos" it is written that St. Joseph was married for 40 years and was a recent widower when he was called to the temple among many others to see to whom the Theotokos would be given.

This book says he was 80 at the time of the choosing in the temple and the Theotokos 13/14 

His 7 children from his first marriage were, James, Jude, Simon, Joses and 3 daughters Salome (named after the first wife, and the mother of Apostle James the younger, and St. John the Theologian) Esther, and the third girl's name hasn't been reliably recorded.

"The enemy of our salvation was keeping an eye on virgins, according to the prophecy of Isiah who spoke of a virgin conceiving. Thus the maiden is given in marriage to Joseph by the priests, thereby deceiving the enemy who always glories in his wisdom. Hence the marriage was both a protection and deception to him who was keeping a watchful eye on virgins"

-St John of Damascus, *An Exposition of the Orthodox Faith*, Book 4"
Subdeacon Niko of ROCOR
