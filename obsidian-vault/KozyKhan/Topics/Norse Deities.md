Below is an extensive outline of many of the Norse deities, organized by their primary groupings and roles within the mythology:

**I. [[The Æsir]]**

  

The Æsir form the principal pantheon of Norse gods, who are chiefly associated with war, wisdom, governance, and fate.

• **[[Odin]]**

• _Role_: The All-Father, chief of [[the Æsir]]; god of wisdom, war, death, magic, and poetry.

• _Key Attributes_: Seeker of knowledge (often at great personal cost), ruler of [[Asgard]], and a central figure in the creation myth.

• **[[Frigg]]**

• _Role_: Odin’s wife and the goddess of marriage, motherhood, and foresight.

• _Key Attributes_: Protector of families and households; associated with prophecy (though she often keeps her knowledge secret).

• **[[Thor]]**

• _Role_: God of thunder, strength, and protection.

• _Key Attributes_: Wields the hammer [[Mjölnir]]; known for battling giants and protecting both gods and mortals.

• **[[Baldr]] (Balder)**

• _Role_: God of beauty, light, love, and purity.

• _Key Attributes_: Symbolizes goodness and innocence; his tragic death is one of the most poignant myths in Norse lore.

• **Höðr ([[Hodr]])**

• _Role_: The blind god associated with darkness; brother of [[Baldr]].

• _Key Attributes_: Involved in the myth of Baldr’s death (manipulated by [[Loki]]).

• **[[Týr]]**

• _Role_: God of war, justice, and law.

• _Key Attributes_: Renowned for sacrificing his hand to bind [[Fenrir]], embodying the high cost of maintaining order.

• **[[Víðarr]]**

• _Role_: God of vengeance; avenges Odin’s death at [[Ragnarök]].

• _Key Attributes_: Known for his immense strength and silent resolve.

• **[[Váli]]**

• _Role_: Born to avenge [[Baldr]]’s death.

• _Key Attributes_: His swift birth and purpose highlight the themes of retribution and cosmic justice.

• **[[Heimdall]]**

• _Role_: Guardian of the [[Bifröst]] (the rainbow bridge); god of vigilance and light.

• _Key Attributes_: Possesses acute senses; foretold to sound the horn at [[Ragnarök]].

• **[[Bragi]]**

• _Role_: God of poetry, music, and eloquence.

• _Key Attributes_: Celebrated for his wisdom and artistry in poetic expression.

• **[[Forseti]]**

• _Role_: God of justice, reconciliation, and mediation.

• _Key Attributes_: Upholds fairness in legal disputes among gods and men.

• **[[Loki]]**

• _Role_: The trickster god, known for his shape-shifting and complex, often ambivalent, morality.

• _Key Attributes_: Both a catalyst for change and a source of mischief; father to several significant monsters (e.g., [[Fenrir]], [[Jörmungandr]], [[Hel]]).

• **[[Ullr]]**

• _Role_: God of archery, hunting, skiing, and winter.

• _Key Attributes_: Associated with skillful hunting and the winter landscape.

• **[[Mímir]]**

• _Role_: Keeper of wisdom and knowledge.

• _Key Attributes_: His well ([[Mímisbrunnr]]) is a source of profound insight; often consulted by the gods for counsel.

• **[[Sif]]**

• _Role_: Wife of [[Thor]]; associated with earth and fertility.

• _Key Attributes_: Known for her golden hair, which symbolizes growth and prosperity.

• **[[Ran]]**

• _Role_: Sea goddess, wife of [[Ægir]].

• _Key Attributes_: Associated with the dangers and mysteries of the ocean; collects the souls of drowned sailors.

• **[[Fulla]]**

• _Role_: Attendant to [[Frigg]].

• _Key Attributes_: Often portrayed as a symbol of domesticity and support in Frigg’s household.

• **[[Lofn]]**

• _Role_: Goddess associated with forbidden love and the uniting of estranged lovers.

• _Key Attributes_: Embodies compassion and the breaking of societal barriers regarding love.

• **[[Syn]]**

• _Role_: Goddess of defensive refusal, who guards against wrongful entry.

• _Key Attributes_: Represents the protective aspects of law and order.

• **[[Saga]]**

• _Role_: Goddess of history, storytelling, and wisdom.

• _Key Attributes_: Associated with the preservation of the past and the lessons it offers.

**II. [[The Vanir]]**

  

The Vanir are another major group of gods, associated primarily with fertility, prosperity, and nature. Their integration into the Æsir reflects a complex mythological reconciliation.

• **[[Freyr]]**

• _Role_: God of fertility, prosperity, sunlight, and fair weather.

• _Key Attributes_: Brings abundance to the earth; often associated with the symbol of his golden boar, Gullinbursti.

• **[[Freyja]]**

• _Role_: Goddess of love, beauty, fertility, and war.

• _Key Attributes_: Possesses both nurturing and martial qualities; often linked to passion and the afterlife for slain warriors.

• **[[Njörðr]] (Njord)**

• _Role_: God of the sea, wind, and wealth.

• _Key Attributes_: Essential to maritime life and trade; associated with the prosperity of coastal communities.

**III. Other Divine and Semi-Divine Figures**

  

These deities or divine beings occupy various specialized roles in Norse mythology, often reflecting aspects of nature or human experience.

• **[[Jörð]]**

• _Role_: Personification of the Earth; sometimes considered a giantess.

• _Key Attributes_: Mother of [[Thor]]; represents fertility and the natural world.

• **[[Sól]]** and **[[Máni]]**

• _Role_: Deities representing the sun (Sól) and the moon (Máni).

• _Key Attributes_: Symbolize the celestial order and the cycles of day and night.

• **[[Nanna]]**

• _Role_: Wife of [[Baldr]].

• _Key Attributes_: Her tragic story following Baldr’s death underscores themes of love and loss.

• **[[Hnoss]]** and **[[Gersemi]]**

• _Role_: Daughters of [[Freyja]].

• _Key Attributes_: Often symbolizing treasures and beauty, reinforcing Freyja’s associations with prosperity.

• **[[Eir]]**

• _Role_: Goddess of healing.

• _Key Attributes_: Known for her ability to restore health and well-being.

• **[[Snotra]]**

• _Role_: Goddess of wisdom and courtesy.

• _Key Attributes_: Embodies refinement and proper social conduct.

• **[[Kvasir]]**

• _Role_: A being born of the gods’ spittle; renowned for his wisdom.

• _Key Attributes_: His blood, after being killed by dwarves, gave rise to the poetic mead—a symbol of divine inspiration and knowledge.

• **Fjörgyn / Jörð**

• _Role_: Often associated with the earth, sometimes considered synonymous with Jörð.

• _Key Attributes_: Represents the nurturing aspect of nature.


**Other Supernatural Beings**


• **[[Light Elves]] (Ljósálfar)**

• _Role_: Ethereal beings of light, residing in [[Alfheim]]; associated with beauty, wisdom, and the blessings of nature.


• **[[Dark Elves]] (Dökkálfar / Svartálfar)**

• _Role_: Often conflated with dwarves, these beings dwell underground and are renowned for their craftsmanship and connection to hidden knowledge.


• **[[Dwarves]] (Dvergar)**

• _Role_: Master smiths and craftsmen who create many of the gods’ most treasured artifacts (e.g., [[Mjölnir]], [[Gungnir]], and [[Draupnir]]).


**[[the Giants]] (Jötnar)**

• **[[Frost Giants]]** like Ymir, the primordial giant, and [[Thiazi]], known for his role in the abduction of [[Idunn]], exemplify the raw, untamed forces of ice and cold.

• **[[Fire Giants]]**, led by figures like [[Surtr]], embody the destructive and transformative power of fire, central to the myth of Ragnarök.


• **[[Norns]]**

• _Role_: Female beings who shape destiny, similar to [[the Fates]] in [[Greek mythology]].

• _Key Attributes_: They weave the fates of gods and men at the base of [[Yggdrasil]].


**[[Valkyries]]**

• These warrior maidens serve [[Odin]] by selecting the bravest warriors to join him in [[Valhalla]], thus playing a vital role in the mythology surrounding battle, honor, and the afterlife.

**V. Conclusion**

  

This outline captures a broad spectrum of Norse deities, from the commanding figures of the Æsir and the fertile powers of the Vanir to other divine and semi-divine beings that contribute to the rich tapestry of Norse mythology. Each deity, with their unique attributes and roles, plays an integral part in the complex interplay of forces that define the Norse cosmos—a legacy that continues to inspire literature, art, and modern spiritual practices.

Feel free to explore any of these figures further, as Norse mythology is replete with fascinating stories and intricate relationships that reveal much about the ancient Norse worldview.
