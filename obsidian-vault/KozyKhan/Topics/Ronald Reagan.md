**Ronald Reagan (1911–2004): The Great Communicator and Champion of Neoliberal Resurgence**

---

**Introduction**

  

Ronald Wilson Reagan served as the 40th President of the United States (1981–1989), spearheading a transformative conservative revolution that reinvigorated [[Neoliberalism]] in American domestic and foreign policy. His presidency reshaped government’s role in the economy, solidified free‑market principles, and redefined American global leadership at the close of the Cold War.

---

**Early Life & Rise to Prominence**

• **Born:** February 6, 1911 in Tampico, Illinois

• Began career as Hollywood actor and union leader (Screen Actors Guild president)

• Elected Governor of California (1967–1975), promoting tax cuts and deregulation

---

**Presidential Tenure (1981–1989)**

|**Policy Domain**|**Initiative**|**Impact**|
|---|---|---|
|Economic|**Reaganomics**: 25% top marginal tax rate cut (1981 Tax Reform); deregulation; spending restraint (excluding defense)|Short‑term recession; sustained economic growth; widening income inequality|
|Monetary|Supported Federal Reserve’s tight money policy|Inflation fell from 13.5% (1980) to 4.1% (1988)|
|Foreign Policy|Military buildup; Strategic Defense Initiative (“Star Wars”); diplomatic pressure on USSR|Accelerated end of Cold War; arms reduction treaties|
|Social|Opposed expansion of welfare; “Morning in America” optimism|Shifted public discourse toward self‑reliance; social safety net reductions|

  

---

**Philosophical & Psychological Dimensions**

|**Dimension**|**Insight**|
|---|---|
|Collective Shadow|Emphasized individualism and market triumphalism, sidelining communal solidarity (Jungian critique)|
|Ethical Vision|Framed economic liberty as moral imperative, melding patriotism with free‑enterprise ethos|

**Legacy & Long‑Term Effects**

|**Domain**|**Legacy**|
|---|---|
|Economic Policy|Institutionalized [[Neoliberalism]] in U.S.; inspired global austerity and privatization waves|
|Political Realignment|Solidified conservative coalition; weakened labor unions; reshaped Republican Party|
|Global Order|Cemented U.S. as sole superpower; promoted democracy through market expansion|

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Reaganomics:** Supply‑side economic policy combining tax cuts, deregulation, and spending control

• **Supply‑Side Economics:** Theory that lower taxes increase production, jobs, and revenue

• **Strategic Defense Initiative (SDI):** Proposed missile defense system leveraging advanced technology

• **Morning in America:** Campaign motif emphasizing national renewal and optimism
