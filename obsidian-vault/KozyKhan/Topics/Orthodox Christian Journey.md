Jesus, the divine Word of God in human flesh, teaches by His Presence, His words and His deeds. His disciples are sent into the world to proclaim Jesus Christ and the "good news" of the Kingdom of God. Those whom Jesus chose were called apostles. They were directly inspired by God's Holy Spirit, the Spirit of Truth John( 15:26), to "make disciples of all nations," teaching them what Christ has commanded (Matthew 28:19).

The early Church "devoted itself to the apostles' doctrine [teaching or instruction]" (Acts 2:42). The disciples of every age and generation accept the apostles' doctrine as the very doctrine of God, proclaiming it everywhere, always, as the doctrine of eternal life through which all are enlightened and saved.

It is also the doctrine of the Christian Church that all sincere striving after truth is fulfilled in Christ. Every true insight into the meaning of life finds its perfection in the Christian Gospel.

The Word and Wisdom of God are revealed to all and found in all who, in the purity of their minds and hearts, have been inspired by the Divine Light. **This Divine Light is the Word of God, Jesus of Nazareth in human flesh, the perfection and fullness of God's self-revelation to the world.**

It cannot be overemphasized that divine revelation in the Old Testament, the New Testament Church, the lives of the saints, wisdom of the Fathers, beauty of creation...and most fully and perfectly, in Jesus Christ, the Son of God, is the revelation of God Himself. God has spoken. He has made Himself known and continues to make Himself known in the lives of His people.

To hear God's voice and see God's actions of self-revelation in the world, we must purify our minds and hearts from everything wicked and false. We must strive to love the truth, love one another and love everything in God's good creation.
