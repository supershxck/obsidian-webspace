**The Trojan War: The Greatest War in Greek Mythology**

  

The **Trojan War** was a legendary conflict between **the Greeks (Achaeans) and the Trojans**, fought over **Helen of Troy**, the most beautiful woman in the world. Though the war lasted **ten years**, its origins stretch back decades before, involving **divine rivalries, fate, and human ambition**. The war ended with **the Greeks’ victory through deception—the Trojan Horse—but its aftermath led to suffering, tragedy, and the downfall of many heroes**.

**I. The Origins of the Trojan War: Fate, Love, and Divine Wrath**

  

**1. The Wedding of Peleus and Thetis**

• The war’s origins trace back to **the marriage of [[Peleus]] (a mortal king) and [[Thetis]] (a sea goddess)**.

• **[[Eris]], the goddess of discord, was not invited** and, in revenge, threw **the Golden Apple of Discord** into the wedding, inscribed with _“To the fairest.”_

  

**2. The Judgment of Paris**

• **[[Hera]], Athena, and Aphrodite** all claimed the apple.

• **Zeus appointed Paris, prince of Troy, to decide**.

• Each goddess **offered him a bribe**:

• **[[Hera]]** promised **power and kingship**.

• **[[Athena]]** promised **wisdom and victory in battle**.

• **[[Aphrodite]]** promised **the love of the most beautiful woman—Helen of Sparta**.

• **Paris chose Aphrodite**, setting the war in motion.

  

**3. The Abduction (or Elopement) of Helen**

• Helen, **wife of Menelaus, king of Sparta**, was either **seduced or abducted** by Paris.

• Menelaus, enraged, called upon **the Oath of Tyndareus**, a pact sworn by all of Helen’s former suitors to **protect her marriage**.

• **The Greek kings united**, launching a massive war against Troy.

**II. The Greek and Trojan Forces**

  

**1. The Greek (Achaean) Army**

• **Agamemnon (Leader of the Greeks)** – King of Mycenae, brother of Menelaus.

• **Achilles (Greatest Warrior)** – The nearly invincible son of Peleus and Thetis.

• **Odysseus (The Cunning One)** – King of Ithaca, master of deception.

• **Ajax the Great (The Giant Warrior)** – Second only to Achilles in battle.

• **Nestor (The Wise Elder)** – A respected advisor.

• **Diomedes (The Fearless Fighter)** – Wounded even the gods in battle.

  

**2. The Trojan Army**

• **Priam (King of Troy)** – Aged and noble ruler of the city.

• **Hector (Troy’s Greatest Warrior)** – Son of Priam, the defender of Troy.

• **Paris (The Cause of the War)** – Prince of Troy, lover of Helen, weak in battle.

• **Aeneas (The Survivor)** – Future founder of Rome.

• **Sarpedon (The Divine Warrior)** – Son of Zeus, destined to die.

**III. The Ten-Year War: Key Events and Battles**

  

**1. The Sacrifice of Iphigenia**

• The Greeks were **unable to set sail** because Artemis was **angry at Agamemnon**.

• To appease her, Agamemnon **sacrificed his own daughter, Iphigenia**, which later led to **his own tragic fate**.

  

**2. Achilles vs. Agamemnon: The Rage of Achilles**

• During the war, Agamemnon **seized Achilles’ war prize, Briseis**, causing Achilles to **withdraw from battle**.

• Without Achilles, the Greeks **suffered heavy losses**.

  

**3. The Death of Patroclus and Achilles’ Revenge**

• **Hector killed Patroclus**, Achilles’ closest companion.

• Enraged, **Achilles returned to battle**, slaying Hector and **dragging his body behind his chariot**.

  

**4. The Death of Achilles**

• Achilles was finally **killed by Paris**, who **shot an arrow guided by Apollo** into his **only weakness—his heel**.

  

**5. The Trojan Horse and the Fall of Troy**

• After years of battle, **Odysseus devised the Trojan Horse**.

• The Greeks **hid inside the wooden horse**, which the Trojans **brought into the city**.

• That night, the Greeks **emerged, opened the gates, and destroyed Troy**.

**IV. The Aftermath: The End of Heroes**

  

**1. The Fate of the Trojans**

• **Priam was slaughtered** at Zeus’ altar.

• **Hector’s son was thrown from the walls** to prevent him from avenging Troy.

• **Aeneas fled**, later founding **Rome (according to Roman myth)**.

  

**2. The Doomed Returns of the Greek Kings**

• **Agamemnon was murdered** by his wife, Clytemnestra, upon his return.

• **Menelaus took Helen back to Sparta**, but their marriage remained cursed.

• **Odysseus wandered for ten more years**, facing **monsters, gods, and trials**, in _The Odyssey_.

**V. Symbolism and Legacy**

  

**1. The Trojan War as Fate vs. Free Will**

• The war was **foretold by prophecy**, yet **driven by human choices and divine meddling**.

  

**2. The Role of Hubris and Arrogance**

• Paris’ arrogance in **choosing Aphrodite** doomed his city.

• Agamemnon’s **pride led to his downfall**.

• Achilles’ **rage cost him everything**.

  

**3. The Trojan War in Later Cultures**

• Inspired **Homer’s Iliad and Odyssey**.

• Became the foundation of **Roman mythology** through **Aeneas**.

• Its themes of **war, heroism, and tragedy** still influence literature today.

**VI. Conclusion: The War That Shaped Mythology**

  

The **Trojan War was more than just a battle—it was the ultimate clash of gods, fate, and human ambition**. It **destroyed heroes, ended an age, and reshaped the world**, leaving behind **one of the greatest stories ever told**. From Achilles’ wrath to the cunning of Odysseus, **its legacy still echoes in literature, art, and history**.
