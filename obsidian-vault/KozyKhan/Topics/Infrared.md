**[[Infrared]] Radiation: Invisible Heat Beyond the Visible Spectrum**

  

**I. Definition & Discovery**

  

**Infrared (IR)** is electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light (700 nm–1 mm) but shorter than microwaves. Discovered by [[William Herschel]] in 1800 while measuring temperature beyond the red end of the visible spectrum, infrared revealed a hidden realm of thermal energy.

---

**II. Physical Properties**

|**Property**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|Wavelength Range|0.7–1000 micrometers (µm)|
|Frequency Range|300 GHz–430 THz|
|Photon Energy|1.24–0.00124 eV|
|Subdivisions|Near‑IR (0.7–1.4 µm), Mid‑IR (1.4–3 µm), Far‑IR (3–1000 µm)|

  

---

**III. Production & Detection**

• **Emission:** All objects above absolute zero emit infrared according to Planck’s law; hotter objects emit shorter‑wavelength IR.

• **Sources:** Sun, incandescent bulbs, heated surfaces, specialized IR LEDs and lasers.

• **Detectors:** Thermocouples, bolometers, pyroelectric sensors, and InGaAs semiconductor photodiodes convert IR energy to electrical signals.

---

**IV. Interaction with Matter**

|**Interaction**|**Effect**|**Application**|
|---|---|---|
|Absorption|Molecule vibrational excitation|Infrared spectroscopy for chemical identification|
|Reflection|Surface emissivity mapping|Thermal imaging contrast|
|Transmission|Penetration through smoke/fog|Night‑vision systems|

---

**VI. Safety Considerations**

• **Non‑Ionizing Radiation:** Infrared does not carry enough energy to ionize atoms; safe at moderate exposure.

• **Thermal Hazard:** Prolonged exposure to high‑power IR can cause burns or eye damage—use shielding and eye protection.

**VIII. Vocabulary & Key Terms**

• **Emissivity:** Efficiency with which a surface emits IR radiation

• **Thermography:** Imaging technique capturing thermal IR patterns

• **Blackbody Radiation:** Idealized emitter of full spectrum IR according to temperature

• **Planck’s Law:** Governs spectral distribution of emitted radiation

• **Bolometer:** Sensor measuring IR intensity via temperature change
