**Ulysses S. Grant (1822–1885): Civil War General and Reconstruction President**

---

**Introduction**

  

Ulysses Simpson Grant was the 18th President of the United States (1869–1877) and the Union Army’s commanding general who secured victory in the American Civil War. His leadership preserved the Union, ended slavery, and his presidency advanced Reconstruction, civil rights, and federal authority.

---

**Early Life & Military Career**

• **Born:** April 27, 1822 in Point Pleasant, Ohio

• **Education:** United States Military Academy at West Point (Class of 1843)

• Served in the Mexican‑American War; resigned in 1854 and struggled in civilian life until Civil War outbreak

---

**Civil War Achievements**

|**Campaign**|**Year**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|---|
|Capture of Fort Donelson|1862|First major Union victory (“Unconditional Surrender”)|
|Vicksburg Campaign|1863|Secured Mississippi River, splitting Confederacy|
|Chattanooga & Atlanta|1863–1864|Opened Deep South to invasion|
|Overland & Appomattox|1864–1865|Defeated Robert E. Lee; surrendered April 9, 1865|

  

---

**Presidency (1869–1877)**

  

**Reconstruction & Civil Rights**

|**Policy**|**Action**|**Impact**|
|---|---|---|
|Enforcement Acts|Protected Black voting rights against Ku Klux Klan violence|Reduced insurgent activity temporarily|
|15th Amendment|Supported ratification guaranteeing African American male suffrage|Expanded constitutional civil rights|
|Civil Service Reform|Began merit‑based appointments|Limited patronage but faced resistance|

**Economic & Foreign Policy**

|**Initiative**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|
|Gold Standard|Stabilized currency post‑Panic of 1873|
|Treaty of Washington (1871)|Settled U.S.–UK disputes (Alabama claims) peacefully|

  

---

**Controversies & Challenges**

• Multiple administration scandals (Whiskey Ring, Crédit Mobilier) tarnished reputation

• Economic depression (Panic of 1873) undermined political support

• Retreat from Reconstruction in final term led to rollback of civil rights gains
