**Eurybia (Εὐρυβία)** is a notable figure in **Greek mythology**, revered as the **goddess of mastery over the seas**, **sea winds**, and **the power of the ocean**. Her name, derived from the Greek words _eurys_ (εὐρύς) meaning “broad” or “wide” and _bia_ (βία) meaning “force” or “power,” aptly reflects her dominion over the expansive and potent forces of the marine world. As one of the **Protogenoi**—the primordial deities who emerged at the dawn of creation—Eurybia plays a crucial role in the divine hierarchy, bridging the elemental forces of nature with the more anthropomorphic Olympian gods. Below is a comprehensive overview of Eurybia’s mythology, roles, attributes, myths, worship, cultural significance, and enduring legacy.

  

**1. Origins and Family**

  

**Parentage**

• **Primordial Origins**: Eurybia is considered one of the **Protogenoi**, the first-generation deities born from the primordial forces of the universe. In **Hesiod’s** **_Theogony_**, Eurybia is described as the **daughter of Pontus** (the Sea) and **Gaia** (Earth), positioning her as a fundamental personification of the sea’s commanding and expansive aspects.

• **Alternative Accounts**: In some traditions, Eurybia is depicted as the daughter of **Thalassa** (another personification of the sea) and **Pontus**, further emphasizing her deep connection to marine elements and the natural world’s vastness.

  

**Siblings**

• **Protogenoi Siblings**: As a primordial deity, Eurybia does not have direct siblings in the traditional sense but is associated with other Protogenoi like **Nereus**, **Thaumas**, **Phorcys**, and **Ceto**. These relationships highlight the interconnectedness of the fundamental forces that shape the cosmos.

  

**Consort and Offspring**

• **Consort**:

• **Poecalion**: In some accounts, Eurybia is married to **Poecalion**, a figure associated with creative endeavors and craftsmanship.

• **Poseidon (Ποσειδῶν)**: In other traditions, she is considered the consort of **Poseidon**, the Olympian god of the sea, further solidifying her role in maritime domains.

• **Children**:

• **Astraeus (Ἀστραῖος)**: The god of the dusk, astrology, and the stars.

• **Pallas (Πάλλας)**: A minor Titan associated with warcraft and strategy.

• **Other Offspring**: Depending on the source, Eurybia may have other children who embody various aspects of the natural and divine world, reflecting her influence over the seas and beyond.

  

**Extended Lineage**

• **Genealogical Importance**: Through her consorts and children, Eurybia is connected to a wide array of deities and heroes, influencing numerous myths and establishing her as a central figure in the divine hierarchy. Her lineage underscores her impact on both divine and mortal realms, particularly in matters related to the ocean’s vast and powerful forces.

• **Mediator of Storms and Calm**: Her ability to control the winds allows her to mediate between storms and calm seas, reflecting the dual nature of the ocean as both a source of life and a realm of formidable challenges.

  

**Symbolism**
