**Classical Liberalism: Foundations of Individual Liberty and Limited Government**

---

**Introduction**

  

**Classical Liberalism** is the political and economic philosophy originating in the 17th–19th centuries that champions individual freedom, private property, free markets, and minimal state intervention. It laid the intellectual groundwork for modern democratic governance and capitalist economies.

---

**Historical Roots**

| **Era**      | **Milestone**                       | **Key Figure**       |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| 17th Century | Emergence of social contract theory | [[John Locke]]       |
| 18th Century | Enlightenment expansion of rights   | [[Adam Smith]]       |
| 19th Century | Political institutionalization      | [[John Stuart Mill]] |

  

---

**Core Principles**

|**Principle**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|Individual Liberty|Sovereignty of the individual over self‑ownership|
|Limited Government|State’s sole roles: protect rights, enforce contracts, defend borders|
|Rule of Law|Equal legal protection; transparent laws|
|Free Markets|Voluntary exchange without coercive regulation|
|Private Property|Foundation for economic incentives and personal autonomy|

  

---

**Key Thinkers & Works**

|**Thinker**|**Contribution**|**Landmark Work**|
|---|---|---|
|John Locke|Natural rights theory|_Two Treatises of Government_ (1689)|
|Adam Smith|Invisible hand of markets|_The Wealth of Nations_ (1776)|
|John Stuart Mill|Harm principle; individual sovereignty|_On Liberty_ (1859)|

**Legacy & Modern Relevance**

• **Economic Policy:** Basis for deregulation, free trade agreements, and global markets

• **Political Institutions:** Influenced constitutional democracies, separation of powers, and human rights charters

• **Contemporary Debates:** Balancing market freedom with social equity amid rising inequality

---

|**Step**|**Action**|**Outcome**|

|2|Strengthen Rule of Law|Ensure property rights and contract enforcement|
|3|Promote Free Trade|Expand market access and consumer choice|

---

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Harm Principle:** Only justification for limiting liberty is prevention of harm to others

• **Invisible Hand:** Unintended social benefits from individuals pursuing self-interest

• **Natural Rights:** Inherent human rights to life, liberty, and property

• **Laissez‑Faire:** Economic doctrine of minimal governmental interference
