**Formalism: Prioritizing Structure Over Content**

---

**Introduction**

  

**Formalism** is an analytical approach in arts and criticism that emphasizes an artwork’s form—its structure, style, technique, and internal relationships—rather than its content, context, or authorial intent. It asserts that meaning emerges from the work’s own formal properties.

---

**I. Historical Origins**

|**Era**|**Development**|**Key Proponent**|
|---|---|---|
|Early 20th Century|Reaction against Romantic subjectivity|Clive Bell, Roger Fry|
|1910s–1930s|Russian Formalism in literary theory|Viktor Shklovsky, Roman Jakobson|
|Mid‑20th Century|New Criticism in Anglo‑America|Cleanth Brooks, T.S. Eliot|

  

---

**II. Core Principles**

|**Principle**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|Autonomy of the Work|Art stands alone; should be evaluated on its internal coherence|
|“Defamiliarization”|Technique making the familiar seem strange to heighten perception (Shklovsky)|
|Close Reading|Detailed analysis of formal elements (language, rhythm, composition)|
|Rejection of Biographical/Contextual Critique|Meaning is intrinsic, not derived from artist’s life or social milieu|

  

---

**III. Applications Across Disciplines**

|**Field**|**Example**|**Focus**|
|---|---|---|
|Literature|Analysis of metaphor, narrative structure|Internal textual patterns|
|Visual Arts|Formal analysis of color, line, composition|Visual harmony and balance|
|Music|Examination of motif, harmony, form|Structural development (sonata form)|
|Film|Editing rhythm, mise-en-scène|Cinematic language and style|

  

---

**IV. Psychological & Philosophical Dimensions**

|**Dimension**|**Insight**|
|---|---|
|Jungian Shadow|Formalism’s detachment from context risks repressing emotional and cultural complexity|
|Epistemology|Suggests knowledge arises through perceiving form rather than external reference|

  

---

**V. Critiques & Counterpoints**

|**Critique**|**Formalist Response**|
|---|---|
|Ignores Social Context|Formal coherence offers universal criteria for aesthetic value|
|Overlooks Meaning|Formal relationships generate implicit meaning|

  

---

**VI. Practical Framework: Conducting a Formalist Analysis**

|**Step**|**Action**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|---|
|1|Identify Formal Elements|Catalog structure, style, technique|
|2|Examine Relationships|Analyze how elements interact (contrast, repetition)|
|3|Interpret Effects|Assess how form evokes aesthetic response|
|4|Evaluate Coherence|Judge unity and integrity of the work|

  

---

**VII. Key Vocabulary**

• **Defamiliarization (Ostranenie):** Technique to make perception fresh

• **Close Reading:** Intensive focus on formal detail

• **Autotelic:** Self‑contained purpose of a work

• **Mise-en-Scène:** Visual arrangement in film/theater |
