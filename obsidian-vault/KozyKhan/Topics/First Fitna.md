The First Fitna (Arabic: الفتنة الكبرى al-fitna al-kubrā, "the great sedition") was the first major civil war within the Islamic community. It led to the overthrow of the Rashidun Caliphate and the establishment of the Umayyad Caliphate. Here's a breakdown of the key aspects:

**Key Points:**

- **Timeline:**
    - Roughly 656–661 CE.
- **Causes:**
    - The assassination of the third caliph, Uthman ibn Affan, in 656. This created a power vacuum and sparked controversy over succession.
    - Disputes over the legitimacy of the fourth caliph, Ali ibn Abi Talib.
    - Political rivalries and regional tensions within the expanding Islamic empire.
- **Key Players:**
    - Ali ibn Abi Talib (the fourth caliph).
    - Mu'awiya ibn Abi Sufyan (governor of Syria).
    - Aisha (one of the Prophet Muhammad's wives).
    - Talha and Zubayr (prominent companions of the Prophet Muhammad).
    - The Kharijites (a group that seceded from Ali's army).
- **Major Events:**
    - **The Assassination of Uthman:** This event was a major catalyst, creating widespread unrest.
    - **The Battle of the Camel (656):** A battle between Ali's forces and those of Aisha, Talha, and Zubayr.
    - **The Battle of Siffin (657):** A battle between Ali and Mu'awiya, which resulted in a stalemate and led to the controversial arbitration.
    - **The emergence of the Kharijites:** A group that broke away from Ali's supporters due to their disagreement with the arbitration.
    - **The Assassination of Ali (661):** Ali was assassinated by a Kharijite.
    - **Mu'awiya's ascension:** Following Ali's death, Mu'awiya consolidated power, establishing the Umayyad Caliphate.
- **Consequences:**
    - A permanent schism within the Muslim community, which contributed to the development of Sunni and Shia Islam.
    - The end of the Rashidun Caliphate and the beginning of the Umayyad Caliphate.
    - Increased political instability within the Islamic empire.

In essence, the First Fitna was a period of intense conflict that had profound and lasting consequences for the history of Islam.
