**Chapter: Alaska Permanent Fund — America’s Resource Dividend Model**

  

**Introduction**

  

The **Alaska Permanent Fund (APF)** is a pioneering sovereign wealth fund created in 1976 to convert Alaska’s finite oil revenues into perpetual public wealth. With **approximately USD 81 billion** under management (2025), the APF uniquely combines intergenerational savings with direct citizen dividends.

---

**Origins & Mandate**

• **Founded:** 1976 via constitutional amendment

• **Primary Objectives:**

1. Preserve a share of oil wealth for future generations

2. Stabilize state revenue volatility

3. Provide annual dividends to all eligible Alaskan residents

---

**Governance & Structure**

|**Entity**|**Role**|**Accountability**|
|---|---|---|
|Alaska Permanent Fund Corporation (APFC) Board|Strategic oversight & policy|Reports to Alaska Legislature|
|Executive Management|Portfolio execution & risk management|Quarterly performance updates|
|Alaska Legislature|Dividend formula & fiscal policy|Annual budgetary review|
|Audit & Risk Committee|Compliance & internal audit|Independent review|

  

---

**Investment Strategy & Portfolio**

|**Asset Class**|**Approx. Allocation**|**Purpose**|
|---|---|---|
|Public Equities|60%|Long‑term growth|
|Fixed Income|25%|Capital preservation|
|Real Assets|10%|Inflation hedge|
|Alternatives|5%|Return enhancement|

• **Geographic Diversification:** Global developed & emerging markets

• **Time Horizon:** Intergenerational focus balancing growth with capital preservation

• **Dividend Policy:**  Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend (PFD) formula distributes ~25% of five‑year average earnings annually

---

|Transparent governance|Volatile dividend payouts|ESG-themed allocations|Market volatility|
|Long-term horizon|Limited scale vs peers|Impact investing|Fiscal policy shifts|

  

---

**Psychological & Philosophical Reflection**

  

The APF embodies the “Shared Inheritance” archetype—transforming collective natural wealth into universal prosperity. Psychologically, it fosters community cohesion; philosophically, it actualizes distributive justice and stewardship.

---

**Actionable Takeaways & Frameworks**

1. **Dividend‑Linked Savings Model:** Allocate a fixed percentage of surplus revenue into a fund that provides regular stakeholder dividends.

2. **Balanced Portfolio Matrix:** Map asset classes across growth vs stability axes; rebalance annually to align with return objectives and risk tolerance.

3. **Fiscal Buffer Framework:** Define withdrawal limits based on multi‑year average returns to mitigate revenue volatility.
