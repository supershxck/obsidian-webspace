### History of the Akkadian Empire and People

#### Early Beginnings
- **Origins (~2350 BCE)**
  - The Akkadians were a Semitic people who settled in central Mesopotamia, around the region of Akkad.
  - They shared cultural and technological advancements with their Sumerian neighbors, including the cuneiform writing system.

#### Rise of the Akkadian Empire
- **Sargon the Great (~2334-2279 BCE)**
  - **[[Sargon of Akkad]]**: Founder of the Akkadian Empire, known for his military conquests and administrative prowess.
  - **Unification of Mesopotamia**: Sargon conquered Sumerian city-states, unifying them under his rule and creating the first known empire in history.
  - **Akkad**: Capital city of the empire, its exact location remains unknown but is believed to be near present-day Baghdad.

#### Expansion and Administration
- **Military Campaigns**
  - **Conquests**: Expansion of the empire to include parts of Syria, Anatolia, and Elam.
  - **Standing Army**: Creation of a professional army to maintain control over vast territories.
- **Governance**
  - **Centralized Administration**: Establishment of a bureaucratic system with governors (ensi) overseeing various regions.
  - **Economic Control**: Standardization of weights and measures, and development of trade networks.

#### Cultural Achievements
- **Art and Architecture**
  - **Stelae and Reliefs**: Commemorative monuments like the Stele of Naram-Sin, depicting military victories and divine favor.
  - **Ziggurats and Temples**: Construction of grand temples dedicated to gods such as Enlil and Ishtar.
- **Language and Literature**
  - **Akkadian Language**: Official language of the empire, written in cuneiform script. It later influenced Babylonian and Assyrian dialects.
  - **Epic Poetry**: Stories such as the Epic of Gilgamesh were preserved and adapted in Akkadian.

#### Notable Rulers
- **Rimush and Manishtushu (~2279-2218 BCE)**
  - Sons of Sargon, who continued to consolidate and expand the empire despite internal revolts and external threats.
- **Naram-Sin (~2254-2218 BCE)**
  - Grandson of Sargon, who declared himself a god and reached the peak of Akkadian power. His reign is marked by military campaigns and extensive building projects.
- **Shar-Kali-Sharri (~2217-2193 BCE)**
  - Faced significant challenges, including invasions and internal strife, leading to the eventual decline of the empire.

#### Decline and Fall
- **Internal Struggles**
  - **Succession Crises**: Dynastic disputes weakened central authority.
  - **Economic Strain**: Overextension and administrative burdens caused economic difficulties.
- **External Threats**
  - **Gutian Invasions**: Nomadic Gutians from the Zagros Mountains invaded Mesopotamia, contributing to the collapse of Akkadian control.
  - **Elamite Incursions**: Pressure from Elamite forces in the east further destabilized the empire.
- **Collapse (~2154 BCE)**
  - The Akkadian Empire fragmented into smaller city-states and was eventually replaced by the rise of the Third Dynasty of Ur.

#### Legacy and Influence
- **Cultural Impact**
  - **Language and Writing**: Akkadian remained a lingua franca in Mesopotamia for centuries, influencing subsequent cultures.
  - **Political Models**: The concept of a centralized empire with a professional army and bureaucracy influenced later empires, including Babylon and Assyria.
- **Archaeological Discoveries**
  - **Artifacts and Inscriptions**: Numerous relics, including statues, tablets, and stelae, provide insights into Akkadian society and governance.

### Summary
The Akkadian Empire, established by [[Sargon of Akkad]], marked the first unified empire in history, setting a precedent for future empires in terms of administration, military organization, and cultural achievements. Despite its eventual fall due to internal and external pressures, the legacy of the Akkadian people continued to shape the history and development of Mesopotamian civilizations.
