**Ethiopia: A Structured Chapter**

  

**I. Introduction**

  

Ethiopia—officially the Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia—is Africa’s oldest independent nation and second‑most populous country (120 million). Renowned for its unique cultural heritage, ancient civilization, and strategic geopolitical role in the Horn of Africa, Ethiopia exemplifies resilience amid complex historical, social, and economic dynamics.

---

**II. Historical Context ([[history]])**

| **Era**                         | **Key Developments**                                                            |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Ancient (c.100 CE–940 CE)       | Aksumite Empire: early Christian kingdom; trade hub between Rome, India, Arabia |
| Medieval (1100–1500)            | Zagwe & Solomonic dynasties; rock‑hewn churches of Lalibela                     |
| Early Modern (1500–1855)        | Islamic invasions, Ethiopian Orthodox consolidation                             |
| Colonial Resistance (1896)      | Victory at Battle of Adwa against Italy — symbol of African sovereignty         |
| [[Haile Selassie]] (1930–1974)  | Modernization reforms; League of Nations appeal; Italian occupation (1936–1941) |
| Derg Regime (1974–1991)         | Marxist‑Leninist dictatorship; Red Terror; famines                              |
| Federal Republic (1995–Present) | Ethnic federalism; rapid urbanization; development strides amid ethnic tensions |

  

---

**III. Geography & Environment ([[economics]])**

• **Location:** Horn of Africa; landlocked since Eritrea’s independence (1993).

• **Topography:** Ethiopian Highlands (“Roof of Africa”), Rift Valley lakes, arid lowlands.

• **Climate:** Varied—temperate highlands (15–25°C), semi‑arid lowlands; prone to droughts and floods.

• **Biodiversity:** Endemic species (gelada baboon, Ethiopian wolf); UNESCO World Heritage sites.

**VI. Political Landscape & Conflict**

• **Ethnic Federalism:** Nine regional states based on ethnicity—aims for representation but fuels separatism.

• **Recent Conflict:** Tigray War (2020–2022); humanitarian crises; ongoing tensions in Oromia and Benishangul-Gumuz.
