**Smedley Butler: Marine, Medal of Honor Recipient, and Critic of War**

  

**Introduction**

  

Smedley Darlington Butler (1881–1940) was one of the most decorated officers in the history of the United States Marine Corps. Renowned for his valor in combat, he later became a prominent critic of U.S. military interventions, famously arguing that war is a racket designed to enrich a few at the expense of many.

  

**Early Life and Military Beginnings**

• **Origins:**

Born in West Chester, Pennsylvania, Butler came from a modest background. He enlisted in the Marine Corps at a young age, quickly rising through the ranks due to his leadership, dedication, and combat prowess.

• **Early Service:**

Butler’s early military career saw him participating in various conflicts, including engagements in the Caribbean and Central America, which provided him with extensive battlefield experience and shaped his understanding of military operations.

  

**Distinguished Military Career**

• **Combat Achievements:**

Butler served in numerous campaigns during his career. He earned the Medal of Honor twice, recognizing his extraordinary bravery and leadership in combat. His tactical acumen and willingness to take on dangerous missions earned him the nickname “Old Gimlet.”

• **Key Engagements:**

Over the course of his service, Butler was involved in conflicts such as the Banana Wars, where he fought in Central America and the Caribbean, and later in more formal military operations as the United States expanded its influence overseas.

  

**Later Life and Criticism of Military Policy**

• **Post-Retirement Activism:**

After retiring from the Marine Corps, Butler became increasingly outspoken about the role of the military in American society. In his later years, he criticized U.S. military interventions and the profit motives behind wars.

• **“War Is a Racket”:**

His 1935 book, _War Is a Racket_, is one of his most enduring contributions. In it, Butler argued that war is orchestrated by a small group of elites for economic gain, often at the expense of the soldiers who fight and the citizens who suffer its consequences. His critique resonated with many who were disillusioned by the costs of military action.

  

**Legacy and Influence**

• **Military and Political Impact:**

Smedley Butler’s career and subsequent activism have made him a significant figure in both military history and the discourse on war and peace. His firsthand experiences provided a rare and critical perspective on U.S. military engagements and the socio-economic dynamics of war.

• **Cultural Icon:**

Today, Butler is remembered not only as a highly decorated Marine Corps officer but also as a courageous whistleblower and a critic of militarism. His writings continue to influence discussions on military ethics and the true costs of war.

  

**Conclusion**

  

Smedley Butler’s life encapsulates the dual legacy of a celebrated military leader and a fierce critic of war. From his distinguished service in the U.S. Marine Corps to his bold denunciation of military profiteering in _War Is a Racket_, Butler remains an enduring symbol of integrity, courage, and the need for accountability in the conduct of war. His contributions continue to inspire debates on the role of the military and the ethical dimensions of conflict in modern society.

This comprehensive overview of **Smedley Butler** provides insights into his military achievements, his later activism, and his lasting impact on discussions about war, ethics, and military policy.
