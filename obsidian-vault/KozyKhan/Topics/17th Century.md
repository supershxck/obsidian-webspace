**17th Century Influential Figures: A Global Outline (100 Figures)**

  

Below is an outline featuring 100 influential people from the 17th century across various regions. Each name is enclosed in double brackets to emphasize their significance during this transformative era.

---

**I. Western Europe (40 Figures)**

1. **[[Galileo Galilei]]**

• Italian astronomer and physicist whose observations transformed our understanding of the cosmos.

2. **[[Johannes Kepler]]**

• German mathematician and astronomer who formulated the laws of planetary motion.

3. **[[René Descartes]]**

• French philosopher and mathematician, often called the father of modern philosophy.

4. **[[Isaac Newton]]**

• English scientist whose work on gravity and motion laid the groundwork for classical mechanics.

5. **[[Francis Bacon]]**

• English philosopher and statesman who advanced the scientific method.

6. **[[Baruch Spinoza]]**

• Dutch philosopher renowned for his rationalist system and ethical writings.

7. **[[Thomas Hobbes]]**

• English political philosopher best known for his work _Leviathan_, which explored the nature of authority.

8. **[[John Locke]]**

• English philosopher whose ideas on empiricism and government influenced modern liberal thought.

9. **[[John Milton]]**

• English poet and intellectual, author of _Paradise Lost_, a cornerstone of English literature.

10. **[[Samuel Pepys]]**

• English naval administrator and diarist whose writings offer a vivid picture of Restoration England.

11. **[[Rembrandt van Rijn]]**

• Dutch master painter celebrated for his portraits, self-portraits, and biblical scenes.

12. **[[Peter Paul Rubens]]**

• Flemish Baroque painter known for his vibrant compositions and dynamic figures.

13. **[[Diego Velázquez]]**

• Spanish court painter whose works captured the realism of 17th‑century life.

14. **[[Molière]]**

• French playwright whose comedies critiqued social norms and human follies.

15. **[[Blaise Pascal]]**

• French mathematician, physicist, and religious philosopher whose _Pensées_ explore faith and reason.

16. **[[Hugo Grotius]]**

• Dutch jurist whose writings on international law laid early foundations for modern legal theory.

17. **[[John Dryden]]**

• English poet and critic influential in shaping Restoration literature.

18. **[[Aphra Behn]]**

• One of the first English women playwrights, whose works helped pave the way for future female authors.

19. **[[Cardinal Richelieu]]**

• French clergyman and statesman whose policies strengthened royal power in France.

20. **[[King Louis XIV of France]]**

• The “Sun King” whose long reign epitomized absolute monarchy and centralized state power.

21. **[[Giambattista Marino]]**

• Italian poet known for his extravagant style, which influenced Baroque literature.

22. **[[John Donne]]**

• English poet and cleric whose metaphysical poetry remains highly influential.

23. **[[Robert Boyle]]**

• Irish natural philosopher whose work on gases led to Boyle’s Law.

24. **[[Antoine Lully]]**

• French composer and conductor who shaped the development of French opera and ballet.

25. **[[Henry Purcell]]**

• English composer celebrated for his contributions to Baroque music.

26. **[[Albrecht von Wallenstein]]**

• Bohemian military leader whose campaigns were central to the Thirty Years’ War.

27. **[[Gian Lorenzo Bernini]]**

• Italian sculptor and architect whose works defined the Baroque style.

28. **[[Caravaggio]]**

• Italian painter known for his dramatic use of light and shadow.

29. **[[Anthony van Dyck]]**

• Flemish Baroque painter celebrated for his elegant portraits.

30. **[[Nicolas Poussin]]**

• French painter whose classical style influenced the development of French art.

31. **[[Samuel Butler]]**

• English poet and satirist, best known for _Hudibras_, a critique of Puritan society.

32. **[[Robert Hooke]]**

• English scientist who made pioneering contributions to biology and physics.

33. **[[Edmund Halley]]**

• English astronomer famous for predicting the orbit of Halley’s Comet.

34. **[[Nicolas Malebranche]]**

• French philosopher whose work integrated rationalism and religious thought.

35. **[[Jean de La Fontaine]]**

• French fabulist whose timeless fables continue to be celebrated.

36. **[[Francis Turretin]]**

• Swiss Reformed theologian whose writings influenced Protestant thought.

37. **[[Richard Baxter]]**

• English Puritan church leader and writer known for his pastoral works.

38. **[[Henry Vaughan]]**

• Welsh metaphysical poet whose works reflect on nature and spirituality.

39. **[[Thomas Browne]]**

• English polymath and writer whose essays bridged science and literature.

40. **[[John Milton]]**

• (Reiterated for emphasis) English poet whose epic works continue to inspire literature.

47. **[[Abbas II of Persia]]**

48. **[[Jahangir]]**

49. **[[Shah Jahan]]**

**III. Eastern Europe (5 Figures)**

56. **[[Ivan IV “the Terrible”]]**

• The first Tsar of Russia, whose centralization of power dramatically transformed Russian governance.

57. **[[Bohdan Khmelnytsky]]**

• Ukrainian Cossack leader who led a significant uprising against Polish rule in 1648.

58. **[[Michael I of Russia]]**

• The first Romanov Tsar, whose election in 1613 established a new dynasty in Russia.

59. **[[Jan III Sobieski]]**

• Polish nobleman and military commander celebrated for his role in the Polish–Lithuanian Commonwealth’s defense.

61. **[[Tokugawa Ieyasu]]**

62. **[[Tokugawa Hidetada]]**

69. **[[Gu Yanwu]]**

70. **[[Wang Yangming]]**

71. **[[Zheng Zhilong]]**

72. **[[Xu Xiake]]**

74. **[[Zhang Xianzhong]]**

77. **[[Feng Menglong]]**

• Chinese writer and playwright whose works captured the vernacular spirit of his time.

78. **[[Tang Xianzu]]**

• Chinese dramatist celebrated for his contributions to classical Chinese theater.

79. **[[Zhang Dai]]**

• Eminent Chinese historian and writer whose memoirs documented the waning days of the Ming Dynasty.

80. **[[Chen Chun]]**

• Influential Chinese painter whose refined style epitomized the aesthetic of late Ming art.

The 17th century was a period of profound transformation, marked by scientific breakthroughs, religious reformation, and geopolitical upheaval.
