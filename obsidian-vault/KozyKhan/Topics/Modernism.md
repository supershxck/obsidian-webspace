**Modernism: Revolution of Form, Thought, and Culture**

---

**Introduction**

  

**Modernism** was a sweeping cultural, artistic, and intellectual movement (circa 1890–1945) that rejected inherited traditions to forge new expressions reflecting a rapidly changing world. It reshaped literature, visual arts, architecture, philosophy, and spirituality — seeking authenticity amid fragmentation.

---

**I. Historical Context & Catalysts**

|**Factor**|**Influence**|
|---|---|
|Industrialization|Urbanization, mechanization, and alienation|
|World Wars|Disillusionment with progress narratives|
|Scientific Advances|Relativity & quantum theories undermining objective truth|
|[[Aestheticism]] & [[Decadence]]|Preceded Modernism’s aesthetic experimentation|

  

---

**II. Core Characteristics**

|**Feature**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|Formal Innovation|Fragmentation, stream of consciousness, abstraction|
|Rejection of Realism|Emphasis on inner experience over external depiction|
|Search for Meaning|Existential questioning, mythic re‑interpretation|
|Interdisciplinary Fusion|Cross-pollination between art, philosophy, psychology|

  

---

**III. Major Movements & Figures**

|**Domain**|**Movement**|**Key Figures**|**Landmark Work**|
|---|---|---|---|
|Literature|Stream of Consciousness|James Joyce, Virginia Woolf|_Ulysses_, _Mrs Dalloway_|
|Visual Arts|Cubism & Abstract|Pablo Picasso, Wassily Kandinsky|_Les Demoiselles d’Avignon_|
|Architecture|International Style|Le Corbusier, Walter Gropius|Villa Savoye, Bauhaus School|
|Music|Atonality|Arnold Schoenberg|_Pierrot Lunaire_|
|Philosophy|Existentialism|Jean-Paul Sartre, Simone de Beauvoir|_Being and Nothingness_|

  

---

**IV. Psychological & Philosophical Dimensions**

|**Dimension**|**Insight**|
|---|---|
|Collective Shadow|Confronts fractured modern psyche; integration of disowned anxieties (Jungian)|
|Existential Freedom|Emphasizes individual choice in absurd world|
|Mythic Renewal|Reworks archetypes for contemporary relevance|

  

---

**V. Esoteric & Metaphysical Context**

• Many Modernists drew on **occult symbolism** (e.g., Kandinsky’s Theosophy) to access deeper layers of consciousness

• Viewed art as vehicle for spiritual transformation beyond materialist reductionism

---

**VI. Impact & Legacy**

|**Influence**|**Example**|
|---|---|
|Contemporary Art|Foundation for postmodern pluralism|
|Architecture & Design|Minimalism, functionalism in built environments|
|Literature & Media|Nonlinear narratives in film, digital storytelling|
|Business Innovation|Agile iteration, design thinking rooted in experimentation|

---

**VIII. Key Vocabulary**

• **Stream of Consciousness:** Narrative technique replicating thought flow

• **Abstraction:** Distillation of form to essential elements

• **International Style:** Architectural ethos of function, simplicity, and universality

• **Existentialism:** Philosophy emphasizing human freedom amid meaninglessness

• **Metanarrative:** Overarching story legitimizing cultural values
