**The Mexican–American War (1846–1848): Conflict, Consequence, and Expansion**

---

**Introduction**

  

The **Mexican–American War** was a pivotal conflict between the United States and Mexico resulting in vast territorial gains for the U.S. Fueled by **Manifest Destiny**, the war reshaped North America’s political map and intensified domestic debates over slavery and national identity.

---

**I. Causes of War**

|**Factor**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|Territorial Ambition|U.S. desire to annex Texas and acquire California and the Southwest|
|Border Dispute|Conflict over Texas boundary: Rio Grande (U.S.) vs Nueces River (Mexico)|
|Manifest Destiny|Ideological belief in U.S. divine right to expand westward|
|Diplomatic Failure|Rejection of U.S. purchase offers; Thornton Affair (April 1846)|

  

---

**II. Major Campaigns & Battles**

|**Theater**|**Key Engagements**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|---|
|Northern Mexico|Battle of Monterrey (Sept 1846); Siege of Veracruz (Mar 1847)|U.S. advances into heartland|
|Central Mexico|Battle of Cerro Gordo (Apr 1847); Battle of Mexico City (Sept 1847)|Capture of capital; collapse of Mexican government|
|California|Bear Flag Revolt (Jun 1846); Battle of San Pasqual|Early U.S. control of Alta California|
|New Mexico|Capture of Santa Fe (Aug 1846)|Bloodless occupation|

  

---

**III. Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo**

|**Signed**|**February 2, 1848**|
|---|---|
|Terms|Mexico ceded ~525,000 sq mi (present-day CA, NV, UT, AZ, NM, parts of CO/WY); U.S. paid $15M; assumed some debts|
|Legacy|U.S. continental expansion completed; intensified slavery debate|

  

---

**IV. Psychological & Philosophical Reflections**

|**Dimension**|**Insight**|
|---|---|
|Collective Shadow|War embodied U.S. projection of manifest destiny—justifying conquest while suppressing moral conflict over displacement of peoples (Jungian)|
|Ethical Paradox|Expansion framed as progress yet enacted through violence and dispossession|

  

---

**V. Consequences & Legacy**

| **Domain**          | **Impact**                                                       |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Territorial         | U.S. grew by ~1/3; set stage for [[California Gold Rush]]        |
| Domestic Politics   | Fueled sectional tensions; contributed to Civil War onset        |
| Mexican Society     | Loss of half its territory; long‑term socio‑economic disruption  |
| Military Innovation | U.S. Army gained experience; emerging professional officer corps |

  

---

**VI. Lessons & Modern Frameworks**

|**Challenge**|**Framework**|**Strategy**|
|---|---|---|
|Territorial Conflict|Multilateral negotiation|Leverage diplomacy; respect sovereignty|
|Historical Reconciliation|Restorative justice|Support cultural preservation and land rights|
|Ethical Expansion|Impact assessment|Prioritize human rights in policy decisions|

  

---

**VII. Key Vocabulary**

• **Manifest Destiny:** Ideology of U.S. territorial expansion

• **Cession:** Formal transfer of territory by treaty

• **Bloodless Occupation:** Military takeover without combat

• **Sectional Tensions:** North‑South conflict over slavery |
