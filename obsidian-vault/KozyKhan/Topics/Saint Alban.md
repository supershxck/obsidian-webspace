Saint Alban was a Roman soldier who was brought to faith in Christ and baptized by a fugitive priest to whom he gave shelter during a persecution in the third century. With all Christian clergy ordered to be arrested, the saint exchanged clothes with the priest, allowing him to escape and ensuring his own martyrdom. Hearing of a priest living in Alban's home, soldiers arrested him and took him before a judge who learned of the deception and threatened Alban with death unless he returned to paganism. Saint Alban refused and glorified God. He was beaten and tortured and taken to his execution. On the way, a bridge was blocked by a crowd who came to witness the saint's martyrdom. Alban prayed, made the Sign of the Cross over the river and it parted. Witnessing this, his executioner threw down his sword, refused to kill the saint and was beheaded along with the saint. Before his death, Saint Alban asked for a drink and a spring gushed forth from the ground and he drank of it.
