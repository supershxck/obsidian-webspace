### History of the Aztec Empire

#### Early Origins and Formation
- **Origins**: The Aztecs, also known as Mexica, were a Mesoamerican people who migrated to the Valley of Mexico in the early 13th century. According to their legends, they originated from Aztlán, a mythical homeland.
- **Founding of Tenochtitlán**: In 1325, the Aztecs established the city of Tenochtitlán on an island in Lake Texcoco, guided by a prophecy involving an eagle perched on a cactus with a snake in its beak.

#### Rise of the Empire
- **Triple Alliance**: In 1428, the Aztecs formed a military alliance with the neighboring city-states of Texcoco and Tlacopan, known as the Triple Alliance. This alliance allowed them to dominate the Valley of Mexico and expand their influence.
- **Expansion**: Under the leadership of rulers like Itzcoatl (1427-1440) and Moctezuma I (1440-1469), the Aztecs embarked on extensive military campaigns, subjugating neighboring regions and demanding tribute from conquered peoples.

#### Peak of the Empire
- **Moctezuma II**: The reign of Moctezuma II (1502-1520) marked the height of the Aztec Empire. The empire expanded to its greatest extent, controlling large parts of central and southern Mexico.
- **Cultural and Architectural Achievements**: Tenochtitlán became a magnificent city with impressive temples, palaces, and causeways. The Templo Mayor, a grand double pyramid, was a central religious site.

#### Society and Economy
- **Agriculture and Economy**: The Aztecs developed advanced agricultural techniques, including chinampas (floating gardens), which increased food production. The economy was based on tribute, trade, and agriculture.
- **Social Structure**: Aztec society was hierarchical, with a ruling nobility, priests, warriors, merchants, artisans, and farmers. The emperor, or tlatoani, was considered semi-divine.

#### Religion and Culture
- **Religion**: The Aztecs practiced a polytheistic religion, worshipping gods like Huitzilopochtli (war and sun god) and Tlaloc (rain god). Human sacrifice was a significant aspect of their religious rituals.
- **Arts and Education**: The Aztecs excelled in arts, poetry, and architecture. They also had a system of education for the nobility and commoners, focusing on military training, religion, and trade skills.

#### Contact with Europeans
- **Arrival of the Spanish**: In 1519, Spanish conquistador Hernán Cortés arrived on the coast of Mexico. He formed alliances with some of the discontented tributary states of the Aztecs.
- **Fall of Tenochtitlán**: In 1521, after a prolonged siege and battles, Tenochtitlán fell to the Spanish forces and their indigenous allies. The fall marked the end of the Aztec Empire.

#### Aftermath and Legacy
- **Colonial Period**: Following the conquest, the Spanish established Mexico City on the ruins of Tenochtitlán. The indigenous population faced dramatic declines due to diseases and harsh colonial policies.
- **Cultural Legacy**: Despite the conquest, Aztec culture and traditions have endured. The Nahuatl language is still spoken, and many aspects of Aztec art, mythology, and customs influence modern Mexican culture.

### Significant Events and High Periods
- **1325**: Founding of Tenochtitlán.
- **1428**: Formation of the Triple Alliance.
- **1440-1469**: Reign of Moctezuma I, significant territorial expansion.
- **1502-1520**: Reign of Moctezuma II, peak of the empire.
- **1519**: Arrival of Hernán Cortés.
- **1521**: Fall of Tenochtitlán and the end of the Aztec Empire.

### Conclusion
The Aztec Empire was a powerful and influential civilization in Mesoamerica, known for its military prowess, architectural achievements, and complex society. Its fall to the Spanish marked a significant turning point in the history of the Americas.
