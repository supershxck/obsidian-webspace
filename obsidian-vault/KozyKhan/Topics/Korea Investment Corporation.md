**Chapter: Korea Investment Corporation — South Korea’s Sovereign Wealth Vehicle**

  

**Introduction**

  

The **Korea Investment Corporation (KIC)** is South Korea’s sovereign wealth fund, established in 2005 to manage a portion of the nation’s foreign exchange reserves. With **approximately USD 165 billion** in assets under management (2025), KIC pursues global diversification, risk‑adjusted returns, and intergenerational wealth preservation.

---

**Origins & Mandate**

• **Founded:** April 2005 by presidential decree

• **Primary Objectives:**

1. Enhance long‑term returns on foreign reserves

2. Diversify Korea’s external assets beyond cash and government bonds

3. Support macroeconomic stability through prudent investment

---

**Governance & Structure**

|**Entity**|**Role**|**Accountability**|
|---|---|---|
|Ministry of Strategy and Finance|Strategic mandate & policy guidance|Reports to National Assembly|
|KIC Board of Directors|Investment policy approval & oversight|Annual performance reviews|
|Chief Executive Officer|Portfolio execution & risk management|Quarterly reporting to Board|
|Audit & Risk Committee|Compliance, risk oversight|Independent internal audits|

  

---

**Investment Strategy & Portfolio**

|**Asset Class**|**Approx. Allocation**|**Representative Investments**|
|---|---|---|
|Public Equities|43%|Global blue‑chip stocks|
|Fixed Income|27%|Sovereign & corporate bonds|
|Private Equity|14%|Buyout & growth funds|
|Real Assets|10%|Infrastructure & real estate|
|Alternatives|6%|Hedge funds, venture capital|

• **Geographic Split:** North America (45%), Europe (30%), Asia‑Pacific (20%), Others (5%)

• **Time Horizon:** 10–15 years focused on total return

• **ESG Integration:** UN PRI signatory; proprietary climate risk analysis

---
