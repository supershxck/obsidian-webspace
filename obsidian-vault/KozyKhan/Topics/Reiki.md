Reiki is a Japanese spiritual healing practice that involves the transfer of universal life energy through the practitioner's hands to promote relaxation, stress reduction, and overall well-being. The word "Reiki" is derived from two Japanese words: "Rei," meaning universal life, and "Ki," meaning energy[1][4].
## Key Aspects of Reiki

**Healing Technique:** Reiki is administered through light touch or by hovering hands over specific areas of the body. Practitioners believe they are channeling life force energy to facilitate healing[2][5].

**Non-Invasive:** It is a gentle, non-invasive method that can be performed with the recipient fully clothed[2][5].

**Holistic Approach:** Reiki aims to treat the whole person, including body, emotions, mind, and spirit[4].

## Reiki Session

A typical Reiki session involves:

- The client lying on a massage table or sitting comfortably
- The practitioner placing hands on or above specific body areas
- Sessions lasting 60 to 90 minutes
- Practitioners holding each position for about 3 minutes or until they sense the energy flow has stopped[2][5]

## Origins and History

Reiki as we know it today was developed by Mikao Usui in Japan in the early 20th century. However, similar healing practices existed before Usui's time[7][10]. The practice gained popularity worldwide largely due to the efforts of Hawayo Takata, who brought Reiki to the United States[7].

## Principles and Techniques

Reiki is based on the idea that an unseen life force energy flows through all living things. Practitioners use various techniques, including:

- Centering
- Clearing
- Beaming
- Extracting harmful energies
- Infusing
- Smoothing and raking the aura[5]

## Applications

Reiki is used for:

- Stress reduction
- Relaxation
- Pain management
- Emotional healing
- Complementing traditional medical treatments[1][4]

While scientific evidence for Reiki's effectiveness is limited, many people report positive experiences and benefits from the practice.
