**Pope Martin V (1368–1431): Restorer of Papal Unity After the Western Schism**

---

**Introduction**

  

Pope Martin V (birth name: Oddone Colonna) served as the 206th pope from November 11, 1417 until his death in 1431. His election at the conclusion of the [[Council of Constance]] ended the nearly 40‑year **Western Schism**, restoring a single, universally recognized pontiff and reestablishing papal authority.

---

**Early Life & Rise**

• **Born:** February 26, 1368 in Rome to the powerful Colonna family

• Educated in canon law; served as cardinal‑deacon under Pope Gregory XII

• Reputation for diplomacy positioned him as consensus candidate at Constance

---

**Election & Ending the Schism**

|**Event**|**Date**|**Significance**|
|---|---|---|
|Council of Constance|1414–1418|Convened to resolve multiple papal claimants|
|Election as Pope|November 11, 1417|Deposed rival claimants; unified Church leadership|

  

---

**Papal Achievements**

|**Area**|**Initiative**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|---|
|Ecclesiastical Unity|Reasserted papal supremacy over conciliarism|Ended institutional division|
|Church Reform|Convened provincial councils; regulated clergy discipline|Began incremental moral renewal|
|Diplomacy & Politics|Negotiated peace between Italian city‑states|Stabilized central Italy and papal territories|
|Patronage|Commissioned rebuilding of St. Peter’s Basilica foundations|Laid groundwork for Renaissance architecture|

**Legacy & Long‑Term Impact**

• **Church Unity:** Papacy regained legitimacy and central control until Reformation

• **Precedent for Reform:** Set stage for Council of Basel (1431) and later conciliar debates

• **Cultural Patronage:** Early support for Renaissance art and architecture in Rome

---

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Western Schism:** Division (1378–1417) with multiple rival popes

• **Conciliarism:** Doctrine favoring councils over papal supremacy

• **Papal Supremacy:** Doctrine of pope’s ultimate authority over Church matters

• **Colonna Family:** Influential Roman noble house
