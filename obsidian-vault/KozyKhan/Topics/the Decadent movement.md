**The Decadent Movement: Artifice, Excess, and the Pursuit of Transgression**

---

**Introduction**

  

The **Decadent Movement** (circa 1880–1910) was a cultural and literary reaction against bourgeois norms, celebrating aesthetic excess, moral ambiguity, and the exploration of taboo. Born from [[Aestheticism]]’s “art for art’s sake,” Decadence intensified its embrace of artifice, sensuality, and the darker aspects of human experience.

---

**Historical Origins & Context**

|**Period**|**Catalyst**|**Significance**|
|---|---|---|
|Late 19th Century|Reaction to Victorian moralism and industrialization|Sought refuge in beauty and individual experience|
|Fin de Siècle Angst|Social anxiety over rapid change and spiritual malaise|Turned inward toward subjective sensation|

  

---

**Core Tenets**

|**Principle**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|Artifice over Nature|Preference for crafted beauty and stylization|
|Transgression|Exploration of taboo (eroticism, morbidity) as creative fuel|
|Pessimism & Ennui|Emphasis on boredom (ennui) as existential condition|
|Decay & Decline|Romanticizing cultural and personal degeneration|

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Ennui:** Profound boredom reflecting existential emptiness

• **Artifice:** Deliberate contrivance prioritizing style over substance

• **Decadence:** Cultural movement celebrating decline and excess

• **Symbolism:** Use of metaphorical imagery to convey inner states
