**William McKinley (1843–1901): The Imperial President and Architect of Modern America**

---

**Introduction**

  

William McKinley was the 25th President of the United States (1897–1901). His administration guided America into the 20th century through economic growth, victory in the [[Spanish‑American War]], and expansion of U.S. influence overseas.

---

**Early Life & Rise**

• **Born:** January 29, 1843 in Niles, Ohio

• **Education:** Allegheny College (left early to join Union Army)

• **Military Service:** Brevet Major in Civil War

• **Political Career:** U.S. Representative (1877–1891); Governor of Ohio (1892–1896)

---

**Presidency (1897–1901)**

|**Issue**|**Action**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|---|
|Economic Policy|Dingley Tariff Act (1897); Gold Standard Act (1900)|Protected U.S. industries; stabilized currency|
|Spanish‑American War|Declared war (1898); naval victory at Manila Bay; Treaty of Paris|Acquired Puerto Rico, Guam, Philippines, Cuba protectorate|
|Open Door Policy|Advocated equal trade rights in China (1899)|Asserted U.S. commercial influence in Asia|
|Progressive Measures|Increased civil service coverage; supported currency reform|Began federal regulatory tradition|

|**Domain**|**Impact**|
|---|---|

|Assassination|Killed Sept 14, 1901 — spurred Theodore Roosevelt’s progressive presidency|

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Dingley Tariff Act (1897):** High protective tariff raising duties

• **Gold Standard Act (1900):** Established gold as sole U.S. currency backing

• **Spanish‑American War:** 1898 conflict marking U.S. entry as imperial power

• **Open Door Policy:** U.S. principle of equal trade access in China
