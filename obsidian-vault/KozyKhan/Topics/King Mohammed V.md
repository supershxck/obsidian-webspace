King Mohammed V of Morocco was a pivotal figure in the country's history, particularly in its struggle for independence from French colonial rule.1 Here's a summary of his life and significance:

- **Key Roles:**
    - He was the last Sultan of Morocco (1927-1953 and 1955-1957) and the first King of Morocco (1957-1961).
- **Independence Movement:**
    - He played a crucial role in the Moroccan independence movement.2 His stance against French colonial rule, especially during his exile, solidified his position as a national hero.
    - His exile by the French in 1953 served to galvanize the Moroccan population, increasing the desire for independence.3
    - He negotiated the treaty that secured moroccan independence from france in 1956.4
- **Protection of Jewish Community:**
    - During World War II, he famously resisted the [[Vichy regime]]'s anti-Jewish laws, protecting Morocco's Jewish population from deportation. This act is a significant part of his legacy.
- **Legacy:**
    - He is remembered as a symbol of Moroccan national unity and independence.5
    - He is highly regarded for his courage and his work to unify the Moroccan people.

In essence, Mohammed V was a key figure in modern Moroccan history, leading his country to independence and demonstrating courage in the face of oppression.6
