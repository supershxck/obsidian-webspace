**William Henry Harrison (1773–1841): America’s Shortest‑Serving President**

---

**Introduction**

  

William Henry Harrison was the ninth President of the United States (March 4–April 4, 1841), serving the shortest tenure in U.S. history—just 31 days before dying of pneumonia. A celebrated military leader and frontier statesman, his presidency underscored questions of executive succession and presidential protocol.

---

**Early Life & Military Career**

| **Detail**      | **Information**                                                                                               |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Born            | February 9, 1773, Charles City County, Virginia                                                               |
| Education       | Studied medicine briefly; pivoted to law                                                                      |
| Military Fame   | Led U.S. forces at Battle of Tippecanoe (1811) against Tecumseh’s confederation—earning nickname “Tippecanoe” |
| [[War of 1812]] | Commanded successful campaigns in the Northwest Territory                                                     |

| **Year**   | **Role**                      | **Significance**                                             |
| ---------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |

| 1814–1816  | Governor of Indiana Territory | Negotiated treaties expanding U.S. frontier                  |

| 1840       | Elected President             | Ran on log cabin campaign slogan: “Tippecanoe and Tyler Too” |

  

---

**Presidency (March 4–April 4, 1841)**

|**Action**|**Notes**|
|---|---|
|Inaugural Address|Delivered longest inaugural speech in cold weather without a hat or coat—likely causing fatal illness|
|No Legislative Record|Died before enacting any policies|

  

---

**Death & Constitutional Impact**

|**Event**|**Outcome**|
|---|---|
|Died April 4, 1841|First president to die in office|
|Succession Precedent|Vice President John Tyler assumed full presidential powers—establishing the “Tyler precedent” for presidential succession**|

  

---

**Legacy & Long‑Term Influence**

|**Aspect**|**Impact**|
|---|---|
|Succession Protocol|Clarified vice presidential succession practice later codified by 25th Amendment|
|Military Reputation|Cemented image as frontier war hero; inspired political campaign style focusing on personal narrative|

  

---

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Tippecanoe:** Nickname derived from Harrison’s victory at the Battle of Tippecanoe (1811)

• **Whig Party:** Political party opposing Jacksonian Democrats, promoting Congressional supremacy

• **Tyler Precedent:** Unwritten rule affirming vice president becomes president (not merely acting) upon death |
