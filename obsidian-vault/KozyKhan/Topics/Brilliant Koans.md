Here’s a list of **brilliant koans**—Zen riddles or paradoxical statements that challenge the mind to move beyond logic and grasp deeper truths through intuition and direct experience.

**Classic Koans**

1. **Joshu’s Mu (The Sound of One Hand Clapping)**

• A monk asked Master Joshu, _“Does a dog have Buddha-nature?”_

• Joshu replied, _“Mu!”_ (A negation beyond yes or no)

• (What does this mean beyond simple affirmation or denial?)

2. **What Is Your Original Face?**

• _“What was your original face before your parents were born?”_

• (A meditation on identity before birth, beyond form.)

1. **Nansen Cuts the Cat**

• Monks were arguing over a cat. Master Nansen held up the cat and said, _“If any of you can say a true word, I will not cut it.”_

• No one spoke, so Nansen cut the cat in two.

• Later, when Joshu heard this, he put his sandals on his head and walked away.

• (What does Joshu’s action mean?)

2. **Shuzan’s Short Staff**

• Master Shuzan held out his staff and said, _“If you call this a staff, you are clinging to a name. If you do not call it a staff, you deny its reality. So what do you call it?”_

• (How do you respond beyond language and logic?)

3. **Hyakujo’s Fox**

• A monk asked, _“Does an enlightened person fall under the law of cause and effect?”_

• Hyakujo answered, _“He does not.”_

• The monk was reborn as a fox for 500 lifetimes.

• (What does this mean about karma and enlightenment?)

**Mystical & Playful Koans**

4. **The Empty Cup**

• A professor went to see a Zen master and began to talk about Zen. The master poured tea until the cup overflowed.

• The professor said, _“It is full! No more will go in!”_

• The master said, _“Like this cup, you are full of opinions. How can I teach you Zen unless you first empty your cup?”_

• (How do you let go of preconceptions?)

5. **The Sound of One Hand**

• A student asked Master Hakuin, _“What is the sound of one hand clapping?”_

• (How do you hear the unheard?)

6. **Seijo and Her Soul Are Separated**

• A young girl, Seijo, loved a boy. One day she left home and married him. Years later, she returned and her parents were shocked—they thought she had never left.

• They found another girl, identical to Seijo, asleep in her room.

• _Which is the real Seijo?_

• (What does this say about identity and duality?)

7. **A Buffalo Passes Through a Window**

• _“A buffalo passes through a window. His head, horns, and front legs pass through. Why can’t his tail pass through?”_

• (What does the tail symbolize?)

8. **Lifting a Finger**

  

• Whenever a master was asked a question about Zen, he simply lifted one finger.

• One day, his student did the same when asked about Zen. The master cut off his finger.

• (What is beyond imitation?)

**More Paradoxical Koans**

9. **Does a Flower Wither and Fall?**

  

• _“If you say the flower withers and falls, you miss the truth. If you say the flower does not wither and fall, you miss the truth.”_

• (How do you see beyond duality?)

  

10. **Three Pounds of Flax**

  

• A monk asked, _“What is Buddha?”_

• The master replied, _“Three pounds of flax.”_

• (How does this answer transcend the question?)

  

11. **Stop the Sound of the Distant Temple Bell**

  

• _“Stop the sound of that bell.”_

• (How do you stop something that exists only in perception?)

  

12. **When You Meet the Buddha, Kill Him**

  

• _“If you meet the Buddha on the road, kill him.”_

• (What does this mean about attachment to ideas and authority?)

  

13. **Does the Mountain Move?**

  

• A monk asked, _“Does the mountain move?”_

• The master replied, _“If you do not see it move, how do you know?”_

• (What does this suggest about perception and reality?)

**Koans for Deep Reflection**

14. **The Moon Cannot Be Stolen**

  

• A thief broke into a monk’s hut but found nothing to steal.

• The monk handed him his robe and said, _“You came a long way. Take this gift.”_

• The thief left, confused. The monk looked at the night sky and said, _“Poor fellow, I wish I could give him the moon.”_

• (What is the true wealth that cannot be stolen?)

  

15. **Buddha Holds Up a Flower**

  

• During a sermon, the Buddha held up a flower without speaking.

• Only Mahākāśyapa smiled.

• The Buddha said, _“I have the true teaching, and I give it to Mahākāśyapa.”_

• (What was transmitted in silence?)

  

16. **What Is Enlightenment?**

  

• A student asked, _“What is enlightenment?”_

• The master kicked over a water jug and walked away.

• (What was he showing?)

  

17. **The Wooden Goose Inside the Bottle**

  

• _“How do you remove a goose from a bottle without breaking the bottle or harming the goose?”_

• (How does the mind create prisons, and how do we free it?)

  

18. **Why Did Bodhidharma Come to China?**

  

• When asked why he came to China, Bodhidharma replied, _“A cypress tree in the courtyard.”_

• (What does this reveal about expectations?)

**Conclusion**

  

Each koan is an invitation to break free from conditioned thought and **experience reality directly**. They are **not puzzles to solve with logic**, but mirrors reflecting the limits of our rational mind. The best way to approach them is to sit with them, let them work on you, and allow sudden flashes of insight to emerge.
