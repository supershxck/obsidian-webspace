### History of the Chinese Empire and People

#### Prehistoric Era and Early Dynasties (ca. 5000 BCE - 221 BCE)
- **Neolithic Cultures (ca. 5000-2000 BCE)**
  - **Yangshao Culture (ca. 5000-3000 BCE)**: Known for painted pottery and early agriculture.
  - **Longshan Culture (ca. 3000-2000 BCE)**: Advanced pottery and early urban settlements.

- **Xia Dynasty (ca. 2070-1600 BCE)**
  - Considered the first dynasty in Chinese history, though its existence is debated.
  - Associated with the Erlitou culture, which shows early signs of state formation and bronze metallurgy.

- **Shang Dynasty (ca. 1600-1046 BCE)**
  - First historically verifiable dynasty.
  - Known for bronze work, oracle bone inscriptions, and a complex social structure.

- **Zhou Dynasty (ca. 1046-256 BCE)**
  - **Western Zhou (ca. 1046-771 BCE)**: Established the Mandate of Heaven concept.
  - **Eastern Zhou (ca. 770-256 BCE)**: Period of political fragmentation, divided into the Spring and Autumn period and the Warring States period.

#### Imperial China (221 BCE - 1912 CE)
- **Qin Dynasty (221-206 BCE)**
  - First unified Chinese empire established by Qin Shi Huang.
  - Standardized weights, measures, and writing systems. Built the first version of the Great Wall.

- **Han Dynasty (206 BCE - 220 CE)**
  - Consolidated the foundations of imperial China.
  - Significant advances in science, technology, and culture. Expansion of the Silk Road.

- **Three Kingdoms Period (220-280 CE)**
  - Division of China into three rival kingdoms: Wei, Shu, and Wu.
  - Period of military conflict and political fragmentation.

- **Jin Dynasty (265-420 CE)**
  - Brief reunification under the Western Jin, followed by division during the Eastern Jin.
  - Marked by internal strife and invasions by non-Han Chinese tribes.

- **Southern and Northern Dynasties (420-589 CE)**
  - Division between northern dynasties ruled by non-Han Chinese and southern dynasties ruled by Han Chinese.
  - Period of cultural and religious flourishing, especially Buddhism.

- **Sui Dynasty (581-618 CE)**
  - Reunified China after centuries of division.
  - Built the Grand Canal and initiated land reforms.

- **Tang Dynasty (618-907 CE)**
  - Golden age of Chinese civilization, marked by cultural, technological, and economic prosperity.
  - Expansion into Central Asia and flourishing of poetry and arts.

- **Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms (907-960 CE)**
  - Period of political fragmentation following the fall of the Tang Dynasty.

- **Song Dynasty (960-1279 CE)**
  - Known for economic prosperity, technological innovation, and cultural achievements.
  - Faced constant military pressure from northern nomadic tribes.

- **Yuan Dynasty (1271-1368 CE)**
  - Established by Kublai Khan, a grandson of Genghis Khan.
  - Mongol rule over China, integration of Chinese and Mongol cultures.

- **Ming Dynasty (1368-1644 CE)**
  - Period of restoration and expansion.
  - Known for maritime explorations, the construction of the Forbidden City, and the Great Wall.

- **Qing Dynasty (1644-1912 CE)**
  - Last imperial dynasty, established by the Manchu.
  - Expanded China's territory to its greatest extent, faced internal rebellions and foreign invasions.

#### Modern China (1912-Present)
- **Republic of China (1912-1949)**
  - Established after the fall of the Qing Dynasty.
  - Period of political instability, warlordism, and civil war between Nationalists and Communists.

- **People's Republic of China (1949-Present)**
  - Established by the Communist Party under Mao Zedong.
  - Period of socialist transformation, economic reforms under Deng Xiaoping, and rapid modernization.

### Summary
The history of China spans thousands of years, beginning with early Neolithic cultures like the [[Yangshao Culture]] and [[Longshan Culture]]. The first dynastic rule began with the legendary [[Xia Dynasty]], followed by the historically verified [[Shang Dynasty]]. The [[Zhou Dynasty]] introduced the [[Mandate of Heaven]] concept, leading to a feudal system and eventual fragmentation.

Imperial China began with the [[Qin Dynasty]] under [[Qin Shi Huang]], who unified China. The [[Han Dynasty]] saw the expansion of the [[Silk Road]] and significant cultural developments. Following periods of division and reunification, the [[Sui Dynasty]] and [[Tang Dynasty]] ushered in prosperity and cultural flourishing.

The [[Song Dynasty]] is noted for economic and technological advances, while the [[Yuan Dynasty]] under [[Kublai Khan]] brought Mongol rule. The [[Ming Dynasty]] restored Han Chinese rule and expanded China's influence. The [[Qing Dynasty]], the last imperial dynasty, expanded China's territory but faced internal and external challenges.

Modern China began with the [[Republic of China]], followed by the establishment of the [[People's Republic of China]] under [[Mao Zedong]]. Economic reforms under [[Deng Xiaoping]] led to rapid modernization and China's current global influence.
