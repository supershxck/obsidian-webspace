**Belisarius: The Last Great General of Rome**

  

**Flavius Belisarius (c. 500–565 AD)** was one of the greatest generals of **the [[Byzantine Empire]]**, serving under **Emperor Justinian I (r. 527–565 AD)**. He is famous for his **brilliant military campaigns** that temporarily **restored much of the Western Roman Empire**, including **North Africa, Italy, and Spain**. Often called **“The Last Roman General,”** he combined strategic genius with **loyalty, adaptability, and an understanding of both warfare and politics**.

**1. Early Life and Rise to Power**

  

Belisarius was born around **500 AD** in **Thrace or Illyria** (modern Bulgaria, Greece, or Albania).

• He entered the **Eastern Roman (Byzantine) army** and rose quickly through the ranks due to his **tactical brilliance and leadership**.

• He became a close ally of **Justinian I**, who recognized his potential and gave him **supreme command of the Byzantine forces**.

**2. The Persian War (527–532 AD)**

  

Before his famous **Western conquests**, Belisarius proved himself **against the Sassanid Persians**.

• **Battle of Dara (530 AD)** – His **innovative tactics** led to a stunning **Roman victory** over a much larger Persian force.

• **Battle of Callinicum (531 AD)** – Despite a defeat, he held the line and **protected the Eastern Roman frontier**.

  

These campaigns established him as **the empire’s top general**.

**3. The Nika Revolt (532 AD): Saving Justinian’s Throne**

  

In **532 AD**, a major **riot broke out in Constantinople**, nearly overthrowing **Justinian I**.

• The **Nika Revolt**, led by angry factions in the **Hippodrome**, turned into an all-out **rebellion**.

• Justinian considered **fleeing the city**, but his wife **Theodora** insisted he stay and fight.

• **Belisarius and General Mundus** led the **imperial troops into the Hippodrome**, **slaughtering over 30,000 rebels**.

• This victory **saved Justinian’s reign** and reinforced Belisarius’s status as the empire’s most **trusted general**.

**4. The Vandal War (533–534 AD): Reconquering North Africa**

  

Justinian wanted to **restore the Western Roman Empire**, and **North Africa** (controlled by the **Vandals**) was his first target.

  

**The Campaign Against the Vandals**

• In **533 AD**, Belisarius **launched an invasion of North Africa** with **15,000 men**.

• **Battle of Ad Decimum (533 AD)** – Belisarius defeated **King Gelimer** near Carthage.

• **Battle of Tricamarum (534 AD)** – The Vandals suffered a final defeat.

• The **Byzantines recaptured Carthage**, restoring **Roman rule to North Africa**.

  

This was **one of the fastest and most successful military campaigns in history**.

**5. The Gothic War (535–554 AD): The Reconquest of Italy**

  

After his victory in Africa, Justinian sent Belisarius to **Italy**, ruled by the **Ostrogoths**.

  

**Early Victories (535–540 AD)**

• **Captured Sicily (535 AD) and Naples (536 AD).**

• Entered **Rome in 536 AD**, **defending it against a Gothic siege** (537–538 AD).

• **Captured Ravenna (540 AD)**, taking **King Witiges prisoner**.

  

**Justinian’s Betrayal**

• Justinian, fearing Belisarius’s power, **recalled him to Constantinople in 540 AD**.

• Without Belisarius, the war **dragged on for another decade**, and Italy **was devastated**.

**6. Later Years: Fall and Redemption**

  

**Persian War (541–544 AD)**

• Belisarius was sent to fight the **Sassanids** again but received **few reinforcements**.

• He managed to hold the **eastern frontier** but was again **recalled by Justinian**.

  

**Final Italian Campaign (544–548 AD)**

• The Goths had retaken much of Italy under **King Totila**.

• Belisarius was **sent back**, but Justinian **refused to give him enough troops**.

• After **years of defensive warfare**, he was **recalled in disgrace**.

  

**The False Treason and House Arrest (562 AD)**

• In **562 AD**, Belisarius was **accused of conspiracy** against Justinian.

• He was **briefly imprisoned** but later **restored to honor**.

• He retired and died **in 565 AD**, just months before Justinian.

**7. Belisarius’s Legacy**

• **One of the greatest generals in history**.

• Successfully **reconquered vast Roman territories**.

• **Loyal to Justinian despite betrayals**.

• His campaigns **inspired later military leaders**, including **Napoleon**.
