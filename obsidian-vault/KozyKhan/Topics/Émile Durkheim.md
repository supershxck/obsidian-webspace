**Émile Durkheim (1858–1917): Founder of Modern Sociology**

---

**Introduction**

  

Émile Durkheim was a pioneering French sociologist whose rigorous study of social facts, institutions, and collective consciousness established sociology as an empirical, scientific discipline. His insights into social cohesion, anomie, and the functions of religion remain foundational to understanding modern societies.

---

**Early Life & Academic Context**

• **Born:** April 15, 1858 in Épinal, France

• Educated at École Normale Supérieure; influenced by positivism and German sociology

• First professor of sociology at the University of Bordeaux (1896) and later at the Sorbonne

---

**Core Contributions & Key Works**

|**Concept**|**Definition**|**Major Work**|**Year**|
|---|---|---|---|
|Social Fact|External norms and structures shaping individual behavior|_The Rules of Sociological Method_|1895|
|Division of Labour|Evolution from mechanical to organic solidarity|_The Division of Labour in Society_|1893|
|Anomie|Normlessness arising from rapid social change|_Suicide_|1897|
|Collective Conscience|Shared beliefs binding society|_The Elementary Forms of Religious Life_|1912|

**Sociological Framework**

  

**Solidarity Types**

• **Mechanical Solidarity:** Social cohesion via similarity in traditional societies

• **Organic Solidarity:** Interdependence in complex, modern societies

  

**Suicide Typology**

|**Type**|**Cause**|**Example**|
|---|---|---|
|Egoistic|Weak social integration|Isolated individuals|
|Altruistic|Excessive integration|Cult martyrdom|
|Anomic|Sudden norm disruption|Economic collapse|
|Fatalistic|Overregulation|Oppressive regimes|

**Key Vocabulary**

• **Social Fact:** Patterns external to individuals exerting coercive power

• **Anomie:** Breakdown of normative guidance in society

• **Collective Conscience:** Common moral framework binding a community

• **Solidarity:** Degree of social integration
