The **Grand Mufti of Jerusalem** is the highest Sunni Muslim cleric responsible for overseeing Islamic holy sites in Jerusalem, including the Al-Aqsa Mosque. The position was established by the British authorities in 1918 to centralize Islamic religious leadership in the region. 

  

Here is a list of individuals who have held the title of Grand Mufti of Jerusalem:

1. **Kamil al-Husayni** (1918–1921): Appointed as the first Grand Mufti under British rule, serving until his death in 1921.

2. **[[Hajj Amin al-Husseini]]** (1921–1948): Appointed in 1921, he was a prominent Arab nationalist and played a significant role in opposing Zionist movements. His tenure was marked by political activism and controversy, including alliances during World War II. 

3. **Hussam ad-Din Jarallah** (1948–1954): Appointed by King Abdullah I of Jordan after the 1948 Arab-Israeli War and the subsequent Jordanian control of East Jerusalem.

4. **Saad al-Alami** (1953–1993): Served as Grand Mufti during a period of significant political changes, including the 1967 Six-Day War and the Israeli occupation of East Jerusalem.

5. **Sulaiman Ja’abari** (1993–1994): Appointed during the early years of the Palestinian Authority, his tenure was brief due to his death in 1994.

6. **Ekrima Sa’id Sabri** (1994–2006): Appointed by Palestinian leader Yasser Arafat, he was known for his outspoken political views. He was removed from office by President Mahmoud Abbas in 2006 due to concerns about his political involvement.

7. **Muhammad Ahmad Hussein** (2006–present): Appointed by President Mahmoud Abbas, he currently serves as the Grand Mufti. His tenure has involved addressing various religious and political issues concerning Jerusalem and the Palestinian territories.

  

_Note: The tenure dates and historical contexts are based on available records and may vary slightly depending on different historical accounts._
