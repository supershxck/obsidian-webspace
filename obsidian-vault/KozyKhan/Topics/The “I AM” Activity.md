**The “I AM” Activity**

  

**Introduction**

  

The **“I AM” Activity** is a spiritual movement founded by [[Guy Ballard]] in the 1930s, centered around the teachings and guidance of ascended masters, particularly Saint Germain. The primary focus of the movement is to help individuals connect with their divine essence, often referred to as the “I AM Presence.” This presence is believed to be the divine spark within each person, which, when awakened and understood, leads to spiritual enlightenment, healing, and personal transformation.

  

The **“I AM” Activity** is most known for its practice of using powerful decrees, affirmations, and invocations to invoke the energy of the divine and accelerate one’s spiritual growth.

**1. Origins of the “I AM” Activity**

  

**1.1 Guy Ballard and His Encounters with Ascended Masters**

  

The “I AM” Activity traces its origins back to the experiences of Guy Ballard, an American engineer and businessman, in the early 1930s. According to Ballard’s account, he had direct encounters with Saint Germain, the ascended master, who imparted profound teachings to him. These teachings formed the foundation of the movement.

  

**Key Events:**

• **Mount Shasta Encounter**

Ballard claimed that during a trip to Mount Shasta in California, he met the ascended master Saint Germain. In this encounter, Saint Germain revealed to him the power of the “I AM” presence and how individuals can tap into this divine energy to transform their lives.

• **The Violet Flame and Spiritual Ascension**

Saint Germain also introduced Ballard to the concept of the [[violet flame]], a transformative spiritual energy that could purify negative emotions, thoughts, and karma, leading to accelerated spiritual ascension.

  

**1.2 The Founding of the “I AM” Activity**

  

Ballard formalized his teachings into the “I AM” Activity, beginning with public lectures and later writing books about his experiences and the lessons he had received. His teachings quickly gained followers, and the movement began to spread in the United States, with the aim of awakening individuals to their divine potential and fostering spiritual enlightenment.

**2. Core Teachings of the “I AM” Activity**

  

**2.1 The “I AM Presence” and Its Importance**

  

At the heart of the “I AM” Activity is the concept of the **“I AM Presence”**, which is the divine essence within every individual. This Presence is understood to be a direct connection to the divine, the higher self, and the source of all creation.

• **Affirmation of the “I AM”**

The “I AM” affirmation is central to the movement. By repeating statements such as “I AM divine light,” “I AM healing,” or “I AM abundance,” practitioners invoke the divine presence within themselves, connecting more deeply with their spiritual essence and raising their vibration.

• **Manifestation of Divinity**

The “I AM Presence” is not just a spiritual concept, but a practical tool for manifestation. The belief is that by consciously invoking the “I AM,” individuals can manifest positive outcomes in their lives—whether it’s healing, abundance, love, or wisdom. The act of affirming “I AM” is believed to align the practitioner’s consciousness with their divine nature, allowing them to create their reality.

  

**2.2 The Role of Decrees and Affirmations**

  

A distinctive feature of the “I AM” Activity is the use of **decrees** and **affirmations**, which are prayer-like statements repeated with intention and focus. These are seen as powerful tools to call forth divine energy and spiritual transformation.

• **Decrees for Transformation**

Practitioners are encouraged to recite specific decrees, often related to healing, protection, prosperity, or spiritual ascension. The energy and intention behind these decrees are believed to trigger positive changes in the practitioner’s life.

• **The Violet Flame**

One of the most significant decrees is the invocation of the **violet flame**, which is thought to purify and transmute negative energy, karmic burdens, and emotional blockages. Reciting decrees connected to the violet flame is believed to accelerate personal healing and spiritual growth.

  

**2.3 Ascended Masters as Guides**

  

The “I AM” Activity teaches that there are a number of **ascended masters**, including Saint Germain, Jesus Christ, Buddha, and others, who act as spiritual guides for humanity. These beings, having transcended physical existence, are believed to help individuals on their spiritual journey by offering wisdom, guidance, and energetic support.

• **Connection with Ascended Masters**

Through decrees, prayers, and meditations, practitioners can call upon the guidance of these masters, receiving insights, healing, and protection in their daily lives.

**3. The “I AM” Activity and Spiritual Practices**

  

**3.1 Invocation and Prayer**

  

The primary spiritual practices of the “I AM” Activity involve invoking the divine presence through prayer and decrees. These invocations focus on specific areas of life—healing, abundance, protection, and spiritual mastery.

• **Daily Practices**

Practitioners are encouraged to dedicate time daily to recite these prayers and affirmations, calling upon the energy of the “I AM Presence” and the ascended masters to guide them. This daily discipline is seen as essential to accelerating spiritual evolution and personal transformation.

  

**3.2 Healing and Purification**

  

A significant aspect of the “I AM” Activity is the idea of **spiritual healing**. The movement teaches that many of the challenges individuals face—whether physical, emotional, or mental—are the result of past karma or negative thought patterns. By invoking the “I AM Presence,” and using the violet flame, practitioners can purify and heal these issues, ultimately aligning with their highest spiritual potential.

**4. The Legacy and Influence of the “I AM” Activity**

  

**4.1 Expansion of the Movement**

  

Following its founding in the 1930s, the “I AM” Activity grew in popularity, particularly among those seeking personal empowerment and spiritual growth. It laid the groundwork for other New Age spiritual movements that focus on self-empowerment, divine connection, and the transformative power of positive affirmations.

• **Books and Publications**

Guy Ballard authored several books detailing the principles of the “I AM” Activity, which remain foundational texts for followers. These books continue to be distributed and read by spiritual seekers today.

• **The Saint Germain Foundation**

The Saint Germain Foundation, established by Guy Ballard’s wife, Edna Ballard, continues to promote the teachings of the “I AM” Activity. The foundation is dedicated to preserving and spreading these teachings through lectures, workshops, and publications.

  

**4.2 Influence on New Age and Ascended Master Teachings**

  

The “I AM” Activity has been a significant influence on the broader **New Age** movement, especially in the area of spiritual self-realization and ascended master teachings. Many modern New Age practitioners and spiritual groups still draw on the concepts introduced by the “I AM” Activity, such as the invocation of the “I AM Presence,” the use of decrees, and the practice of connecting with ascended masters.

• **Connection to Other Teachings**

The teachings of the “I AM” Activity share common ground with other metaphysical and esoteric traditions, such as those found in **Theosophy** and the **Ascended Master Teachings**. These traditions emphasize the importance of personal spiritual growth, connection to higher beings, and the manifestation of divine attributes in everyday life.

**5. Criticism and Controversy**

  

**5.1 Commercialization of the Teachings**

  

As with many spiritual movements, there has been criticism of the commercialization of the “I AM” Activity. Some critics argue that the movement’s focus on selling books, workshops, and other materials detracts from the authenticity of the teachings and undermines the spiritual purity that is supposed to be at the core of the practice.

  

**5.2 Lack of Verification of Claims**

  

Like many esoteric and New Age traditions, the “I AM” Activity is sometimes criticized for its unverifiable claims, particularly those related to Guy Ballard’s mystical experiences and encounters with ascended masters. Skeptics often point out that these claims lack independent verification, and question the authenticity of the channeling process.

**Conclusion**

  

The **“I AM” Activity** offers a unique spiritual approach to personal empowerment, healing, and transformation, with a focus on the invocation of divine presence through affirmations, decrees, and connection to ascended masters. It emphasizes the idea that each individual possesses a divine essence, and by invoking this essence, they can manifest spiritual and material changes in their lives. While controversial and critiqued for its commercial aspects, the movement continues to inspire those who seek deeper connection with their divine potential and accelerated spiritual growth.
