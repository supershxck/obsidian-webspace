### Akathist Hymn to Saint Xenia of St. Petersburg

**Kontakion 1** 
> O chosen wanderer and most glorious fool-for-Christ, who desired above all to receive insult and abuse for Thy Bridegroom, Christ, and to serve Him unceasingly with love and humility: grant us who honor thee with joy and love to glorify thee and cry unto thee: 
> Rejoice, O blessed Xenia, faithful intercessor for our souls!
 
  **Ikos 1** 
> In the holy city of St. Petersburg, the Lord chose thee to live as a fool-for-Christ. Thy husband’s untimely death turned thee to a life of holiness, and thou didst leave all earthly comforts to follow Christ, taking up the cross of foolishness and bearing it with love and humility. We, therefore, sing praises to thee: Rejoice, for thou didst forsake all for the love of Christ! Rejoice, for thou didst choose the path of self-denial! Rejoice, for thou didst follow the Savior with joy and humility! Rejoice, for thy life was a living Gospel! Rejoice, O blessed Xenia, who ever intercedeth for our souls!
 
 **Kontakion 2** 
> Seeing the vanity of worldly life, O blessed Xenia, thou didst give all thy possessions to the poor, embracing poverty and wandering the streets in the guise of thy departed husband. In the eyes of men, thou wast a fool, but in the eyes of God, thou art greatly glorified. Therefore, with love and veneration we cry unto thee: Alleluia!

 **Ikos 2** 
> Thou didst live in the fields and alleys of the city, enduring hunger, thirst, and cold for the sake of Christ. Thy life was hidden with Christ in God, but He revealed thee to be a bright light of piety. For this reason, we cry out to thee: Rejoice, for thou didst endure scorn and ridicule for Christ’s sake! Rejoice, for thou didst clothe thyself in the rags of humility! Rejoice, for thou didst bear patiently the trials of poverty and want! Rejoice, for thou didst love God above all earthly comforts! Rejoice, O blessed Xenia, who ever intercedeth for our souls!
 
 **Kontakion 3** 
> By the mercy of God, thou wast filled with the grace of the Holy Spirit, which led thee to the heights of divine love. Thy prayers and intercessions obtained healing for the sick and comfort for the sorrowful. Therefore, with gratitude and reverence, we cry unto thee: Alleluia!

 **Ikos 3** 
> Through many trials and tribulations, thou didst keep thy faith unshaken, O blessed Xenia. Thou didst live in the world but was not of the world, and thy soul rejoiced in the Lord. Therefore, we offer thee these hymns of praise: Rejoice, for thou didst renounce the vanities of the world! Rejoice, for thy heart was fixed on the Kingdom of Heaven! Rejoice, for thou didst dwell in the house of the Lord all the days of thy life! Rejoice, for thy life was a fragrant offering to God! Rejoice, O blessed Xenia, who ever intercedeth for our souls!> 
### Concluding Prayer
> O holy and righteous Mother Xenia, who lived under the protection of the Most High and was strengthened by the Mother of God, and who endured hunger and thirst, cold and heat, insult and reproach, for the sake of your deep faith, hope, and love for God: we beseech you, be our constant intercessor before the Holy Trinity, and grant us by your prayers all that is beneficial for our souls and bodies, but especially help us to obtain the forgiveness of our sins. Protect our homes and families, keep us from all misfortunes and sorrows, and inspire us to repentance, humility, and the love of Christ. That being preserved by your prayers, we may obtain eternal salvation and may praise and glorify God, together with you, unto the ages of ages. Amen.
