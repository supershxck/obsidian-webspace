This quote is from [[Marion Woodman]], a Jungian analyst, who often explored the integration of the feminine and masculine aspects of the psyche. The **"pregnant virgin"** represents a powerful metaphor in Jungian depth psychology. Let’s break it down:

### 1. **The Pregnant Virgin as a Symbol**
   - The "virgin" aspect represents **purity, wholeness, and potential**—a part of the psyche untouched and untainted by external influences or societal conditioning.
   - The "pregnant" aspect represents **fertility, creativity, and transformation**, suggesting that this untouched part of the self contains immense potential for growth and new life.

### 2. **The Outcast Part of the Psyche**
   - Woodman refers to this aspect of the self as being "outcast." This aligns with Jung's concept of the **Shadow**, the parts of ourselves we deny, reject, or neglect.
   - This "pregnant virgin" lies within the darkness of the unconscious, representing latent creativity and insight that can only be accessed through introspection and facing one's inner darkness.

### 3. **Going Into Darkness**
   - Mining "our leaden darkness" refers to the **alchemical process** Jung spoke about, where the "lead" (base aspects of the self) is transformed into "gold" (enlightenment or integration).
   - This involves confronting difficult truths, wounds, and repressed emotions. Only by embracing this darkness can we transform it into something valuable—symbolized by bringing out "silver."

### 4. **Bringing Consciousness to the Unconscious**
   - By becoming aware of this hidden, creative, and transformative part of ourselves, we unlock new dimensions of our personality. The silver we "bring out" is the **wisdom, creativity, and personal growth** that emerges from integrating the outcast parts of ourselves.

### Practical Implications:
This metaphor invites individuals to:
- **Explore the unconscious**: Reflect on the parts of yourself you’ve ignored or denied.
- **Accept duality**: Recognize that creativity and transformation arise from the interplay of light and dark within.
- **Birth new possibilities**: Tap into your inner potential and bring forth something wholly authentic to you.

This aligns deeply with Jungian principles of individuation, where the journey involves integrating all aspects of the self for wholeness.
