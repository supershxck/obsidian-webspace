Chapter: Korea Investment Corporation — South Korea’s Sovereign Wealth Vehicle

Introduction

The Korea Investment Corporation (KIC) is South Korea’s sovereign wealth fund, established in 2005 to manage a portion of the nation’s foreign exchange reserves. With approximately USD 165 billion in assets under management (2025), KIC pursues global diversification, risk‑adjusted returns, and intergenerational wealth preservation.

Origins & Mandate

• Founded: April 2005 by presidential decree

• Primary Objectives:

  1. Enhance long‑term returns on foreign reserves

  2. Diversify Korea’s external assets beyond cash and government bonds

  3. Support macroeconomic stability through prudent investment

Governance & Structure

• Time Horizon: 10–15 years focused on total return

• ESG Integration: UN PRI signatory; proprietary climate risk analysis
