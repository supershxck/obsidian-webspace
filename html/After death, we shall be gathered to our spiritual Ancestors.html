6 “Do not dishonor a man in his old age, For some of us are growing old as well. 7 Do not rejoice over any dead person; Remember that we all must die.” Sirach 8:6-7 OSB

  • The expression “gathered to his kin” is found only 10x in the OT & only in the Pentateuch 
  • Gen. 25:8, 17, 35:29, 49:29, 33, Num. 20:24, 26, 27:13, 31:2, Deut. 32:50
  • The similar phrases “(gathered to) or (buried with) his fathers” also appear 
  • Gen. 15:15, 47:30; Judg. 2:10

To be gathered to one’s fathers does not mean to be entombed in the grave, but rather it implies  Though the Eastern Church disagrees with the Augustinian idea that babies go to hell because they weren’t circumcised, yet Augustine notes on the passage in Gen. 25:17: 

“It was not of any temporal death that this fear was inflicted, since of righteous persons, when they died, it used rather to be said, “And he was gathered unto his people;”[Genesis 25:17] or, “He was gathered to his fathers:” for no attempt to separate a man from his people is long formidable to him, when his own people is itself the people of God.”

For the Christian, when the days of our life reach its end and our souls leave our bodies to enter into that heavenly abode of bliss and perfection, we go also to join our Patriarchs and Fathers on Mount Zion in the divine life (Ps. 125:1, Heb. 12).

“That one is gathered to one’s kin/fathers before being buried implies either a belief in a continued existence in the netherworld or that the spirit of the deceased joined the ancestors in the underworld, and that even in death family solidarity was not broken.” —Hamilton, NICOT p. 168

“This phrase means that the deceased joins his ancestors in the realm of the dead; the expression of may be associated with the practice of family burial.” —Matthew’s, NAC p. 356
