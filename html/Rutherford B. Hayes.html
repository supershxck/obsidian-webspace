Rutherford B. Hayes (1822–1893): Reconciler of Reconstruction and Advocate of Civil Service Reform

Introduction

Rutherford Birchard Hayes was the 19th President of the United States (1877–1881). His administration ended Reconstruction, restored civilian rule in the South, and initiated key civil service reforms aimed at curbing patronage and restoring public trust in government.

Early Life & Career

• Born: October 4, 1822 in Delaware, Ohio

• Education: Harvard College (BA 1842), Harvard Law School (1845)

• Civil War Service: Brigadier General in Union Army; wounded at South Mountain and Cedar Creek

• Political Rise: Ohio Governor (1868–1872; 1876–1877); U.S. Representative (1865–1867)

Presidency (1877–1881)

|Issue|Action|Outcome| |---|---|---| |End of Reconstruction|Withdrew federal troops from Southern states as part of Compromise of 1877|Marked close of Reconstruction; ushered in Jim Crow era| |Civil Service Reform|Established first federal civil service commission|Began transition from spoils system toward merit appointments| |Education & Veterans|Advocated improved pensions and support for Civil War veterans|Strengthened veteran welfare programs| |Economic Policy|Maintained gold standard; vetoed inflationary currency bills|Promoted fiscal stability|
