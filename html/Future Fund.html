Chapter: Future Fund — Australia’s Intergenerational Sovereign Wealth Fund

Introduction

The Future Fund is Australia’s sovereign wealth fund, established in 2006 to pre‑fund the nation’s unfunded public sector superannuation liabilities. With approximately USD 225 billion in assets under management (2025), it transforms fiscal obligations into a disciplined, long‑term investment engine.

Origins & Mandate

• Founded: May 2006 by the Australian Government

• Primary Objectives:

  1. Offset the Commonwealth’s future public sector pension liabilities

  2. Deliver returns above the Consumer Price Index plus 4–5% over rolling 10‑year periods

  3. Maintain strong risk management and governance

Governance & Structure

|Body|Role|Accountability| |---|---|---| |Future Fund Board of Guardians|Strategic oversight & investment policy|Reports annually to Parliament| |Chief Executive Officer & Executive Team|Portfolio management & execution|Quarterly performance reporting| |Audit & Risk Committee|Compliance, risk governance|Independent review|

• Geographic Spread: North America (40%), Europe (30%), Asia‑Pacific (25%), Others (5%)

• Time Horizon: 10–20 years, focused on intergenerational returns

• ESG Integration: UN PRI signatory; climate risk scenario analysis

Psychological & Philosophical Reflection

The Future Fund embodies the “Generational Trustee” archetype—transforming current liabilities into future prosperity. Psychologically, it institutionalizes patience and discipline; philosophically, it enshrines intergenerational equity and fiscal prudence.

Actionable Takeaways & Frameworks

  1. Liability‑Driven Investment Model: Map your long‑term obligations and structure assets to match projected cash outflows.

  2. Diversification Matrix: Use a quadrant framework (risk vs return; public vs private) to balance growth and stability.

  3. Governance Scorecard: Define clear performance metrics, oversight roles, and risk limits—review semi‑annually.
