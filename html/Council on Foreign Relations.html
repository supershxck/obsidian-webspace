Overview

  • Full Name: Council on Foreign Relations
  • Type: Nonprofit think tank
  • Founded: 1921
  • Headquarters: New York City, New York, USA

Purpose

The Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) is dedicated to being a resource for its members, government officials, business executives, journalists, educators, and students, among others, to help them better understand the world and the foreign policy choices facing the United States and other countries.

Activities

  • Publications: Produces "Foreign Affairs," a prominent journal on international relations and U.S. foreign policy.
  • Events: Hosts discussions, roundtables, and panels with experts in various fields of international relations.
  • Research: Conducts independent research through its fellows and research programs, producing reports, books, and articles on global issues.
  • Education: Provides educational resources and programs for teachers, students, and the general public to enhance understanding of global affairs.

Influence

The CFR is known for its significant influence on U.S. foreign policy, with many of its members holding key positions in government, academia, and industry. It acts as a forum for nonpartisan discussion and analysis of international issues and policy solutions.

Membership

  • Individual Membership: By invitation only, with members including influential leaders from various sectors.
  • Corporate Membership: Available to businesses and organizations, offering access to CFR resources and events.

Key Resources

  • Foreign Affairs Magazine: A leading publication in the field of international relations, featuring articles by policymakers, scholars, and practitioners.
  • CFR.org: The organization's website, providing access to reports, articles, event recordings, and other resources.
  • CFR Events: Regularly hosts meetings and discussions that are sometimes available to the public via webcasts.

Notable Members

Many prominent figures in U.S. and international politics, business, and academia have been associated with the CFR, using it as a platform to shape and debate foreign policy.

Conclusion

The CFR plays a critical role in shaping and influencing the discourse on global affairs and U.S. foreign policy through its research, publications, events, and educational initiatives. It serves as a crucial resource for policymakers and the public in understanding and navigating international relations.
