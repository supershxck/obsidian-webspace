Driver (Dominant) - part you identify most with  - Flow state uses this function  - Go-to tool  - Natural talent but also a skill that needs development  “Either way, it’s your responsibility to manage the Driver function by scheduling plenty of time for its use and development. If you don’t clock enough time using it, you will find yourself dysthymic and depressed.” “people often enter this state of flow when they are fully and actively using their Driver process in an increasingly proficient way.”

Copilot (Auxiliary) - part that is identified with, but slightly less then driver, undervalued  - Growth state - A natural talent that is usually undeveloped because of personal preference, only used when needed - Opposite attitude of driver, ex: if driver is E then copilot is I “If you are deeply introverted, you may avoid your extraverted Copilot to evade a scary outer world that gives you sensory overload or negative feedback that makes you feel like your thoughts and feelings are wrong and that you aren’t enough. On the other hand, if you are highly extraverted, your introverted Copilot may force you to face uncomfortable introspection, including painful memories, trauma you haven’t worked through, or unfamiliar and unexplored parts of yourself.” “It’s the combination of the Driver and Copilot functions that gets you where you want to go” “but the more conscientious work you do to develop the Copilot, the more traction you’ll get as you gain the necessary mechanisms for dealing with life: information gathering, decision making, inner-world introspection, and outer-world feedback.” “remember that this is usually your single best leverage point for growing yourself.”

Summary The driver and copilot will be opposites to each other, one being extroverted and the other being introverted. It is important to have both as such so that the balance between the outer world and the inner world is negotiated between the two and so that information can be understood and used in an efficient manner. Your driver and copilot will be the areas in which you will have a natural talent but require work to be put in to sufficiently use in a skillful manner. The driver is important for entering the flow state and getting a lot of work done in your most natural way, it can be optimized through practice and consistency to be a powerful tool in life. The copilot is essential to develop consciously and because it will allow you to discern the places of improvement needed for further development.  Backseat passengers are helpful to imagine as children. They are valuable and contribute but childlike in perspectives and characteristics. They have moments of brilliance but can get overwhelmed and be overestimated. 

10yo (Tertiary) - called the defensive position  - Sometimes works well sometimes trips up - Helpful to the copilot for future planning, personal insight, and meaning in life  - Same attitude as driver  - When relied upon by the driver a loop is formed in which the copilot wisdom is excluded throwing off balance - When parents are balanced, 10yo can provide helpful wisdom and insight  - Building up copilot skill can help to avoid the driver/10yo loop “when two adults provide a united front and make the rest of the family aware of their decision or insight, the 10-Year-Old can lean into the relationship and add a helping hand, maybe even provide wisdom in that “kids say the darnedest things!” sorta way. By this, we mean that sometimes, your 10-Year-Old can be an unexpected source of insight.”

3yo (Inferior) - Most unsophisticated part of you - Influences from the shadows through inner turmoil, also called the blind spot  - Being caught in the grip of the 3yo occurs when either the driver is exhausted or the 3yo has been neglected for a while - Must be given attention periodically  - Part of you where your childlike dreams and longings or wonder and excitement manifests  - Technically called your aspiration - The catalyst to find new solutions becoming the source of invention and creativity 

“hand the wheel to a 3-Year-Old, which will yield predictable results. When this happens, you experience tantrums, breakdowns, and moments of pure paranoia or paralysis. These are the moments you don’t really share with anyone other than people who will forgive you afterward, and if these behaviors are seen in public, you feel deeply ashamed or embarrassed. They are your toddler moments.” “Your 3-Year-Old function is … where you get in touch with the most fragile, precious aspects of yourself. It also holds your aspirations, as only a child who hasn’t been touched by cynicism can.” “If you see someone working in their passion, purpose, or mission, there’s almost always some aspect of their 3-Year-Old function whispering in their ear, breathing fire and determination, keeping the coals of their purpose warm. When we utilize the process as a guide for our life’s work, we’re on the right track.”
