Social Liberalism: Balancing Freedom with Social Justice

Introduction

Social Liberalism is a strand of liberal political thought that builds on [[Classical Liberalism]]’s commitment to individual liberty while emphasizing the state’s positive role in ensuring equality of opportunity and social welfare. It underpins much of modern [[British Liberalism]] and Western social‑democratic policy.

|Principle|Description| |---|---| |Positive Liberty|Freedom to achieve one’s potential via education, health, social security| |Fair Opportunity|State intervention to remove structural barriers| |Social Safety Net|Universal healthcare, unemployment insurance, progressive taxation| |Rule of Law & Rights|Protection of civil liberties alongside economic rights|

Key Vocabulary

• Positive Liberty: Capacity to act on one’s free will through enabling conditions

• Welfare State: Government provision of social services to ensure minimum living standards

• Progressive Taxation: Higher rates on greater incomes to fund public goods

• Social Contract: Implicit agreement prioritizing mutual obligations
