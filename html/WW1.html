Germany’s Unrestricted Warfare

[[World War I]] [[Triple Entente]] [[Central Powers]] [[Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand]] [[Western Front]] [[Eastern Front]] [[Trench Warfare]] [[Treaty of Versailles]] [[League of Nations]] [[1914-1918]]

Background

  • Pre-War Tensions: Europe in the early 20th century was a powder keg of national rivalries, militarism, and complex alliances. The main powers were divided into two blocs: the [[Triple Entente]] (France, Russia, and the United Kingdom) and the [[Central Powers]] (Germany, Austria-Hungary, and Italy, although Italy switched sides in 1915).

Outbreak of War

  • Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand: On June 28, 1914, Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary was assassinated by [[Gavrilo Princip]], a Bosnian Serb nationalist, in Sarajevo. This event triggered a chain reaction of alliances and treaties.
  • Declarations of War: Austria-Hungary declared war on Serbia, leading to Russia's mobilization in defense of Serbia. Germany declared war on Russia, and subsequently on France. The UK declared war on Germany after the German invasion of Belgium.

Major Fronts

  • Western Front: Characterized by trench warfare, the Western Front saw a series of brutal battles, including the Battle of the Marne, the Battle of Verdun, and the Battle of the Somme. The front lines remained relatively static for most of the war.
  • Eastern Front: More fluid than the Western Front, the Eastern Front saw significant movements and battles between the Central Powers and Russia. Key battles included Tannenberg and the Brusilov Offensive.

Global Involvement

  • Colonial Theaters: The war extended to colonies in Africa and Asia, with battles and skirmishes in German colonies.
  • Middle East: The British, along with Arab forces led by T.E. Lawrence, fought the Ottoman Empire in campaigns like Gallipoli and the Sinai and Palestine Campaign.
  • Naval Warfare: The war at sea included the Battle of Jutland and unrestricted submarine warfare by Germany, which eventually contributed to the U.S. entering the war.

Key Events

  • Entry of the United States: In 1917, the U.S. entered the war on the side of the Allies, providing a crucial boost in manpower and resources.
  • Russian Revolution: In 1917, Russia underwent two revolutions, leading to the withdrawal of Russia from the war after the Treaty of Brest-Litovsk in 1918.
  • Armistice: The Central Powers began to collapse in 1918, leading to an armistice on November 11, 1918.

Aftermath

  • Treaty of Versailles: Signed in 1919, the treaty placed heavy reparations and territorial losses on Germany. It also established the [[League of Nations]] aimed at preventing future conflicts.
  • Political Changes: The war led to the downfall of empires: the German, Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, and Russian Empires all collapsed.
  • Human Cost: Approximately 10 million soldiers and 7 million civilians died as a result of the war. The conflict also caused widespread destruction and economic upheaval.

Legacy

  • Impact on Europe: The war reshaped the political map of Europe and set the stage for World War II due to the harsh terms of the Treaty of Versailles.
  • Technological Advances: WWI saw the use of new military technologies, including tanks, airplanes, and chemical weapons, which changed the nature of warfare.
