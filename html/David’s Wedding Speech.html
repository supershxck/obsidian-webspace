Wedding Speech

Hello, everyone. I’ve been given the honor, or perhaps the daunting challenge, of speaking to my little brother on this wonderful day. They say marriages are made in heaven, but anyone who’s been married for any amount of time knows they're maintained on earth. That's where humility, diligence, patience, and love come into play. OH and coffee. Can’t forget coffee

Let’s start with humility. In Philippians it says, Do nothing out of selfish ambition or selfish conceit but rather in humility value others above yourselves. It’s a quality David  is quite familiar with, especially now that he’s officially out of his league. Jokes aside, watching him with Karen you can see true humility in the way they genuinely put each other first, a trait as admirable as it is essential for those times when you find yourself arguing over what Netflix series to binge next. Humility doesn’t work alone though. 

Diligence complements humility through hard work and persistence. It’s about the conscious dedication to one’s path and purpose, and the journey shared with your partner. David’s unwavering commitment is complimented by his example of being a present and supportive partner. It’s a balance of pursuing what is deeply meaningful while ensuring the foundation of their relationship is nurtured with attention, care, and most importantly, the Lord. For a marriage to thrive, this is the kind of diligence that allows both individuals to grow, not just side by side, but into one, weaving their dreams and aspirations into a tapestry of full life.

As the Good Book says (Proverbs 15:18), "A hot-tempered person stirs up conflict, but the one who is patient calms a quarrel." and “the fruit of the Spirit, is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, faith, gentleness and self control. Now choosing what to watch on Netflix can indeed test the patience of saints—but it's in these moments that patience shines through. It’s said that: “Marriage is like a dance, it requires a lot of stepping on each other’s toes. And if you’re lucky, it’s to music.” So David and Karen may your life together always have music to dance to, may your steps be gentle, and when you do step on each other’s toes, let it be a step in a dance of full of love. 

Now love. Song of Solomon 8:6-7, is a testament to the enduring power of love: ‘For love is as strong as death, its jealousy unyielding as the grave. It burns like blazing fire, like a mighty flame. Many waters cannot quench love; rivers cannot sweep it away.’ Love is what gets you through those moments after discovering your significant other’s idea of a fancy night involves pizza and watching Shrek for the millionth time. But love, real love, is about being excited about that microwaved pizza and Shrek because it means spending another day together. It’s laughter shared over burnt pizza and dreams built on the foundation of that which is good, excellent, honorable, and lovely!

May God bless you both on this special day and the years to come and the journey ahead. May it be filled with humility, diligence, gentleness, and a love so deep that even the worst microwave pizzas taste like gourmet feasts. God bless the bride and groom!
