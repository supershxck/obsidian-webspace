Neuroticism

Volatility and Withdrawal

Withdrawal = behavior of freezing Volatility = defensive aggression, touchy 

Withdrawal trait descriptors
  • Feel threatened easily 
  • Often feel depressed
  • Easily discouraged
  • Embarrassed easily
  • Overwhelmed by events
  • Afraid of many things
Volatility trait descriptors
  • Get angry easily 
  • Get irritated easily 
  • Get upset easily
  • Cannot control emotions
  • Change my mood alot
  • Loses composure often 
  • Moods go up and down easily
  • Easily annoyed 
  • Easily agitated
  • Stirred up easily 

Hyper reactivity to frustration, disappointment, and pain Withdrawal is associated with freezing related anxiety  Volatility is associated with sensitivity to punishment 

High Neuroticism vs Not
  • Worrying versus calm
  • Nervous versus at ease. 
  • High-strung versus relaxed. 
  • Insecure versus secure. 
  • Self-pitying versus self-satisfied. 
  • Vulnerable versus hardy.
  • Irritable, moody, touchy
Basic elements of negative emotion:

Frustration = when you’re approaching a goal, and you’re blocked.  Disappointment = when you’re pursuing a goal, and it doesn’t work.  Grief = when you lose something. 

Pain, threat, uncertainty, anxiety 

the pain system and the anxiety system actually function separately because anxiety is a marker for pain.  Imagine pain is a marker for damage, and anxiety is a marker for pain or for the possibility of damage.
