St. Bernard of Clairvaux: An Overview

Introduction

St. Bernard of Clairvaux (1090–1153) was a French abbot, theologian, and mystic, and one of the most influential figures in the Cistercian Order of monks during the 12th century. He played a critical role in the reform and expansion of the Cistercian Order, and his teachings have had a profound impact on Christian mysticism, spirituality, and theology. St. Bernard is best known for his deep devotion to the Virgin Mary, his eloquent writings, and his emphasis on divine love as the central theme of the Christian life.

He was canonized as a saint in 1174, and his feast day is celebrated on August 20th. St. Bernard’s influence reached across Europe, and he was recognized as a spiritual leader, advisor, and preacher. His works, particularly on the love of God and the interior life, continue to inspire Christian spirituality to this day.

1. Early Life and Background

1.1 Birth and Family

St. Bernard was born in 1090 in Fontaine-lès-Dijon, in the Burgundy region of France, to a noble family. His father, Tescelin, was a knight, and his mother, Adele, was a pious woman. Bernard was the third of seven children. The family had a strong Christian faith, and Bernard was raised in an environment that fostered both intellectual and spiritual development.

• Education and Early Spirituality

Bernard received an education at the local monastery, where he grew interested in spiritual matters and felt a growing desire to dedicate his life to God. Though he initially studied law, Bernard experienced a powerful conversion that led him to pursue a monastic vocation.

2. Entry into the Monastic Life

2.1 Founding of Clairvaux

At the age of 25, in 1112, Bernard entered the Cistercian Abbey of Cîteaux, a newly established monastery founded by St. Robert of Molesme. The Cistercians were committed to a simplified monastic life, focusing on manual labor, prayer, and the observance of strict discipline.

• Clairvaux and Leadership

After a few years, Bernard was sent with a group of monks to establish a new abbey in Clairvaux (meaning “clear valley”), in the Champagne region of France, in 1115. Bernard became the abbot of Clairvaux, and the monastery soon became one of the most influential centers of monastic life in Europe.

• Expansion of the Cistercian Order

Under Bernard’s leadership, Clairvaux flourished and grew rapidly. Bernard’s charismatic leadership and deep spirituality attracted many followers, and he helped to establish over 60 Cistercian monasteries throughout Europe, expanding the order’s influence and reforming other monastic communities.

3. Theological Contributions and Mysticism

3.1 Emphasis on Divine Love

Bernard’s theology and spirituality centered on the theme of divine love. He believed that the deepest calling of humanity was to love God fully and to express that love in everyday life. For Bernard, love was the foundation of the Christian life and the means through which individuals could attain true communion with God.

• Lover and Beloved: Bernard often used the language of romantic love to describe the relationship between the soul and God. He viewed God as the Beloved and the soul as the lover, expressing the longing of the human heart for divine union.

• The Song of Songs: One of Bernard’s most famous works is his commentary on the Song of Songs (also called the Song of Solomon), an ancient biblical poem. Bernard interpreted the Song of Songs as an allegory of the love between the soul and God, emphasizing the mystical union between them. His reflections on this text are some of the most well-known mystical writings of the Middle Ages.

3.2 Mystical Theology and Spirituality

St. Bernard was a mystic and believed in the importance of experiencing God personally through prayer, contemplation, and devotion. He emphasized the spiritual ascent of the soul toward God through stages of purification, illumination, and union.

• Humility and Purity of Heart: Bernard taught that in order to experience divine love, one must cultivate humility, purity of heart, and detachment from worldly things. He believed that the soul must empty itself of pride and ego to receive God’s love fully.

• Contemplative Prayer: Bernard emphasized the importance of contemplative prayer, where the soul silently seeks communion with God. For Bernard, prayer was not only about speaking to God but about listening to Him and being receptive to His divine presence.

3.3 Advocacy for the Virgin Mary

One of Bernard’s most distinctive theological contributions was his deep devotion to the Virgin Mary. Bernard was a key advocate for the development of Marian devotion in medieval Christianity, particularly the idea of Mary as the mediatrix between humanity and God.

• Mary’s Role as Intercessor: Bernard promoted the belief that Mary could intercede on behalf of humanity and bring God’s mercy and grace to those who called upon her. His famous prayer, “Remember, O most gracious Virgin Mary,” remains a beloved prayer in Christian tradition.

• Mariology: Bernard’s theological focus on Mary contributed to the development of Mariology, the study and veneration of Mary’s role in salvation. His teachings emphasized Mary’s purity, compassion, and role as the Mother of God.

4. Bernard’s Influence and Legacy

4.1 Influence on Monasticism

St. Bernard had a profound impact on Cistercian monasticism and on Christian monasticism as a whole. His leadership and reform efforts helped establish the Cistercians as one of the most influential monastic orders of the Middle Ages.

• Reforming Monasteries: Bernard’s reforms focused on simplicity, humility, and piety. His influence helped shape monastic life to emphasize manual labor, silent prayer, and ascetic discipline. Many monastic communities adopted his way of life, contributing to a broader movement of religious renewal in the Church.

4.2 Theological and Mystical Impact

Bernard’s emphasis on divine love, contemplative spirituality, and the relationship between the soul and God has had a lasting influence on Christian mysticism and spirituality.

• In the Catholic Tradition: Bernard’s writings on prayer, humility, and love continue to influence Catholic spiritual practices. His teachings on the spiritual life continue to inspire Christians seeking a deeper relationship with God through prayer and meditation.

• Influence on Later Mystics: Bernard’s mystical writings paved the way for later mystics like Teresa of Ávila and John of the Cross, whose works also focus on the union between the soul and God and the importance of love in the spiritual journey.

4.3 Advocate of Crusades

In addition to his monastic and mystical contributions, Bernard was also involved in the Second Crusade (1147–1149). He preached for the crusade and sought to rally Christian forces against Muslim rulers in the Holy Land. However, the crusade ended in failure, and Bernard later expressed regret over the violence associated with the crusade.

5. Conclusion

St. Bernard of Clairvaux was a towering figure in 12th-century Christianity, known for his theological depth, mystical insights, and reform of monastic life. His focus on divine love, the mystical union of the soul with God, and the importance of contemplative prayer have left a profound legacy in Christian spirituality. St. Bernard’s works continue to inspire those seeking a deeper, more intimate relationship with God, and his contributions to Christian mysticism and devotion to the Virgin Mary remain influential in Catholicism today. His life and writings exemplify the potential for spiritual transformation through love, humility, and devotion.
