Pleione: The Mother of the Pleiades in Greek Mythology

Pleione is a figure from Greek mythology, known primarily as the mother of the [[Pleiades]], the seven sister nymphs who were later transformed into the famous star cluster in the constellation Taurus. She is associated with protection, motherhood, and celestial guardianship, symbolizing the divine feminine force that nurtures and preserves cosmic harmony.

I. Mythological Origins and Role

1. Family Lineage

Pleione is described as an Oceanid nymph, a daughter of [[Oceanus]] and [[Tethys]], making her one of the many water spirits that represented freshwater sources, particularly the sea and rivers.

She became the wife of the Titan Atlas, the primordial giant who held up the heavens. Together, they had several significant children:

• The Pleiades (Seven Sisters):

• [[Maia]] (mother of Hermes with [[Zeus]])

• [[Electra]]

• [[Taygete]]

• [[Alcyone]]

• [[Celaeno]]

• [[Sterope]]

• [[Merope]]

• The Hyades: A group of rain-bringing nymphs associated with storms and fertility.

• Hyas: A son associated with rain and sometimes linked to the Hyades’ mourning.

2. The Chase by Orion

A key myth involving Pleione is her pursuit by Orion, the great hunter. In some versions, Orion attempted to abduct her, but she managed to escape. However, he later pursued her daughters, the Pleiades, across the sky. To protect them, Zeus transformed them into stars, which became the Pleiades constellation. This myth symbolizes:

• Protection of the divine feminine from violation.

• The eternal dance between hunter (Orion) and the celestial nymphs.

• The theme of cosmic cycles, as Orion continues to “chase” the Pleiades across the sky each year.

II. Esoteric and Astrological Significance

1. Celestial Guardian and Star Mythology

The Pleiades star cluster has been significant in ancient astrology, navigation, and mysticism. Pleione, as their mythological mother, represents:

• A protector of wisdom and cosmic knowledge.

• The source of mystical or hidden knowledge (as the Pleiades were often linked to esoteric traditions).

• The nurturing, feminine force in the cosmos (opposed to Atlas’ burdened masculinity).

2. Astrological Influence

• The Pleiades cluster is often associated with spiritual insight, heightened intuition, and connection to the divine realms.

• Some astrologers believe that individuals born under strong Pleiadian influences (such as placements near the Pleiades cluster in Taurus) may have mystical abilities, an affinity for esoteric knowledge, and a deep longing for celestial origins.

3. Connection to Divine Feminine Archetypes

Pleione embodies themes of:

• Matriarchy and guardianship – a divine mother figure overseeing celestial daughters.

• Mysticism and hidden knowledge – linked to the secretive nature of the Pleiades.

• Spiritual ascension – as the Pleiades are often seen as a gateway to higher consciousness.

III. Pleione in Modern Interpretations

1. Astronomy: The Star Pleione

There is a real star named Pleione in the Pleiades cluster, designated 28 Tauri. It is a Be star known for its rapid rotation and periodic brightness variations, reflecting its dynamic nature—much like its mythological counterpart.

2. New Age and Esoteric Thought

• The Pleiades are often referenced in mystical and extraterrestrial theories, with some spiritual traditions claiming that Pleione represents an ancient, nurturing wisdom from beyond Earth.

• In occult traditions, the Pleiades and Pleione are linked to divine mothers, priestesses, and celestial lineages.

IV. Conclusion: Pleione as a Cosmic Matriarch

Pleione represents the protective, maternal force of the heavens, shielding her daughters and guiding their celestial journey. Whether in mythology, astrology, or esoteric traditions, she is a symbol of wisdom, guardianship, and the divine feminine presence in the cosmos. She remains a key figure in the great mythological and astrological tapestry of the Pleiades, embodying the interplay between mysticism, femininity, and cosmic order.
