To make the most of your 17 Todoist projects and exponentially boost your workflow, follow these steps:

1. Organize Projects by Major Life Areas

Map each project to a major area of your life or work. Examples: - Personal Development: Kundalini awakening, spiritual growth. - Work/Business: IT, coding, and business goals. - Learning: Greek philosophy, Carl Jung, Obsidian setup. - Health & Wellness: Fitness routines, gardening, etc. - Creative Projects: The Alchemy of the Soul or tarot-related projects.

2. Define Actionable Subcategories

If a project is too broad, create subcategories using sections or labels within each project: - "Personal Development" could include sections like Meditation, Daily Affirmations, and Dream Journals. - "Work/Business" might divide into Current Clients, Tech Skills, and Side Hustles.

3. Leverage Recurring Tasks and Deadlines

Use recurring tasks for regular activities like: - Affirmations or shadow work. - Weekly business reviews. - Monthly project milestones.

4. Use Filters and Labels to Create Cross-Project Views

Labels can group similar tasks across projects: - Labels for Energy: Tag tasks by their energy level (e.g., HighEnergy, QuickWins). - Labels for Time: Tag tasks like 15min or DeepWork to match time available.

Filters allow you to see all tasks across projects based on: - Deadlines (e.g., "today & QuickWins"). - Priority (e.g., "P1 & DeepWork").

5. Implement Eisenhower Matrix via Priority Levels

  • P1: Urgent and important (focus immediately).
  • P2: Important, not urgent (schedule proactively).
  • P3: Urgent, not important (delegate if possible).
  • P4: Not urgent or important (consider eliminating).

6. Use Kanban for Visual Workflow

  • Within each project, create sections as Kanban boards (e.g., To Do, In Progress, Completed). This is great for tracking writing, coding, or planning workflows.

7. Weekly and Daily Reviews

  • Weekly: Review and prioritize tasks in each project.
  • Daily: Review the "Today" tab and adjust based on your energy and schedule.

8. Integrate Todoist with Other Tools

  • Obsidian: Link key Todoist tasks to your second brain.
  • Google Calendar: Sync deadlines for better time management.
  • Zapier: Automate task creation from emails or notes.

9. Gamify Your Productivity

  • Track how many tasks you complete weekly per project.
  • Reward yourself for milestones, like completing major tasks in Alchemy of the Soul or launching a website.

10. Archive and Rotate Projects

If certain projects are seasonal or temporary, archive them to reduce clutter and revisit them as needed.
