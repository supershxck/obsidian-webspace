Compassion and Politeness

Compassion trait descriptors
  • not interested in peoples problems (reversed)
  • Feels others emotions
  • Inquires about others well being
  • Can’t be bothered about other peoples needs (reversed)
  • Sympathize with others needs
  • Indifferent to the feelings of others (reversed)
  • Take no time for others (reversed)
  • Takes in interest in others lives
  • Doesn’t have a soft side (reversed)
  • Likes to do things for others
Politeness trait descriptors
  • Respect authority 
  • Believe I am better then others 
  • Hate to seem pushy (reversed)
  • Take advantage of others 
  • Avoid imposing my will on others (reversed)
  • Rarely put pressure on people (reversed)
  • Insult people 
  • Seek conflict
  • Love a good fight
  • Im out for my own personal gain
Agreeable people are…
  • Good natured vs irritable 
  • Soft hearted vs ruthless
  • Courteous vs rude
  • Forgiving vs vengeful
  • Sympathetic vs callous
  • Agreeable vs disagreeable

Agreeableness helps us solve the problem of how much to value our own interests compared to others.  Language is biased to make it sound like agreeableness is better but there is no obvious advantage to either. 

Both have pros and cons  Agreeable behavior is useful in maternal care  Disagreeable behavior is useful in predatory aggression

Disagreeable people are unlikely to be used by others, often receive higher salaries, and do not become resentful.  Agreeable people often become resentful and passive aggressive over time
