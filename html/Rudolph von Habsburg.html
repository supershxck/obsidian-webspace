  • Full Name: Rudolph I of Habsburg
  • Born: May 1, 1218
  • Died: July 15, 1291
  • Title: King of the Romans, Holy Roman Emperor
  • Reign: 1273-1291

Early Life and Rise to Power

  • Origins: Born to Count Albert IV of Habsburg and Heilwig of Kyburg in what is now Switzerland.
  • Initial Status: He began his career as a minor noble in the Aargau region. The Habsburgs were relatively obscure at this time.
  • Political Landscape: The Holy Roman Empire was fragmented with several powerful duchies and principalities. After the death of Emperor Frederick II, the empire went through a period of interregnum (1250-1273) without a universally recognized emperor.

Election and Reign

  • Election as King: Rudolph was elected King of the Romans in 1273, largely due to the support of the Archbishop of Mainz and other influential electors who preferred a relatively weak and manageable candidate.
  • Challenges: Faced significant opposition from Ottokar II of Bohemia, who controlled significant territories in Central Europe.
  • War with Ottokar: Rudolph defeated Ottokar II in the Battle of Marchfeld in 1278, establishing Habsburg control over Austria and Styria, thus laying the foundation for Habsburg power in Central Europe.

Contributions and Legacy

  • Administration: Strengthened the administration of the empire and worked to restore the authority of the central monarchy.
  • Dynastic Foundations: His reign marked the beginning of the Habsburgs’ long-lasting influence in European politics. His descendants would go on to become one of the most powerful dynasties in Europe.
  • Death: Died in 1291 and was succeeded by his son, Albert I.
