Below is a (very) extensive list of divinatory practices from around the world and throughout history. Many of these end with the suffix “-mancy” (from the Greek manteia, meaning “divination”), though some go by other names. This compilation is not absolutely exhaustive—variations and regional forms abound—but it should give you a broad overview of how many methods human cultures have developed to divine insight or hidden information.

Extensive List of Divinatory Practices

A

• Ailuromancy – Divination by observing the behavior of cats.

• Aleuromancy – Divination using flour or dough (e.g., messages baked inside bread, akin to fortune cookies).

• Alomancy – Divination by interpreting the patterns of salt.

• Anthracomancy – Divination by observing burning coals.

• Aeromancy – Divination by interpreting air phenomena or atmospheric conditions (cloud shapes, winds, etc.).

• Alectoromancy – Divination by observing a rooster picking at grains placed on letters.

• Alectryomancy – Variant spelling of Alectoromancy.

• Ambulomancy – Divination by walking randomly (letting one’s route “reveal” signs).

• Amniomancy – Interpreting omens from the caul or membranes around a newborn.

• Anthropomancy – An archaic/forbidden form, allegedly involving sacrifice/entrails of humans.

• Apantomancy – Reading omens in chance encounters with animals or objects.

B

• Belomancy – Divination using arrows or darts; messages are written on them, and one is drawn by lot.

• Bibliomancy – Randomly opening a sacred or special book (often the Bible) and interpreting the passage.

• Botanomancy – Divination through herbs and plants (burning or arranging them).

• Brontomancy – Divination by listening to thunder.

C

• Capnomancy – Reading the shapes and movements of smoke from a fire or incense.

• Cartomancy – Divination by cards (generic term for methods like Tarot, Lenormand, playing cards).

• Cartouche Reading – Using Egyptian-inspired oracle cards called “cartouche.”

• Catoptromancy – Divination using mirrors (sometimes called “mirror scrying”).

• Ceromancy – Pouring melted wax into water and interpreting the shapes that form.

• Ceroscopy – Alternate name for Ceromancy.

• Causimancy – Observing the behavior of items burned in a fire.

• Cephalomancy – Reading omens from the head (usually of an animal) cooked over a fire.

• Chalcomancy – Listening to sounds made by striking copper or bronze basins.

• Chanomancy – Divination by interpreting tea leaves (more commonly known as Tasseomancy, but “chan” references tea in some languages).

• Chiromancy (Palmistry) – Reading the lines, shapes, and texture of the hand.

• Chronomancy – Divination to determine the most auspicious times for actions.

• Cledonism (or Cledonomancy) – Divination by chance words or sounds overheard.

• Cleromancy – Casting lots, dice, bones, or other random objects to gain insights.

• Coscinomancy – Using a balanced sieve or shears to detect truth or falsehood.

• Crithomancy – Using barley cakes or grain in divination.

• Crystallomancy (Crystal Scrying) – Gazing into a crystal or reflective surface to see visions.

• Cubomancy – Divination by throwing dice (numbers or shapes have significance).

• Cyclomancy – Divination by spinning a wheel or top and interpreting the result.

D

• Dactylomancy – Using a suspended ring or pendulum, often over letters or symbols.

• Daphnomancy – Burning laurel leaves and interpreting crackling sounds or flames.

• Dendromancy – Reading the knotholes, bark, or leaves of a tree (or how logs burn).

• Dowsing (Rhabdomancy) – Using a forked stick or rods to find water, minerals, or other hidden objects.

• Dririmancy – Interpreting dripping blood.

• Dririmantia – Alternate name/spelling for Dririmancy.

• Druidic Ogham Divination – Using Celtic Ogham letters inscribed on sticks or stones.

E

• Enoptromancy – Using a mirror or a glass container (a form of catoptromancy).

• Extispicy – Reading omens from the entrails of sacrificial animals (see also Haruspicy).

F

• Face Reading (Physiognomy) – Judging personality or destiny from facial features.

• Feng Shui (Chinese Geomancy) – Interpreting the flow of Qi (energy) in living spaces and landscapes.

• Floriography – Although not exactly “divination,” the language of flowers can be used symbolically to interpret signs.

G

• Gastromancy – Listening to or interpreting sounds from the stomach or from ventriloquism in a trance.

• Gelomancy – Divination from laughter (the manner or timing of laughs).

• Geomancy – Interpreting markings or patterns in earth, sand, or soil (or using random dots for a 16-figure system).

• Graphology – Analyzing handwriting to read personality or fate (more “character reading” than direct divination).

• Gyromancy – Walking or dancing in a circle until dizzy and interpreting where one falls or what letters they land on.

H

• Halomancy – Observing salt patterns (related to Alomancy).

• Haruspicy – Entrail reading (commonly associated with ancient Rome).

• Hepatoscopy – Examining the liver of animals for omens (subset of Haruspicy).

• Hieromancy – Divination using sacred objects or texts.

• Hippomancy – Reading omens from the behavior of horses.

• Horoscopy (Astrology) – Casting a horoscope based on planetary positions at birth or significant events.

• Hydromancy – Observing water (ripples, color, ebb and flow) for signs.

I

• I Ching (Yijing) – Chinese divination using hexagrams formed by yarrow stalks or coins, combined with an ancient text of wisdom.

• Ichthyomancy – Reading patterns of fish (movement, entrails, or their remains).

• Idolomancy – Divination involving idols or statues (observing movements, lots drawn before them, etc.).

• Ink Scrying – Gazing into ink (often black or colored) poured into a bowl.

K

• Kapnomancy – Variant spelling or practice akin to Capnomancy.

• Keraunomancy – Divination by thunder and lightning.

• Krithomancy – Another term for divination with grain or barley (similar to Crithomancy).

• Kybomancy – Dice divination (another term for Cubomancy).

L

• Lampadomancy – Reading the flame of a lamp or candle.

• Leconomancy (Lecanomancy) – Divination by observing water in a basin, sometimes with oil droplets.

• Lenormand Readings – A type of cartomancy using the oracle deck attributed to Mlle. Lenormand.

• Libanomancy – Divination by interpreting the smoke of incense.

• Lithomancy – Casting stones, crystals, or gems and interpreting their positions.

M

• Margaritomancy – Using pearls: observing how they react when heated, or the movements they might make in a dish.

• Mathematical Divination (Arithmancy/Numerology) – Using numbers, names, or birthdates to divine insights.

• Meteormancy – Observing meteors or falling stars.

• Metoposcopy – Reading a person’s forehead lines.

• Molybdomancy – Pouring molten metal (often lead or tin) into water and interpreting the shapes.

• Myomancy – Divination by interpreting the behavior of mice.

N

• Narcomancy – Divination by trance or dream states.

• Necromancy – Calling upon the dead or ancestral spirits to gain knowledge.

• Nihomancy – Divination by ashes or burnt offerings (closely related to pyromancy).

• Numerology – Interpreting the mystical significance of numbers (a form of Arithmancy).

O

• Oculomancy – Divination via the eyes (reading energy through gazing or a subject’s eye movements).

• Odontomancy – Divination by teeth (observing how they grow, fall out, or certain patterns in animal teeth).

• Oinomancy – Divination by observing wine (color, sediment, movement in the cup).

• Omphalomancy – Examining the navel (commonly the umbilical cord of a newborn) to predict family size or destiny.

• Oneiromancy – Dream interpretation (decoding symbols appearing in dreams).

• Onomancy (Onomatomancy) – Divination using the letters of a person’s name.

• Onychomancy – Gazing at the reflection of sunlight on fingernails or the shapes formed by them.

• Oomancy – Divination by eggs (cracking an egg into water and interpreting the patterns).

• Ophiomancy – Observing snakes’ movements and behavior.

• Ornithomancy – Observing birds (flight patterns, species, calls).

• Osteomancy – Casting or interpreting bones; used traditionally in many cultures (e.g., African bone casting).

P

• Palmistry (Chiromancy) – Reading palm lines and shapes (listed again here under “P”).

• Papyromancy – Divination by paper (e.g., folding or burning paper shapes).

• Pegomancy – Observing water from springs or fountains (related to Hydromancy).

• Pendulum Divination – Using a weighted object on a string or chain to answer yes/no questions.

• Phrenology – Reading personality traits from skull shape (historically used more for “character reading,” though sometimes viewed as a form of divination).

• Phyllorhodomancy – Clapping rose leaves in one’s hand to listen for certain sounds.

• Physiognomy – Interpreting facial features and expressions.

• Pyromancy – Watching flames, fire behavior, or how objects burn.

Q

• Qabbalistic Gematria – Using Hebrew letters’ numerical values and corresponding mystical meanings.

R

• Radiesthesia – A general term that includes dowsing, pendulum work, and sensitivity to “rays” or energies.

• Rhapsodomancy – Randomly picking a verse or passage from a poem (often Homer, Virgil) and interpreting it.

• Rhabdomancy – Divination by rods or wands (e.g., dowsing rods).

• Ruinomancy – Divination by ruins or rubble, though some use this term for Runes.

• Runecasting (Runic Divination) – Interpreting the symbols of the runic alphabet when cast or drawn.

S

• Scapulomancy – Examining the shoulder blades of animals (after heating or scraping).

• Sciomancy – Communication with shadows or shades (spirits).

• Scrying – Generic term for gazing into reflective surfaces (crystal balls, mirrors, water) for visions.

• Sebomancy – Using wax or tallow to interpret shapes (related to Ceromancy).

• Sideromancy – Observing straws placed on a hot iron and reading how they burn.

• Sortilege – Any form of drawing lots or random selection of tokens.

• Spodomancy – Reading ashes left after a fire.

• Sycomancy – Divination by figs or fig leaves.

T

• Tasseomancy (Tasseography) – Reading tea leaves (or coffee grounds) left in a cup.

• Tectomancy – Observing the cracks that form in cooling objects, often pottery or heated materials.

• Tephromancy – Divination by observing ash (similar to Spodomancy).

• Theomancy – Prophetic divination claimed to be under direct inspiration of a deity.

• Tiromancy – Reading omens from cheese formations (aging, coagulation patterns).

• Tuphonomancy – Observing fossil formations or petrified objects.

U

• Uromancy – Interpreting urine (historically used in medical contexts but also for divinatory signs).

V

• Vitomancy – Reading vine growth patterns or the fermentation of wine.

• Volvomancy – Divination from the entrails or shapes of womb/placenta (rarely referenced, but exists in some contexts).

X

• Xylomancy – Observing driftwood, twigs, or fallen tree branches (their position or shape).

Y

• Yijing (I Ching) Divination – Casting yarrow stalks or coins to form hexagrams, then consulting the Book of Changes.

Z

• Zoomancy – Generic term for divination by observing animals (encompasses many narrower forms like Ophiomancy, Ornithomancy, etc.).

Additional/Related Categories

• Astrology (Multiple Traditions):

• Western Astrology – Twelve zodiac signs, birth charts, planetary transits.

• Vedic Astrology (Jyotisha) – Indian system with sidereal zodiac and unique chart calculations.

• Chinese Astrology – Twelve animal signs, five elements, cycles of years.

• Mesoamerican Astrology (Mayan/Aztec) – Complex calendars (Tzolk’in, Haab) with day signs.

• Tarot and Oracle Systems:

• Tarot de Marseille, Rider-Waite-Smith, Thoth, etc. – Using 78 cards for introspection or prediction.

• Lenormand Cards – A 36-card system for direct, practical readings.

• Kipper Cards – Another European cartomantic deck focusing on daily life events.

• Oracle Cards – Any non-traditional deck (often thematic) used for intuitive readings.

• Dream Interpretation (Oneiromancy):

• Practiced in multiple cultures, from ancient Egyptian dream books to psychoanalytic theories.

• Spirit Communication & Mediumship:

• Ouija / Spirit Boards – Letters and numbers on a board to channel messages.

• Seances – Attempting direct contact with spirits for messages or guidance.

• Trance Mediumship – A medium enters an altered state to relay spiritual messages.

• Modern or Less Common Forms:

• Cybermancy – Seeking synchronicities or signs through the internet, social media, or random online generators.

• Emoji Divination – A playful contemporary spin, interpreting random emojis as signs.

• App-based Tarot / Digital Rune Casting – Electronic versions of classic systems.

Observations and Notes

  1. Overlap & Synonyms: Many of these terms overlap or appear under different names in various cultures. For instance, “bones” can be used in osteomancy (Western term) or “throwing the bones” in African traditions.

  2. Symbolic vs. Literal: In many cases, the ritual or method is symbolic, providing a focus for intuition or spiritual guidance rather than purely “predictive” mechanics.

  3. Cultural & Historical Roots: Some forms (e.g., haruspicy, hepatoscopy) emerged in ancient civilizations (Mesopotamia, Rome, Etruria). Others (e.g., Tarot, cartomancy) blossomed in medieval or Renaissance Europe. Still others (like I Ching) trace back to ancient China.

  4. Evolving Practices: New forms of divination emerge as technology and cultural creativity evolve—people adapt ancient methods or invent modern spins.

Final Thoughts

Human beings have, across time and cultures, created a stunning variety of methods to seek insight into the unknown—be it the future, hidden realities, or the deeper self. Divination often serves both a psychological and a spiritual purpose, helping practitioners interpret synchronicities and engage with intuition. While some forms remain widely practiced (e.g., astrology, Tarot), others are historical curiosities. Together, they highlight our enduring desire to find meaning in the patterns of the world around us.
