Simon Grunau: The Chronicler of Prussian History

Simon Grunau (c. 1465–1530) was a Roman Catholic priest and historian known for writing the “Prussian Chronicle” (Preussische Chronik), the earliest detailed history of Prussia. His work, written in the early 16th century, is a mix of historical fact, legend, and nationalist storytelling, making it both an important source and a controversial one.

1. Who Was Simon Grunau?

• Grunau was a Catholic monk, possibly a Dominican, from Royal Prussia (a region that was under Polish rule after the Teutonic Knights’ defeat in 1466).

• Lived during a time when Prussian identity was contested between the Teutonic Order, Poland, and the emerging Lutheran movement.

• Traveled extensively, collecting oral traditions, documents, and folklore about the Old Prussians and the Teutonic Knights.

2. The Prussian Chronicle (1510–1529)

Grunau’s Prussian Chronicle (Preussische Chronik) was an attempt to preserve Prussian history from ancient times to the 16th century.

A. Structure of the Chronicle

• Volume 1: Myths and early Prussian history.

• Volume 2: The rise of the Teutonic Knights and Christianization of Prussia.

• Volume 3: The decline of the Order and contemporary events.

B. Key Themes

• Pagan Prussian Mythology: He recorded legendary Prussian gods and kings, though much of this is likely fabricated.

• Teutonic Order’s Conquest: He described their crusade against the Old Prussians (13th century).

• Pro-Catholic, Anti-Protestant Views: He was critical of Lutheranism, which was spreading in Prussia.

3. Controversies and Problems

• Invented Mythology: His accounts of Prussian pagan gods (e.g., Perkunos, Potrimpos, and Peckols) were likely fabricated or based on Baltic folklore.

• Anti-Teutonic Order Bias: Though he admired their past, he criticized the Knights’ corruption.

• Pro-Catholic, Anti-Lutheran: He attacked the spread of Protestantism in Prussia.

Despite these issues, his work remains one of the few detailed sources on early Prussia.

4. Legacy and Influence

• Used by later historians as a reference for Prussian history.

• Helped shape Prussian identity during later periods.

• Criticized for its inaccuracies, but still valuable for its mix of history and folklore.
