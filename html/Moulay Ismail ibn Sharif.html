Moulay Ismail ibn Sharif was a significant figure in Moroccan history.1 Here's a summary of key information about him:

  • Ruler of Morocco:
    • He was the Sultan of Morocco from 1672 to 1727, and the second ruler of the 'Alawi dynasty.2
    • His reign was one of the longest in Moroccan history.3
  • Consolidation of Power:
    • He is known for consolidating 'Alawi power and establishing a strong central government.4
    • He created a powerful army, notably the "Black Guard" ('Abid al-Bukhari), which was crucial to his military successes.5
  • Military Achievements:
    • He successfully recaptured several ports from European powers, including the English and Spanish.6
    • He engaged in military campaigns to secure Morocco's borders.
  • Legacy:
    • He made Meknes his capital and undertook extensive construction projects, leaving behind impressive architectural legacies.7
    • His reign is marked both by his strength and his ruthlessness.
  • Personal life:
    • He is known to have had a very large amount of offspring.8

In essence, Moulay Ismail ibn Sharif was a powerful and influential sultan who played a crucial role in shaping the history of Morocco.9
