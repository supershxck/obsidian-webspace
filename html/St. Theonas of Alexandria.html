St. Theonas of Alexandria: The Bishop Who Strengthened the Early Church

St. Theonas of Alexandria (d. 300 AD) was the 16th Patriarch of Alexandria, serving as bishop from c. 282 to 300 AD. He played a key role in strengthening the Alexandrian Church, preparing it for the coming persecution under Diocletian, and promoting Christian scholarship and monasticism. His tenure set the stage for the leadership of his successor, St. Peter of Alexandria, who became a martyr.

1. Historical Background and Episcopal Leadership

Theonas became Patriarch (Bishop) of Alexandria around 282 AD, at a time when Christianity was growing but still facing imperial hostility. His leadership was marked by spiritual and structural development, helping to expand Christianity in Egypt and beyond.

Key Aspects of His Episcopacy

  1. Preparation for Persecution

• During Theonas’s time, Emperor Diocletian (r. 284–305 AD) initially tolerated Christianity but later launched the Great Persecution (303–311 AD).

• Theonas foresaw the dangers ahead and strengthened the faith of the Alexandrian Christians, ensuring they remained steadfast.

  1. Christian Scholarship and the Catechetical School

• Alexandria was home to the Catechetical School, the greatest Christian intellectual center of the time.

• Theonas continued its support, fostering Christian scholarship, including the allegorical interpretation of Scripture developed by Origen.

  1. Expansion of Christian Worship

• He reportedly built a large church in Alexandria, one of the first grand Christian places of worship in the city, allowing Christians to gather more openly.

2. Connection to Imperial Politics

Advisor to Emperor Diocletian?

Some traditions suggest that Theonas had connections to the imperial court. A letter attributed to him offers advice to a high-ranking Christian in Diocletian’s service, instructing him on how to maintain his faith while working in a pagan government.

This shows that:

• Some Christians held government positions while remaining faithful to Christ.

• Theonas was concerned with Christian conduct in public life.

3. Death and Succession

St. Theonas died around 300 AD, just before Diocletian’s Great Persecution began. His successor, St. Peter of Alexandria (r. 300–311 AD), would soon face intense persecution and martyrdom.

4. Legacy and Veneration

Why St. Theonas Matters

• Strengthened the Alexandrian Church before persecution.

• Promoted theological study and monasticism, influencing later figures like St. Athanasius and the Desert Fathers.

• Helped expand Christian influence in Egypt and North Africa.

Feast Day

• Eastern Orthodox Church & Coptic Church: November 23

• Roman Catholic Church: Not widely commemorated

5. Conclusion: The Silent Architect of Alexandrian Christianity

Though not as well-known as Origen, Athanasius, or Cyril of Alexandria, St. Theonas played a crucial role in preparing the Egyptian Church for persecution and future theological debates. His leadership ensured that Christianity in Alexandria survived and flourished, paving the way for the great theological battles of the 4th century.
