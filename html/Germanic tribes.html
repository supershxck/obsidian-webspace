Outline of Germanic Tribes from the Anglo-Saxon Era

[[Angles]]

  • Origin: Originated from the region of Angeln in modern-day Germany.
  • Migration: Migrated to Britain in the 5th and 6th centuries.
  • Settlement: Established the kingdoms of East Anglia, Mercia, and Northumbria.

[[Saxons]]

  • Origin: Originated from the region of Saxony in modern-day Germany.
  • Migration: Migrated to Britain in the 5th and 6th centuries.
  • Settlement: Established the kingdoms of Wessex, Essex, and Sussex.

[[Jutes]]

  • Origin: Originated from the Jutland Peninsula in modern-day Denmark.
  • Migration: Migrated to Britain in the 5th and 6th centuries.
  • Settlement: Established the kingdom of Kent and parts of the Isle of Wight and Hampshire.

[[Frisians]]

  • Origin: Originated from the coastal region of modern-day Netherlands and Germany.
  • Migration: Some migrated to Britain alongside the Angles, Saxons, and Jutes.
  • Settlement: Influenced the cultural and linguistic development of early Anglo-Saxon England.

[[Franks]]

  • Origin: Originated from the region along the Rhine River in modern-day Germany and France.
  • Expansion: Established a powerful kingdom in Gaul (modern-day France) under leaders like Clovis.
  • Influence: Played a significant role in the early medieval history of Europe, including interactions with the Anglo-Saxons.

[[Alemanni]]

  • Origin: Originated from the region of modern-day southwestern Germany.
  • Expansion: Formed a confederation of tribes that settled in the region of modern-day Germany, Switzerland, and Alsace.
  • Conflict: Frequently clashed with the Roman Empire and later with the Franks.

[[Burgundians]]

  • Origin: Originated from the island of Bornholm in the Baltic Sea.
  • Migration: Moved southward and established a kingdom in the region of modern-day eastern France and western Switzerland.
  • Influence: Became a significant power in the early medieval period before being absorbed by the Franks.

[[Lombards]]

  • Origin: Originated from the region of modern-day northern [[Germany]].
  • Migration: Migrated southward into Italy in the 6th century.
  • Settlement: Established the Kingdom of the Lombards in Italy, which lasted until the 8th century.

[[Visigoths]]

  • Origin: Originated from the region north of the Black Sea.
  • Migration: Moved westward, sacking Rome in 410 AD, and eventually settled in Hispania (modern-day Spain and Portugal).
  • Kingdom: Established the Visigothic Kingdom, which lasted until the early 8th century.

[[Ostrogoths]]

  • Origin: Originated from the region north of the Black Sea.
  • Migration: Moved westward and established a kingdom in Italy in the late 5th century under Theodoric the Great.
  • Influence: Played a significant role in the history of Italy until being conquered by the [[Byzantine Empire]].

[[Vandals]]

  • Origin: Originated from the region of modern-day Poland.
  • Migration: Moved westward through [[Gaul]] and [[Hispania]], eventually crossing into North Africa.
  • Kingdom: Established a kingdom in North Africa, sacking Rome in 455 AD, and ruling until conquered by the Byzantine Empire in the 6th century.

Conclusion

The Germanic tribes of the Anglo-Saxon era played crucial roles in the transformation of Europe during the early medieval period. Each tribe contributed to the cultural, political, and social development of the regions they settled, leaving lasting legacies that shaped the future of Europe.
