Chapter: Operation Kadesh — Israel’s Sinai Offensive in the Suez Crisis

Introduction

Operation Kadesh (Hebrew: מבצע קדש) was Israel’s military campaign into the Sinai Peninsula from 29 October to 7 November 1956, conducted in coordination with British and French forces during the wider Suez Crisis. Its goals were to secure Israel’s southern border, reopen the Straits of Tiran to Israeli shipping, and weaken Egyptian military capabilities.

Historical Context

  • Preceding Tensions: Repeated cross‑border raids by Fedayeen guerrillas from Gaza; Egyptian blockade of Eilat shipping through the Straits of Tiran
  • Diplomatic Prelude: Secret Protocol of Sèvres (October 1956) coordinated Anglo‑French plan to retake the Suez Canal after Egyptian President Nasser nationalized it

Strategic Objectives

| Objective | Description | |-----------|-------------| | Border Security | Eliminate Fedayeen bases in Sinai | | Freedom of Navigation | Reopen Gulf of Aqaba and Straits of Tiran | | Strategic Deterrence | Demonstrate military capability to deter future Egyptian aggression |

Timeline of Key Events

| Date | Event | |------|-------| | 29 Oct 1956 | Israel launches pre‑emptive air strikes and ground invasion into Sinai | | 30 Oct 1956 | Israeli forces capture Rafah and begin advance toward Mitla Pass | | 1 Nov 1956 | British and French ultimatum to Egypt; subsequent Anglo‑French air strikes | | 2 Nov 1956 | Israeli forces reach Sharm el‑Sheikh — Straits of Tiran reopened | | 6 Nov 1956 | UN Security Council calls for ceasefire; deployment of UNEF begins | | 7 Nov 1956 | Israel completes withdrawal from Sinai under international pressure |

Outcomes & Legacy

| Dimension | Impact | |-----------|--------| | Territorial | Temporary occupation of Sinai (until March 1957) | | Diplomatic | Cemented U.S. influence in Middle East; decline of British/French regional power | | Military | Validated Israel’s pre‑emptive strike doctrine; enhanced IDF reputation | | Strategic | Established United Nations Emergency Force (UNEF) as first peacekeeping mission |

Psychological & Philosophical Reflection

Operation Kadesh illustrates the “Preemptive Security” archetype—balancing existential threat perception against international norms. It underscores tensions between sovereign self‑defense and multilateral legitimacy.
