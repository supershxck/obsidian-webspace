Dmitri Mendeleev (1834–1907): Father of the Periodic Table

Introduction

Dmitri Ivanovich Mendeleev was a Russian chemist whose creation of the Periodic Table of Elements in 1869 transformed chemistry from descriptive cataloguing into a predictive, unifying science. His work remains foundational to modern chemistry, materials science, and our understanding of matter.

Early Life & Education

• Born: February 8, 1834 in Tobolsk, Siberia

• Overcame family hardship after losing sight temporarily at age 13

• Studied at the Main Pedagogical Institute in Saint Petersburg; earned doctorate in 1865

|Milestone|Year|Significance| |---|---|---| |First Table Published|1869|Organized 63 known elements by atomic weight and properties| |Prediction of Elements|1871|Accurately forecast properties of gallium, scandium, and germanium| |Periodic Law Formulated|1871|“Properties of elements are a periodic function of their atomic weights”|

Key Vocabulary

• Periodic Law: The recurring trends in chemical properties when elements are ordered by atomic number

• Group (Family): Column in the periodic table sharing valence electron configuration

• Period: Row in the periodic table indicating principal energy level

• Predictive Chemistry: Use of systematic patterns to forecast undiscovered phenomena
