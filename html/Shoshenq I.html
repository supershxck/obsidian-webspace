Shoshenq I: The Libyan Pharaoh Who Invaded Israel

Shoshenq I (c. 943–922 BC) was the founder of Egypt’s 22nd Dynasty and the first Libyan ruler of Egypt. He is most famous for:

• Uniting Egypt after political fragmentation.

• Expanding Egyptian influence into Canaan.

• Possibly being the biblical “Shishak”, who invaded Jerusalem during King Rehoboam’s reign.

His reign marked a shift from native Egyptian rule to Libyan leadership, as his family came from the Meshwesh Berber tribe that had settled in Egypt generations earlier.

1. Shoshenq I’s Libyan Origins and Rise to Power

Shoshenq came from the Meshwesh Libyans, a group that migrated into Egypt during the 20th Dynasty (c. 1189–1077 BC).

• His ancestors served as military commanders and governors under the weakened Egyptian pharaohs of the 21st Dynasty.

• As the High Commander of Egypt’s armies, he gained immense power.

• Around 943 BC, he seized the throne peacefully and became Pharaoh, marrying into the royal family to legitimize his rule.

This began the 22nd Dynasty, known as the Libyan or Bubastite Dynasty, with its capital in Bubastis (eastern Nile Delta).

2. His Military Campaign in Canaan (c. 925 BC)

One of Shoshenq’s most famous achievements was his campaign into the Levant, recorded in both Egyptian inscriptions and the Bible.

A. The Biblical Connection: “Shishak”

• The Book of Kings (1 Kings 14:25-26) and Chronicles (2 Chronicles 12:1-9) mention “Shishak”, a pharaoh who invaded Jerusalem in King Rehoboam’s fifth year (c. 925 BC).

• The Bible states that Shishak:

• Plundered the Temple of Solomon.

• Took golden treasures and shields from the city.

• Allowed Rehoboam to remain king as a vassal.

• Many historians believe Shoshenq I = Shishak, as his conquests in Canaan match the biblical timeframe.

B. The Karnak Inscriptions: Shoshenq’s Conquests

• In Karnak Temple, Shoshenq boasts of his victories over cities in Israel and Judah.

• His inscriptions mention:

• Conquering Megiddo, Gezer, and other Canaanite cities.

• Subduing Israelite and Philistine kings.

• His army was a mix of Egyptians, Libyans, and mercenaries.

However, Jerusalem is not specifically mentioned, leading some scholars to question if he actually attacked the city or just received tribute.

3. Domestic Rule: Strengthening Egypt

Shoshenq I worked to restore Egyptian power, which had declined under the previous weak pharaohs.

• Reduced the power of Theban priests, centralizing power in his [[Bubastite Dynasty]].

• Built monuments and expanded Karnak, reviving Egypt’s grand temple projects.

• Strengthened the military using both Egyptians and Libyan mercenaries.

4. Death and Succession

Shoshenq I died around 922 BC, and his son, Osorkon I, succeeded him.

His dynasty ruled for over a century, but later pharaohs were weaker, leading to the eventual collapse of Libyan rule in Egypt.

5. Legacy and Historical Impact

• First foreign pharaoh to rule Egypt (of Libyan descent).

• Revived Egyptian military expansion.

• May have plundered Solomon’s Temple, influencing biblical history.

• His dynasty set the stage for later Kushite and Assyrian invasions of Egypt.
