Eusebius of Caesarea, often regarded as the "Father of Church History," meticulously documented the lineage and succession of early Christian leaders in his seminal work, Ecclesiastical History. One of the key figures in his account of apostolic succession is Polycarp, the Bishop of Smyrna. Through his writings, Eusebius provides a crucial link between the apostles and the early church leaders, emphasizing the continuity of ecclesiastical authority and doctrinal integrity.
