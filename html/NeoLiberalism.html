Neoliberalism: Market Primacy and the Restructuring of State

Introduction

Neoliberalism is a late‑20th‑century political‑economic paradigm prioritizing free markets, minimal state intervention, and individual entrepreneurship. Emerging as a response to perceived failures of Keynesian welfare states, it reshaped global policy frameworks — influencing everything from trade to social welfare.

Historical Origins & Context

Core Principles

|Principle|Description| |---|---| |Deregulation|Remove state controls over markets| |Privatization|Transfer public assets/services to private sector| |Fiscal Austerity|Cut government spending to reduce deficits| |Free Trade|Eliminate tariffs and capital controls| |Market-Based Solutions|Social outcomes achieved through competition|

|Tradition|State Role|Economic Focus| |---|---|---| |[[Classical Liberalism]]|Limited to protection of rights|Free markets| |[[Social Liberalism]]|Active welfare provision|Regulated markets| |Neoliberalism|Enable markets to self‑regulate|Market efficiency above all|
