The Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, as designated by UNESCO, includes a wide range of cultural practices, traditions, and expressions from around the world. Below is a list of some notable examples, organized by region. Note that this is not an exhaustive list, as UNESCO continues to add new elements regularly. For the most comprehensive and up-to-date list, visit the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage website.

Africa

  1. Al-Qatt Al-Asiri (Saudi Arabia) - Traditional interior wall decoration by women.
  2. Gule Wamkulu (Malawi, Mozambique, Zambia) - A ritual dance of the Chewa people.
  3. Ifa Divination System (Nigeria) - A system of knowledge and divination.
  4. Taskiwin (Morocco) - A martial dance of the western High Atlas.
  5. Yaaral and Degal (Mali) - Festivals celebrating the crossing of cattle.

Asia and the Pacific

  1. Peking Opera (China) - A traditional form of Chinese theater.
  2. Kimjang (South Korea) - Making and sharing kimchi.
  3. Nôgaku Theatre (Japan) - Traditional Japanese musical drama.
  4. Kumbh Mela (India) - The largest religious gathering in the world.
  5. Wayang Puppet Theatre (Indonesia) - Traditional shadow puppetry.
  6. Mongolian Traditional Music: Long Song and Throat Singing (Mongolia).
  7. Batik (Indonesia) - Traditional textile dyeing technique.
  8. Taekkyeon (South Korea) - A traditional Korean martial art.
  9. Lakalaka (Tonga) - Dances and sung speeches.
  10. Tugging Rituals and Games (Multiple countries in Asia).

Europe

  1. Flamenco (Spain) - A passionate dance and music form.
  2. Mediterranean Diet (Multiple countries) - A traditional dietary practice.
  3. Carnival of Binche (Belgium) - A historic carnival celebration.
  4. Opera dei Pupi (Italy) - Sicilian puppet theatre.
  5. Fado (Portugal) - A melancholic music genre.
  6. Österreichische Volkstanzbewegung (Austria) - Austrian folk dance.
  7. Kihnu Cultural Space (Estonia) - Traditional island culture.
  8. Falconry (Multiple countries) - A living human heritage.
  9. Tango (Argentina/Uruguay) - A passionate dance and music style.
  10. [[Yoga]] ([[India]]) - A physical, mental, and spiritual practice.

Latin America and the Caribbean

  1. Carnival of Barranquilla (Colombia) - A vibrant carnival celebration.
  2. Mariachi (Mexico) - Traditional Mexican music.
  3. Candombe (Uruguay) - A drum-based musical tradition.
  4. Rumba (Cuba) - A dance and music genre.
  5. Oral and Graphic Expressions of the Wajapi (Brazil) - Indigenous art and storytelling.
  6. Day of the Dead (Mexico) - A celebration honoring the deceased.
  7. Talavera Pottery (Mexico) - Traditional ceramic craftsmanship.
  8. Reggae Music (Jamaica) - A globally influential music genre.

Middle East

  1. Arabic Calligraphy (Multiple countries) - The art of beautiful writing.
  2. Mevlevi Sema Ceremony (Turkey) - The Whirling Dervishes' ritual.
  3. Traditional Turkish Archery (Turkey) - A historic martial art.
  4. Al-Sadu (Kuwait) - Traditional weaving.
  5. Coffee Culture (Turkey) - Traditional preparation and sharing of Turkish coffee.
  6. Palestinian Hikaye (Palestine) - A traditional storytelling form.

North America

  1. Indigenous Festivity of the Dead (Mexico) - Celebrations honoring ancestors.
  2. Michoacán Cuisine (Mexico) - Traditional Mexican culinary practices.
  3. Bluegrass Music (USA) - A traditional American music genre.
  4. Tsimshian Oral Traditions (Canada) - Indigenous storytelling.

Multinational Elements

  1. Falconry (Multiple countries) - A traditional hunting practice.
  2. Mediterranean Diet (Multiple countries) - Shared dietary traditions.
  3. Tugging Rituals and Games (Multiple countries in Asia).
  4. Arirang Folk Song (North Korea/South Korea) - A shared cultural expression.
  5. [[Nowruz]] (Multiple countries) - The Persian New Year celebration.

This list highlights the diversity of intangible cultural heritage recognized by UNESCO. Each element represents a unique tradition, practice, or expression that contributes to the cultural richness of humanity. For a complete and updated list, visit the official UNESCO website.
