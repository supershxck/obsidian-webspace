James K. Polk (1795–1849): Manifest Destiny’s President

Introduction

James Knox Polk served as the 11th President of the United States (1845–1849), accomplishing all four major objectives of his single term and firmly embedding the doctrine of Manifest Destiny into American policy. He is remembered as one of the most effective—and purposeful—presidents in U.S. history.

Early Life & Political Rise

• Born: November 2, 1795, Mecklenburg County, North Carolina

• Education: University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill (BA, 1818)

• Political Career: U.S. House of Representatives (1825–1839); Speaker of the House (1835–1839); Governor of Tennessee (1839–1841)

Presidential Agenda & Achievements (1845–1849)

| Goal | Action | Outcome | | --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | | Reduce Tariffs | Walker Tariff (1846) | Lowered rates, stimulated trade | | Re-establish Independent Treasury | [[Independent Treasury Act]] (1846) | Separated federal funds from private banks | | Acquire Oregon Territory | Negotiated 49th parallel compromise (1846) | Peaceful acquisition of Pacific Northwest | | Acquire California & Southwest | Oversaw [[Mexican-American War]] (1846–1848) | Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo; U.S. gained present-day CA, NV, UT, AZ, NM, and parts of CO & WY |

Key Vocabulary

• Manifest Destiny: Belief in U.S. divine right to expand across North America

• Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo: 1848 treaty ending Mexican-American War

• Walker Tariff: Revenue tariff reducing import duties to promote commerce

• Independent Treasury: System isolating federal funds from private banks
