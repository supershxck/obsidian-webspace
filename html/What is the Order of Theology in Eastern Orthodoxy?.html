In Eastern Orthodoxy, while there isn't a rigid hierarchy in the study of theology, the following categories can be considered as an order or framework for theological exploration:

1. Dogmatic Theology (Dogmatics) This branch of theology deals with the official teachings, doctrines, and beliefs of the Christian faith as expressed in the Nicene Creed and other authoritative statements of faith. Dogmatic theology explores topics such as the Trinity, Christology, the nature of salvation, the sacraments, and eschatology (the study of the end times).

2. Patristic Theology Patristic theology refers to the study of the writings, teachings, and theological insights of the Church Fathers (the early Christian theologians and writers from the apostolic era to the early centuries of the Church). This includes works by theologians such as Athanasius of Alexandria, Gregory of Nyssa, John Chrysostom, and Cyril of Alexandria, among others.

3. Liturgical Theology Liturgical theology focuses on the study of worship practices, liturgical rites, sacraments, and the theological significance of the Church's worship life. It explores the theology behind the Divine Liturgy, the sacraments (mysteries), hymnography, and the Church's liturgical calendar.

4. Moral Theology (Ethics) Moral theology deals with ethical principles, moral teachings, and the application of Christian ethics in daily life. It addresses topics such as virtue ethics, moral decision-making, social justice, bioethics, and the ethical implications of Christian teachings.

5. Ascetical Theology Ascetical theology focuses on spiritual disciplines, practices of prayer, fasting, repentance, and the pursuit of holiness and spiritual growth. It explores the teachings of the Desert Fathers and Mothers, monastic spirituality, spiritual warfare, and the inner life of the Christian believer.

6. Canonical Theology Canonical theology examines the canons (church laws), ecclesiastical governance, the structure of the Church, and the relationship between theology and the administration of the Church. It addresses topics such as ecclesiology (the study of the Church), canon law, pastoral theology, and the role of bishops and clergy.

7. Historical Theology Historical theology studies the development of Christian doctrine, theology, and practices throughout history. It explores the historical context of theological controversies, doctrinal formulations, ecumenical councils, and the contributions of theologians and movements to the ongoing development of Orthodox theology.

This order of theology in Eastern Orthodoxy reflects a holistic approach to theological inquiry, encompassing both doctrinal teachings and practical aspects of the Christian life within the context of the Church's tradition, liturgy, and spiritual heritage. Each area of theology contributes to a comprehensive understanding of Orthodox Christian faith and practice.
