15th Century Influential Figures: A Global Outline (100 Figures)

Below is an outline featuring 100 influential people from the 15th century across various regions. Each name is enclosed in double brackets to emphasize their significance during this transformative era.

I. Western Europe (40 Figures)

  1. [[Leonardo da Vinci]]

• Renaissance genius: painter, inventor, and scientist whose innovations reshaped art and engineering.

  1. [[Michelangelo Buonarroti]]

• Master sculptor and painter whose works, including the Sistine Chapel ceiling, epitomize High Renaissance art.

  1. [[Raphael]]

• Celebrated painter and architect known for his harmonious compositions and influential frescoes.

  1. [[Sandro Botticelli]]

• Italian painter whose masterpieces, such as The Birth of Venus, capture the spirit of the Renaissance.

  1. [[Albrecht Dürer]]

• German artist and printmaker noted for his detailed engravings and influential treatises on art.

  1. [[Christopher Columbus]]

• Explorer whose 1492 voyage opened the way for European exploration and colonization of the Americas.

  1. [[Ferdinand II of Aragon]]

• Co-ruler of Spain whose marriage to Isabella I paved the way for unified Spanish power.

  1. [[Isabella I of Castile]]

• Queen whose patronage of exploration and support for Columbus transformed global history.

  1. [[King Henry V of England]]

• Warrior king whose victories in the Hundred Years’ War boosted English prestige.

  1. [[King Henry VI of England]]

• Monarch whose long and troubled reign saw both triumphs and internal strife.

  1. [[Richard III of England]]

• Controversial king whose defeat at Bosworth marked the end of the Plantagenet dynasty.

  1. [[Charles VII of France]]

• King whose leadership helped expel English forces during the latter phase of the Hundred Years’ War.

  1. [[Louis XI of France]]

• Shrewd monarch who strengthened royal power and laid foundations for a modern French state.

  1. [[Philip the Good]]

• Duke of Burgundy known for his patronage of the arts and influential court culture.

  1. [[Charles the Bold]]

• Ambitious Duke of Burgundy whose military campaigns aimed to expand his territory.

  1. [[John II of Portugal]]

• Monarch who initiated the Portuguese Age of Discoveries.

  1. [[Afonso V of Portugal]]

• Ruler whose naval exploits and conquests expanded Portuguese influence.

  1. [[Infante Dom Henrique (Henry the Navigator)]]

• Pioneer of maritime exploration who spurred European discovery along Africa’s coast.

  1. [[Pope Nicholas V]]

• Pope whose patronage and reform initiatives influenced art, learning, and exploration.

  1. [[Pope Callixtus III]]

• Pontiff active during the Crusades, shaping church policy in turbulent times.

  1. [[Giovanni Boccaccio]]

• Writer and humanist whose works helped revive classical literature.

  1. [[Francesco Petrarch]]

• Poet and scholar celebrated as the “Father of Humanism.”

  1. [[Jan Hus]]

• Bohemian reformer whose challenges to church authority foreshadowed later Protestant movements.

  1. [[Thomas Malory]]

• Author of Le Morte d’Arthur, which shaped the legend of King Arthur.

  1. [[William Caxton]]

• Printer who introduced the printing press to England, revolutionizing the spread of knowledge.

  1. [[Martin Behaim]]

• Cartographer renowned for creating one of the earliest globes of the Earth.

  1. [[Giovanni Pico della Mirandola]]

• Philosopher whose work celebrated human potential and classical wisdom.

  1. [[Marsilio Ficino]]

• Renaissance humanist who reintroduced Platonic philosophy to Europe.

  1. [[Leon Battista Alberti]]

• Architect and theorist whose treatises laid the groundwork for Renaissance art and architecture.

  1. [[Piero della Francesca]]

• Painter and mathematician whose balanced compositions exemplify Renaissance ideals.

  1. [[Donatello]]

• Sculptor celebrated for his realistic and innovative bronze and marble works.

  1. [[Filippo Brunelleschi]]

• Architect and engineer credited with pioneering linear perspective in art.

  1. [[Giovanni di Bicci de’ Medici]]

• Founder of the Medici bank, whose patronage spurred the Florentine Renaissance.

  1. [[Cosimo de’ Medici]]

• Powerful banker and statesman who became the de facto ruler of Florence.

  1. [[Piero di Lorenzo de’ Medici]]

• Influential Florentine ruler whose leadership navigated turbulent political waters.

  1. [[Niccolò Machiavelli]]

• Political philosopher whose writings on power and statecraft influenced modern political thought.

  1. [[Margaret of Anjou]]

• Queen consort of England whose political and military actions impacted the Wars of the Roses.

  1. [[Edward IV of England]]

• Monarch whose reign consolidated Lancastrian and Yorkist claims during the Wars of the Roses.

  1. [[Henry VII of England]]

• Founder of the Tudor dynasty, whose accession marked a new era in English history.

  1. [[John of Lancaster, Duke of Bedford]]

• Key English nobleman whose leadership influenced the politics of late medieval England.

  1. [[Sultan Ahmad Shah I]]

• Founder of the Bahmani Sultanate, whose rule set the stage for Deccan cultural flowering.

  1. [[Sultan Mahmood Shah]]

• Ruler of the Bahmani state noted for his administrative reforms and patronage.

  1. [[Deva Raya II]]

• Vijayanagara emperor celebrated for consolidating power and fostering cultural achievements.

  1. [[Rana Kumbha]]

• Rajput ruler of Mewar renowned for his military successes and architectural patronage.

  1. [[Rana Sanga]]

  1. [[Bahlul Lodi]]

• Founder of the [[Lodi dynasty]], whose reign initiated a new era in North Indian politics.

  1. [[Guru Nanak]]

VI. Africa (5 Figures; 88–92)

  1. [[Sunni Ali]]

• Ruler of the [[Songhai Empire]], whose conquests laid the foundation for West African dominance.

  1. [[Askia Mohammad I]]

• Sultan who reformed and expanded the [[Songhai Empire]], promoting trade and learning.

  1. [[Muhammad Rumfa]]

• Kano ruler famed for his administrative innovations and cultural contributions.

  1. [[Sultan Suleiman of the Mali Empire]]

  1. [[Mansa Mahmud]]

  1. [[Axayacatl]]

Concluding Reflections

The 15th century was a pivotal era of cultural rebirth, exploration, and political transformation. From the luminaries of Western Europe who defined the Renaissance to the dynamic rulers and scholars of the Islamic world, from the resilient last days of Byzantium to the flourishing courts of East Asia, and from the transformative states of South Asia and Africa to the emerging civilizations in the Americas and Central Asia—the 100 figures outlined above, each encapsulated in double brackets, played vital roles in shaping our world. Their enduring legacies continue to illuminate the pathways of art, science, governance, and spiritual thought, offering rich insights into a century of profound change and interconnected global evolution.
