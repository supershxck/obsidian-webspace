Ancient History

  • Dacian Kingdom: The area now known as Romania was originally inhabited by the Dacians, an ancient Indo-European people. The Dacian Kingdom reached its peak under King [[Burebista]] (82-44 BCE) and later under King [[Decebalus]] (87-106 CE).
  • Roman Conquest: In 106 CE, the Roman Empire, under Emperor Trajan, conquered Dacia. The area became a Roman province, leading to significant Roman cultural and architectural influence.

Middle Ages

  • Early Middle Ages: After the Roman withdrawal in the 3rd century, the region experienced invasions and settlements by various migratory peoples, including Goths, Huns, Avars, Slavs, and Bulgars.
  • Formation of Principalities: By the late Middle Ages, the regions of Wallachia and Moldavia emerged as distinct principalities. Wallachia was founded by [[Basarab I]] around 1330, and Moldavia by [[Bogdan I]] in the mid-14th century.
  • Hungarian Influence: The region of Transylvania became part of the Kingdom of Hungary in the 11th century, leading to a mix of cultures and influences.

Ottoman Era

  • Ottoman Influence: From the 15th century onwards, Wallachia and Moldavia came under the suzerainty of the Ottoman Empire, though they retained a degree of autonomy.
  • Resistance Leaders: Figures like [[Vlad the Impaler]] (Vlad III) in Wallachia and [[Stephen the Great]] in Moldavia became known for their resistance against Ottoman control.

Habsburg and Modernization

  • Habsburg Monarchy: In the late 17th century, the Habsburg Monarchy took control of Transylvania, further complicating the region’s political landscape.
  • Unification Efforts: The 19th century saw growing nationalist movements in the principalities, leading to efforts towards unification and independence from Ottoman and Habsburg control.

Kingdom of Romania

  • Unification: In 1859, Wallachia and Moldavia united under Prince [[Alexandru Ioan Cuza]], forming the basis of modern Romania. Full independence from the Ottoman Empire was achieved in 1877-1878.
  • Kingdom: In 1881, Romania became a kingdom under King [[Carol I]]. The country expanded its territory following the Balkan Wars and World War I, gaining Transylvania, Bukovina, and Bessarabia.

World War II

  • Axis Alliance: During World War II, Romania initially aligned with the Axis powers under the dictatorship of [[Ion Antonescu]], participating in the invasion of the Soviet Union.
  • Switch to Allies: In 1944, King [[Michael I]] led a coup that overthrew Antonescu, and Romania switched sides to join the Allies.

Communist Era

  • Soviet Influence: After World War II, Romania fell under Soviet influence and became a socialist republic in 1947. The communist regime, led by figures such as [[Gheorghe Gheorghiu-Dej]] and later [[Nicolae Ceaușescu]], implemented strict authoritarian policies.
  • Ceaușescu's Rule: Ceaușescu's rule from 1965 to 1989 was marked by severe repression, economic mismanagement, and widespread human rights abuses.

Revolution of 1989

  • Fall of Communism: In December 1989, a popular uprising led to the overthrow and execution of Ceaușescu. Romania transitioned to democracy and a market economy, joining NATO in 2004 and the European Union in 2007.

Modern Romania

  • EU Membership: Since joining the EU, Romania has experienced significant political and economic changes, striving to integrate with Western Europe while addressing internal challenges such as corruption and economic disparities.
