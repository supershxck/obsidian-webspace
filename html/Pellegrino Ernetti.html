Pellegrino Ernetti (1925–2016) was an Italian Benedictine monk best known for his involvement in various esoteric and pseudoscientific theories, particularly those surrounding alleged time manipulation devices and ancient extraterrestrial artifacts. While his claims have captivated the imaginations of many, they remain unverified and are generally regarded with skepticism by mainstream scholars and scientists.

1. Early Life and Monastic Career

1.1 Birth and Background

• Born: July 22, 1925, in Italy.

• Religious Life: Ernetti became a Benedictine monk, dedicating his life to religious pursuits and scholarly activities within the monastic community.

1.2 Education and Interests

• Scholarship: Ernetti was educated in areas typical for monastic scholars, including theology, philosophy, and possibly the sciences.

• Esoteric Interests: Beyond his formal education, Ernetti developed interests in esoteric subjects, such as time manipulation, ancient civilizations, and extraterrestrial life.

2. Notable Claims and Theories

2.1 The Rome’s Time Sphere

2.1.1 Description

• Concept: Ernetti claimed to have been involved in the creation of a device known as the “Rome’s Time Sphere” during the mid-20th century.

• Functionality: According to Ernetti, the Time Sphere was capable of manipulating time, allowing for the transportation of objects from the past to the present.

• Collaborators: He asserted that the project was a collaboration between scientists and the Vatican, aimed at preserving valuable artifacts.

2.1.2 Alleged Uses

• Rescue Missions: Ernetti suggested that the Time Sphere was used to rescue artifacts from catastrophic events, such as the sinking of the RMS Titanic or the destruction of the Arch of Constantine in Rome.

• Secret Operations: He maintained that these operations were classified, with details hidden from the public and mainstream historical records.

2.2 Dropa Stones

2.2.1 Association with Dropa Stones

• Connection: Ernetti has been linked to the Dropa Stones, another controversial topic involving alleged ancient extraterrestrial artifacts found in China.

• Claims: He claimed that the Dropa Stones contained hieroglyphic-like symbols and were evidence of extraterrestrial contact with early human civilizations.

2.2.2 Criticism and Skepticism

• Lack of Evidence: There is no credible archaeological evidence supporting the existence of the Dropa Stones as described in Ernetti’s accounts.

• Academic Rejection: Mainstream scholars and archaeologists dismiss these claims as fabrications or modern myths without basis in factual discovery.
