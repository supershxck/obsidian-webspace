Overview

The hexagram Return (Fu) is a deeply symbolic representation of cycles, beginnings, and the natural flow of return after a period of decline. It symbolizes the turning point where darkness (Yin) gives way to light (Yang). This hexagram often speaks of renewal, rebirth, and the promise of growth after a period of difficulty.

  • Name: Return (Fu)
  • Trigrams:
  • Bottom Trigram (Inner): Thunder (☳) – Action, movement.
  • Top Trigram (Outer): Earth (☷) – Receptivity, stability.

Judgment (The Advice)

The judgment text says:

"Return. Success. Going out and coming in without error. Friends arrive. No blame. The way is clear for return. After seven days comes return. It is favorable to have somewhere to go."

Key Themes:

  1. Turning Point: This hexagram represents the turning point when the light begins to return. In the natural world, it corresponds to the winter solstice, when the longest night has passed, and days start growing longer.

  2. Renewal: A time to embrace the cycles of life. If you’ve been through a challenging time, this hexagram suggests that things are beginning to shift in your favor.

  3. Patience and Timing: The idea of "seven days" emphasizes that change is gradual and cyclical. Be patient, as the return will happen naturally if you align with the flow.

  4. Harmony and Companionship: Friends and allies will appear, offering support. The judgment encourages reuniting with people or values that resonate with your true self.

Image (The Metaphor)

The accompanying image text states:

"Thunder within the Earth: The image of Return. Thus the superior person closes the door and rests. Closing the door does not mean stagnation but preparation for what is to come."

Key Symbolism:
  • Thunder within the Earth: This suggests latent energy ready to emerge. It’s like a seed underground in winter, gathering strength to sprout in spring.
  • Rest and Reflection: The image advises a period of retreat and preparation. This is a time to reflect on past actions, consolidate your energy, and prepare for new opportunities.

Analogy: Imagine pulling back a bowstring before firing an arrow. This phase of return is the "drawing back" that allows for future momentum.
