Accomplishments of the [[Sixth Ecumenical Council]] (Third Council of Constantinople, 680-681 AD)

1. Condemnation of Monothelitism

  • Key Issue: The council condemned [[Monothelitism]], a doctrine that claimed Christ had only one will (the divine will) rather than two wills (divine and human).
  • Affirmation of Dyothelitism: The council affirmed that Christ possesses two wills and two energies, corresponding to his two natures, divine and human. This teaching is known as [[Dyothelitism]].

2. Restoration of Orthodoxy

  • Reinforcement of Chalcedonian Doctrine: By affirming the existence of two wills in Christ, the council reinforced the [[Chalcedonian Definition]] and maintained the distinction yet unity of Christ’s two natures.
  • Clarification of Christology: The decision clarified the orthodox understanding of Christ's incarnation, emphasizing that both his divine and human wills are in perfect harmony, without conflict or separation.

3. Condemnation of Heretical Leaders

  • Notable Condemnations: The council condemned several prominent figures who had supported Monothelitism, including Patriarch Sergius I of Constantinople, Pope Honorius I of Rome, and Patriarch Cyrus of Alexandria.

4. The Role of the Papacy

  • Papal Influence: The council recognized the significant role of the papacy in upholding orthodoxy. Pope Agatho and his successors, particularly Pope Leo II, played crucial roles in supporting the council’s decisions and ensuring their implementation.

5. Issuance of Canons and Decrees

  • Administrative and Disciplinary Canons: The council issued several canons to address issues of church administration, discipline, and governance, further strengthening the ecclesiastical structure of the Church.

6. Reconciliation Efforts

  • Healing Divisions: The council aimed to heal the divisions within the Church caused by the Monothelite controversy and restore unity among Christians by affirming the orthodox doctrine.

Key Figures

  • Emperor Constantine IV: Convened the council and played a significant role in its proceedings, supporting the orthodox position.
  • Pope Agatho: Provided a detailed doctrinal letter to the council, which was instrumental in its deliberations and decisions.
  • Patriarch George of Constantinople: Presided over the council sessions and supported the condemnation of Monothelitism.

Conclusion

The Sixth Ecumenical Council, or the Third Council of Constantinople, was pivotal in addressing the Monothelite controversy and clarifying the orthodox doctrine of Christ’s two wills. By condemning Monothelitism and affirming Dyothelitism, the council reinforced the Chalcedonian understanding of Christ’s two natures and maintained the unity and harmony of his divine and human wills. The decisions made at this council were crucial in preserving orthodox Christology and healing the divisions within the Church. The council also underscored the important role of the papacy in upholding and promoting orthodox doctrine.
