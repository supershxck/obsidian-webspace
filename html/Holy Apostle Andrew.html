The Holy Apostle Andrew, also known as "Andrew the First-Called," is traditionally credited with establishing several Christian communities in various regions during his missionary journeys. Here are the main places where he is believed to have established churches:

1. [[Byzantium]] (Constantinople)

  • Founding of the Church in Byzantium: According to tradition, Andrew was the first to preach the Gospel in Byzantium (modern-day Istanbul, Turkey). He is said to have established a Christian community there and appointed Stachys, one of the Seventy Apostles, as the first [[Bishop of Byzantium]]. This church would later become the center of the [[Eastern Orthodox Church]].

2. [[Scythia]] (Southern [[Russia]] and [[Ukraine]])

  • Missionary Work in Scythia: The Apostle Andrew is believed to have traveled through the region of Scythia, which encompasses parts of modern-day southern Russia, Ukraine, and Romania. According to some traditions, he preached the Gospel along the [[Black Sea]] coast and in the area around the Dnieper River, where the city of Kiev now stands. This region would later become a significant center for the spread of Christianity in the Slavic world.

3. [[Greece]]

  • Preaching in Greece: Andrew's missionary work also took him to Greece, where he is said to have preached in several cities, including Patras. In Patras, he was martyred by crucifixion on an X-shaped cross, which is now known as St. Andrew's Cross. The church he founded there became an important early Christian community.

4. [[Thrace]]

  • Establishing Churches in Thrace: Thrace, a region that includes parts of modern-day Greece, [[Turkey]], and [[Bulgaria]], is another area where Andrew is believed to have preached and established Christian communities.

5. [[Epirus]] and [[Achaia]]

  • Spreading the Gospel in Epirus and Achaia: Andrew is also credited with establishing Christian communities in the regions of Epirus (northwestern Greece) and Achaia (in the northern part of the Peloponnese). His work in these regions contributed to the spread of Christianity throughout Greece.

6. Asia Minor

  • Missionary Activity in Asia Minor: Some traditions hold that Andrew traveled through parts of Asia Minor (modern-day Turkey), preaching the Gospel and establishing churches, though specific cities are less well-documented compared to other regions.

Conclusion

The Apostle Andrew's missionary journeys were extensive, and he is revered in many Christian traditions as a key figure in the establishment of early Christian communities, especially in the regions of Byzantium, Scythia, and Greece. His legacy is particularly strong in the Eastern Orthodox Church, where he is considered the founder of the Patriarchate of Constantinople.
