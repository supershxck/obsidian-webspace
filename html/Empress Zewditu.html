Empress Zewditu: A Structured Chapter

I. Introduction

Empress Zewditu (1876–1930) was Ethiopia’s first—and only—female monarch in modern history, reigning from 1916 until her death. Her tenure bridged traditional imperial authority and the early stirrings of modernization under Regent Ras Tafari Makonnen (later Haile Selassie).

II. Early Life & Ascension ([[history]])

• Born: January 29, 1876, daughter of Emperor Menelik II and Empress Taytu Betul.

• Elevation: Proclaimed Empress (Negiste Negest) after the 1916 coup deposing Emperor Lij Iyasu, chosen for her lineage and Orthodox piety.

VIII. Conclusion

Empress Zewditu’s reign represents a pivotal transitional era in Ethiopian history—upholding imperial tradition while setting the stage for modernization. Through [[history]], [[Political Philosophy]], and [[psychology]], her legacy offers lessons on balancing continuity and change, and the nuanced dynamics of female authority in patriarchal societies.
