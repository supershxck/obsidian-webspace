The Seven Sages of Greece were a group of ancient Greek philosophers, statesmen, and lawgivers who were admired for their wisdom, practical knowledge, and contributions to Greek society in the 6th century BCE. They are often remembered for their proverbs and aphorisms that reflected moral and ethical insights. While the exact list varies slightly, the most common Seven Sages are:

  1. [[Thales]] of Miletus – Known for his work in philosophy, mathematics, and astronomy; believed that water was the fundamental substance of the universe.

  2. [[Solon]] – A statesman and lawgiver, famous for his reforms in Athens and for promoting justice and fairness.

  3. [[Chilon of Sparta]] – A Spartan lawgiver known for his sayings on ethics, modesty, and temperance.

  4. [[Pittacus of Mytilene]] – A ruler and general, he was known for his wisdom in leadership and his advice on moderation and self-control.

  5. [[Bias of Priene]] – A philosopher known for his ethical and moral teachings, often emphasizing the importance of justice and friendship.

  6. [[Cleobulus of Lindos]] – A statesman and philosopher who focused on the importance of self-discipline, moderation, and the pursuit of virtue.

  7. [[Periander of Corinth]] – A ruler known for his practical wisdom in governance, often emphasizing the importance of good leadership and control.

The Seven Sages were revered as embodying the ideal of practical wisdom, and their aphorisms have influenced Western thought and philosophy throughout history.
