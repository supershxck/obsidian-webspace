It's important to note that these interpretations are based on the portrayal of Jesus in religious texts, which are matters of faith and belief. What is a persona?
The persona is a concept introduced by Jung to describe the social mask or facade that an individual presents to the outside world

What is a personality? Personality is a broader and more comprehensive term that encompasses the entire psychological makeup of an individual. Including not only the persona but also the individual's unique characteristics, traits, preferences, and the deeper aspects of their psyche. Personality is a more holistic and complex concept that goes beyond the social roles and masks we adopt in different situations. It encompasses the individual's psychological type, archetypal influences, unconscious elements, and the ongoing process of individuation.

the persona is a specific component of an individual's personality that represents the outward-facing, socially constructed aspect, while the personality encompasses the entirety of an individual's psychological makeup, including both conscious and unconscious elements.

What does it mean to be extraverted or introverted? Typically intro- or extra- version is defined as energy brought to a social scene. 

In simple terms, Carl Jung describes extraversion as a mindset where decisions and actions are driven by external realities rather than personal perspectives. Extraverts are those whose lives show a clear alignment with objective conditions, emphasizing the importance of the external world over subjective views. This means they focus more on external factors and are less likely to see absolute truths within their own inner experiences.

In simpler terms, Carl Jung distinguishes introversion by the fact that, unlike extraverts who focus on external realities, introverts prioritize subjective factors. This means introverts insert their personal views between the perception of an object and their actions, giving precedence to inner determinants over external situations. While introverts are aware of external conditions, they rely more on the subjective aspects, making their actions sometimes appear out of sync with the immediate external world. Unlike extraverts who respond directly to external stimuli, introverts filter these stimuli through their dominant mental function.

What is a cognitive function?  First there are the two cognitive processes: percieving and judging  Perceiving is further broken down into sensing or intuition  Ex:perception in daily life is either based on your intuition or sensory data (or a combination of both Judging is further broken down into feeling or thinking  Ex: people typically make judgement calls or decisions based on logic (thinking) or feelings (head over heart, or heart over head) You can further break these four these processes down into extraverted or introverted variants

the top 4 functions in your cognitive function stack map to your 4 letter type. 

Among those 4 functions will be an  extraverted perceiving function,  introverted perceiving function,  extraverted judging function,  and introverted judging function.  One will be sensing;  one will be intuition;  one will be thinking;  and one will be feeling.

Your top 2 functions will come most naturally to you, and will be comprised of an introverted and an extraverted function; one will be perceiving and one will be judging.
