Grover Cleveland (1837–1908): The Veto President and Champion of Integrity

Introduction

Stephen Grover Cleveland served as the 22nd and 24th President of the United States (1885–1889; 1893–1897), uniquely elected to two non‑consecutive terms. Renowned for his steadfast honesty, fiscal conservatism, and commitment to principled governance, Cleveland earned the moniker “Veto President.”

Key Vocabulary

• Veto President: Cleveland’s frequent use of veto power to check Congress

• Gold Standard: Monetary system pegging currency to gold, championed by Cleveland

• Interstate Commerce Commission: First federal agency regulating railroads

• Sherman Silver Purchase Act: Legislation requiring government purchase of silver, repealed to protect gold reserves
