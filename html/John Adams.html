John Adams (1735–1826): Founding Father and Champion of Republican Principles

Introduction

John Adams was a leading American statesman, lawyer, diplomat, and political theorist who served as the second President of the United States (1797–1801). A principal architect of American independence and constitutional government, Adams’s commitment to rule of law and balanced governance shaped the early Republic.

Early Life & Revolutionary Leadership

• Born: October 30, 1735 in Braintree (now Quincy), Massachusetts

• Education: Harvard College (BA, 1755); trained in law

• Key Roles: Delegate to Continental Congress; principal author of the Massachusetts Constitution; advocate for independence (Declaration of Independence signer)
