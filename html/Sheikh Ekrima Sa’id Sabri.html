Before Sheikh Muhammad Ahmad Hussein’s appointment as [[Grand Mufti of Jerusalem]] in 2006, the position was held by Sheikh Ekrima Sa’id Sabri from 1994 to 2006.

Sheikh Ekrima Sa’id Sabri:

• Tenure: 1994–2006

• Background: Appointed by Palestinian Authority President Yasser Arafat in 1994, Sabri was known for his outspoken sermons and political activism.

• Notable Aspects:

• His tenure was marked by sermons that often addressed political issues, reflecting the Palestinian struggle and resistance.

• Sabri’s statements sometimes led to tensions with Israeli authorities and concerns among Palestinian leadership about the politicization of the religious office.

• Post-Tenure: After his removal in 2006 by President Mahmoud Abbas, Sabri continued to be an influential religious figure, often delivering sermons at Al-Aqsa Mosque and remaining active in Palestinian religious and political matters.

Transition of Office:

In 2006, President Mahmoud Abbas appointed Sheikh Muhammad Ahmad Hussein as the new Grand Mufti, aiming to have a religious leader perceived as a political moderate. This transition reflected the Palestinian Authority’s intent to balance religious authority with political considerations.

Note: The role of the Grand Mufti of Jerusalem has historically been both religious and political, with each mufti’s tenure reflecting the complexities of their time.
