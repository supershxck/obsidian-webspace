Sola Scriptura might be seen as limited or incomplete from an EO perspective. Understanding why they think like this involves reaching mutual understanding and respecting both traditions' views. The Eastern Orthodox Church places great emphasis on Holy Tradition, which encompasses not just the Bible but also the writings of the Church Fathers, liturgical texts, ecumenical councils' decisions, and the lived experience of the Church through the centuries.

Unity of Scripture and Tradition For Eastern Orthodoxy, Holy Tradition and Scripture are not in competition or contradiction. Instead, Scripture is part of Holy Tradition, which is the life of the Holy Spirit in the Church. Tradition is seen as the context within which the Scriptures were written, canonized, and have always been interpreted. This perspective holds that removing the Bible from this context can lead to misinterpretation or a diminished understanding of the faith.

the Role of the Church in Canon Formation The canon of Scripture (the list of books recognized as genuine and inspired) was itself determined by Tradition through the consensus of the early Church councils and leaders. This process was guided by the Holy Spirit but relied on the lived and communal faith experience of the early Church(John 14:26). Thus, the very Bible that Sola Scriptura upholds comes to us through the authority of the Church's Tradition.

the Continuity of Faith Eastern Orthodoxy sees Holy Tradition as ensuring continuity in the faith, connecting believers today directly with the Apostles and Jesus Christ himself. This continuity is crucial for maintaining the integrity of the faith, safeguarding against individualistic or novel interpretations of Scripture that stray from the apostolic faith.

the Limitations of Text Alone A text, especially one as rich and complex as the Bible, requires a living, breathing community to interpret it. The Eastern Orthodox Church views Holy Tradition as the life of the Holy Spirit guiding the Church in interpreting Scripture correctly. Without this living tradition, interpretations can become disconnected from the historical and spiritual roots of the faith.

the Value of Sola Scriptura Sola Scriptura has value, namely, because of its emphasis on the authority of Scripture and the accessibility of God’s Word to all believers.  Holy Tradition provides a nuanced framework that has been tested and refined through centuries of prayer, reflection, and communal life in the Spirit.

Experience Liturgy and Church Life Sometimes, understanding comes not just from theological argument but from experience. Go participate in the liturgical life of an Eastern Orthodox Church to receive a tangible sense of how Scripture and Tradition are lived out in worship, prayer, and community life.

Remember patience, humility, and a genuine love for the unity of all Christians.  May the depth and beauty of the faith bear fruit in your heart.  May Jesus Christ, the Son of God, have mercy on you.
